Jonathan Coulton (born December 1, 1970) is an American singer-songwriter, whose claim to fame was his use of the Internet to distribute his music.

Biography

In 2005, he quit his job as a software writer to embark on a project he named "Thing a Week", for which he released a song every Friday for an entire year. This project was a massive success, and Coulton is now regarded an "Internet super-star". He now makes his living as a musician.

Famous for his music containing themes of geek culture, he has been featured in two video games by Valve , the first being Portal , for which he wrote the credits song, " Still Alive ", sung by Ellen McLain . On The Orange Box Original Soundtrack , a version of the song sung by him is featured. The other is one of his earlier songs, "Re: Your Brains", which was included, along with "Still Alive", in a jukebox in Left 4 Dead 2 . This song is framed as a memo sent by an infected zombie horde representative to his old co-worker trapped inside a mall indefinitely.

Coulton returned for the sequel to Portal , Portal 2 , and again provided the credits song, this time titled " Want You Gone ". . In 2015, he returned once more to provide the credits song for LEGO Dimensions, "You Wouldn't Know" [1] , featuring GLaDOS of course.
