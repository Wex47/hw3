Lamarr , sometimes referred to as Hedy , is a pet Headcrab belonging to Isaac Kleiner .

Biography

Background

Lamarr is a Headcrab who has been debeaked, preventing her from latching onto ('coupling with') and controlling a host. It is unknown when or why Kleiner originally captured Lamarr, though he has tamed her (to some extent) and keeps her as a pet.

Appearances

Half-Life 2

Lamarr's first appearance is in the Half-Life 2 chapter A Red Letter Day , when Kleiner gives Barney the honor of presenting the famed HEV Suit to Gordon. As Barney opens the door to the suit, Lamarr attempts to latch onto Barney's head. After being removed Lamarr crawls around, observing the area. She then jumps up on a locker and into a vent. Kleiner remarks that "It'll be another week before I can coax her out of there". Later, while Gordon is about to teleport to Eli's lab , Lamarr jumps out of a vent and into the teleporter, damaging wiring along the way, and causing Gordon to teleport randomly to various areas around City 17 and eventually directly outside Kleiner's lab. This forces Gordon to travel to Black Mesa East on foot. Lamarr can be seen briefly in one of the teleported locations, in what looks like a sandy wasteland with a destroyed car. Lamarr jumps away from Gordon (apparently escaping the reflux) to chase after some crows. Gordon then teleports away, Lamarr seemingly being too far away to teleport with him. It's possible she transitioned to several other areas as Gordon did following a separate path.

Through whatever means, Lamarr eventually found her way back to Kleiner. She is seen peeking out of the door with Kleiner when Gordon and Alyx appear in the teleportation chamber before scurrying off to hide from the group, perhaps frightened by Gordon's crowbar , as Kleiner suggests.

In her last appearance, she drops from the top of the screen after the ending credits and jumps toward the player.

Half-Life 2: Episode One

Lamarr is safe and sound in Half-Life 2: Episode One , where she makes a brief cameo appearance, causing further mischief to Dr. Kleiner during his live and unedited evacuation broadcast in City 17. In this cameo, she is wandering aimlessly around Kleiner's lab, while he warns her to stay away from hot lamps. It is indicated that some Resistance troops take a dim view on Kleiner (and Lamarr), as the man in the Consoling Couple states: "We're all starving, and Kleiner's Headcrab is probably eating grade-A head!".

Half-Life 2: Episode Two

In Half-Life 2: Episode Two Lamarr goes to sleep inside Arne Magnusson 's rocket while the door to it is still open. If the player chooses to close the rocket's hatch, Kleiner notices a "payload anomaly of an extra eight and a half pounds" directly before the launch. Not realizing the significance, he and Magnusson dismiss this and decide to launch anyway, sending Lamarr into space. What happened to Lamarr is unknown but it is presumed that she did not survive due to the lack of survival supplies.

To accomplish the Little Rocket Man achievement, the gnome found in the first chapter of Episode Two must be placed in the rocket next to Lamarr, so that (according to the Prima guide) "she can have a companion on her voyage."

Appearance

Lamarr differs from the Standard Headcrab in that the typical black "teeth" are missing. Otherwise, she is virtually indistinguishable from a normal Headcrab. Although Kleiner refers to Lamarr as a female, Headcrabs have never been explicitly established to have multiple genders or clear gendered organs. The Gonarch is suspected to be a female headcrab due to its ability to produce small headcrab-like creatures, and Lamarr lacks its rather distinctive reproductive housing sack. Perhaps referring to her as female is merely an affectation on the part of Kleiner, or perhaps she is merely still a juvenile on her way to greater things, it is not known. Due to being debeaked, Lamarr appears to be unable to draw nutrients as a normal Headcrab does, instead subsisting on a diet of watermelons—this is indicated by a post-it note posted on a board in Kleiner's laboratory, serving as a reminder to find more watermelons (presumably suitable due to its general resemblance to a human head).

Personality

Lamarr's behavior seems to prove it is possible to "tame" a Headcrab. Simply being debeaked would presumably fail to suppress the aggressive, parasitic nature of the species. Kleiner states that she may attempt to "couple with your head... fruitlessly, of course." Though Lamarr would seem to not be entirely docile, as shown when she is first met — lunging at Barney, attacking a bird on the rafters, and then escaping into a ventilation shaft — she can also be seen walking beside Dr. Kleiner and peeking curiously around a corner.

Behind the scenes

  • Lamarr was named after the late 1930s actress and scientist Hedy Lamarr , who co-invented an early form of spread spectrum communications technology.
  • Alyx originally was the character to own a female pet alien named " Skitch ".
  • In the beta files, Barney asks Kleiner how he can keep "that pest", after "what it did to your buddies". It is unknown if he is referring to Lamarr directly as harming people (which obviously does not happen in HL2 nor its expansions, and if Lamarr has tried to acquire a host she would be impossible to remove anyway), or Headcrabs in general. He may be referring to the fact that the majority of Headcrab victims were scientists during the Black Mesa Incident .

Trivia

  • In Kleiner's Lab, one of the notes on the wall reads, "Find more watermelons for Lamarr", suggesting that Headcrabs are omnivores (most likely not pure herbivores, during the Black Mesa Incident several Headcrab Zombies can be seen shoveling meat from a dead scientist into their "mouths").
  • In the Episode Two menu screen for White Forest , Lamarr can occasionally be seen.
  • In the Half-Life 2 chapter A Red Letter Day , Lamarr can be killed by using the "Impulse 101" cheat. It will reveal red blood instead of yellow. Kleiner will still follow his AI scripting and talk to her as if she was still alive, and the following "teleporter incident" will still occur. It is not possible to harm Lamarr after the player has come back from Nova Prospekt.
  • In the Episode Two chapter Our Mutual Fiend , Lamarr can be seen lunging into the white forest rocket. The hatch can be closed to trap Lamarr inside, and Kleiner will notice a "payload anomaly of eight and a half pounds" but the rocket launch will go ahead anyway.
  • When revisiting Kleiner's Lab after leaving Nova Prospekt, if the player waits long enough after being told to go with Dog , Kleiner will say to Gordon "Lamarr is extremely wary of your crowbar", presumably a reference to the crowbar as a commonly used weapon to kill Headcrabs with.
  • At the end of the Half-Life 2 credits, Lamarr enters the screen through a hidden entryway while Kleiner searches for her and jumps at the camera.
  • During Episode One , Lamarr's model has teeth.
