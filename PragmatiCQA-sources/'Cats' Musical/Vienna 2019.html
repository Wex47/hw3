Vereinigte Bühnen Wien
The first Vienna revival opened at the Ronacher Theatre on 20 September 2019. It is produced by Vereinigte Bühnen Wien (VBW) and performed in German. Following the tradition in Vienna, the show runs in seasons September - July, taking a summer break.

Cats previously played in Vienna from 1983 to 1990, initially at the Theater an der Wien before moving to the Ronacher in 1988.

Production Specifics

The production follows the Paris revival , using the same set and with the revised Growltiger sequence . Unlike Paris though, this production uses the rock star version of Rum Tum Tugger instead of the Street Cat . It also uses the name Carbucketty instead of Pouncival for the tom kitten.

The production generally plays 7 performances a week, with two shows on Saturday and Mondays off. There are occasional weeks when they play 6 or 8 performances instead. Four preview performances were held on 12, 13, 14 and 19 September before the official opening in 2019. Similar to the original Vienna production , the show runs in seasons beginning in September, with a break during the summer months (August/September) before the next season.

Due to the coronavirus pandemic, the 2019–2020 season was suspended from 10 March 2020 onwards. The production held test performances in front of a small (250) audience on the 3rd and 4th of July, and resumed full performances on 24 September 2020. However, due to a second wave of the pandemic, performances were again suspended from 31st October. Among the new precautions introduced, cast members will no longer interact with the audience during the show.

On 8 May 2021, it was announced that the production was to reopen on 26 May. This second half of season 2 closed on 27 June, and season 3 opened on 11 September. The third season was suspended from 22 November 2021 to 14 December 2021 due to another nationwide lockdown.

A live recorded cast album containing the entire score was released on 12 November 2021, which was recorded live between 23 to 25 June 2021.

Cast

This production shows a blend of the London style production, including character names "Admetus", "Bill Bailey", "Carbucketty" and "Jemima", while also including the German translations for "Alt Deuteronimus" and "Jenny Fleckenreich (Gumbie Katze)". The cast list also includes the UK-specific use of the name "Quaxo" as an alternate identity for "Mistoffelees".

This production reverted the changes made to the Broadway revival, where Gus performs as Rumpus Cat and the scene "Growltiger's Last Stand" is cut. Instead, "Pekes and Pollicles" has returned to its usual place in Act I, with Alonzo playing the Rumpus Cat. "Growltiger's Last Stand" is restored in its London revival version, but .

Auditions

The production will be performed in German, and as such all information is in German. However, from this audition notice we can speculate on certain elements: Rum Tum Tugger will be the Rock-star, not the Street cat; the production may well use the English production names of Jemima and Bill Bailey rather than Sillabub and Tumblebrutus (however role names can change between audition and production). One could also infer by the absence of characters like Old Deuteronomy, Gus or Grizabella, but the inclusion of Tugger, that private auditions may have already taken place to cast the principal vocalists.
