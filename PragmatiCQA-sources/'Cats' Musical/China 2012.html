The Chinese production of Cats (《猫》中文版) was a slow tour around major cities in China. Starting in Shanghai on 17 August 2012, then playing in Beijing in December 2012, the show continued to visit other cities throughout 2013 and 2014.

The Chinese version of Cats is the 15th language version (after English, German, Japanese, Korean etc) of the musical.

Production Specifics

While the production was based on the Australasian tour and directed by Jo-Anne Robinson , the producers made efforts to make the show more relevant to their audience, with local dialects and references in the translation and Asian influences in the costume and makeup. This included the introduction of a new ensemble character Grumbuskin , a "Chinese martial arts cat". The translations were done by a team of over 10 experts, enthusiasts and college students, led by Xue Fan (薛范).

As with the Australasian productions, the show included all the swings as named on-stage characters .

Television Specials

Two television documentaries aired in Shanghai that followed the making of the Chinese production, from auditions to the premiere.

  • "Cat's Time" - produced by United Asia Live Entertainment (亚洲联创) and aired by the Shanghai Media Group (SMG). in 16 parts.
  • "中国猫 演出开始" ("Chinese Cats - Performances Begin") - an episode on Cultural Sky that aired on the Shanghai Arts and Humanities Channel. in 6 parts.
