Yokohama Baseball Stadium (横浜スタジアム) is a baseball stadium in Yokohama Park, Kanagawa Prefecture in Japan. The stadium is the oldest multi-purpose stadium constructed in Japan, but it is mainly used for baseball. The venue opened in 1978, and has a total capacity of 34,046. Yokohama Baseball Stadium was selected to co-host the baseball and softball competitions at the 2020 Summer Olympics , together with Fukushima Azuma Baseball Stadium , when the sport returned to the Olympic Games after a thirteen-year hiatus. The capacity of the stadium during the Olympic Games was 35,000.
