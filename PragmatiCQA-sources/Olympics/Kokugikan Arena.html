Kokugikan Arena (国技館) or Ryōgoku Kokugikan (両国国技館) is a world-renowned arena and the home of sumo wrestling, but the arena is also used for other events, such as boxing tournaments and pop concerts. The building was opened in 1909 but was rebuilt and reopened in 1985. The arena is formed in a shape of a bowl to make sure that spectators are able to watch the bouts from wherever they are within the stadium. The arena was used for the boxing competition at Tokyo 2020 , and had a capacity of 7,300 during the Olympic Games, and a capacity of 11,098 outside of Olympic competition.
