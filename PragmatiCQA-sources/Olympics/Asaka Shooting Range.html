Asaka Shooting Range (陸上自衛隊朝霞訓練場) is a shooting range originally built to host shooting competitions at the 1964 Summer Olympics , and the venue was later adapted and reused to host shooting at the 2020 Summer Olympics . Despite being located in Tokyo Prefecture, it was not part of the Tokyo Heritage Zone during the 2020 Summer Olympics, as one of fifteen venues outside of the Tokyo clusters. In 2021, it had a capacity of 3,000 at clay shooting events, 800 at rifle qualifications, and 2,400 at rifle finals.
