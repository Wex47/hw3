The women's 100 metre of Athletics 2020 during the 2020 Summer Olympics was held on 30-31 July 2021 in the Tokyo Olympic Stadium . With 72 athletes competing, the event was won by Elaine Thompson-Herah from Jamaica , with Shelly-Ann Fraser-Pryce from Jamaica taking the silver medal and Shericka Jackson from Jamaica taking bronze.

Format

The competition exists out of three rounds, each with races of up to nine athletes. In each round, the top two with the fastest time advance to the next round, together with a number of fastest non-qualifiers. The fastest competitor in the final is the winner of the event.

Preview

Seventy-two athletes qualified for the event with seemed to become a battle between the three Jamaican athletes. Shelly-Ann Fraser-Pryce was the reigning world champion and won Olympic gold in 2008 and 2012, but Elaine Thompson-Herah was the defending Olympic champion in the 100 metres and the 200 metres and won the Pan-American Games. The third Jamaican athlete Shericka Jackson won the bronze medal in the 400 metres in 2016 and at the world championships in 2019, but had little international experience in the shortest distance. Marie-Josee Ta Lou seemed to be the biggest contender for the three, having won the bronze and silver at the two most recent world championships. Other big names and medal contenders included Blessing Okagbare , Dina Asher-Smith , Javianne Oliver , Teahna Daniels , and Ajla del Ponte .
