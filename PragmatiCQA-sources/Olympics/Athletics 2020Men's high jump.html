The men's high jump of Athletics 2020 during the 2020 Summer Olympics was held on 30 July-1 August 2021 in the Tokyo Olympic Stadium . With 33 athletes competing, the event was won by Mutaz Essa Barshim from Qatar and Gianmarco Tamberi from Italy , and Maksim Nedasekau from Belarus taking bronze.

Format

The competition exists out of two separate rounds. In the qualification, each competitor attempts to jump over a bar set at a certain height, with three failed attempts at the same height ending their competition. After four set heights, the twelve best performance advance to the final, including athletes tied for twelfth place. In the final, the qualified athletes once again attempt to jump over a certain height, but contrary to the qualification, the competition is continued until there is only a single competitor remaining.

Preview

Thirty-three athletes qualified for the event with Mutaz Essa Barshim as one of the big favorites for the title, after winning the world championships in 2019 and 2017, as well as taking silver at the previous edition of the Olympic Games. Some of his biggest challengers would be Ilya Ivanyuk , the bronze medalist of the most recent world championships, Maksim Nedasekau , the silver medalist of the most recent European championships and the reigning indoor European champion, and Mikhail Akimenko , the silver medalist of the most recent world championships. Juvaughn Harrison was also one of the athletes to look out for, just like home representative Naoto Tobe , Brandon Starc , and Andriy Protsenko . Other potential outsiders also included Gianmarco Tamberi , Django Lovett , Loic Gasch , and Shelby McEwan .
