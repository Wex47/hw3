Blake Pieroni is a swimmer who has competed for the United States. He competed at the 2016 and 2020 Summer Olympic Games.

He was part of the men's relay swimming team that won a gold medal in the 4x100 metres freestyle. He took part in the third leg of the second heat, following Ryan Held and preceding Anthony Ervin . With the sixth split time of the third legs of all sixteen teams in the heats, he helped the American team to the win in the second heat, despite staying just behind James Roberts during his entire leg. In the final, Held was the only team member to remain in the team, with Michael Phelps , Caeleb Dressel , and Nathan Adrian taking up the remaining three spots, winning a gold medal in the event.

Five years later, he returned to the Olympic stage in a postponed edition in Tokyo, and just like in 2016 only in relay events, after he just finished third in the national trials. He first swam in the second leg of the second heat of the 4x100 metres freestyle relay. Opening swimmer Brooks Curry brought the team to a fifth place after the first leg, and Pieroni got one place back, starting the push to get the team to first place in the heat and second-fastest time in the heats, behind Italy. With Caeleb Dressel as the starting swimmer in the final, the United Stated States started of very strong, immediately taking the lead in the race. Pieroni once again swam the second leg and he maintained the lead of about 0.3 seconds to Florent Manaudou , with the United States winning the event with over a second ahead of Italy. In the double distanced relay, he competed in the fourth leg in the second heat, handing over from Patrick Callan who was in a tight battle with Elijah Winnington for second place in the heat. Pieroni lost about half a second on his direct opponent Zac Incerti to bring the United States to a third place and fifth-fastest time in the heats. In the final, he was one of three swimmers to be replaced, with the team finished just outside of the medals on fourth place. He also raced in the fourth freestyle leg in the first heat of the 4x100 metres medley relay, following Tom Shields . With his contribution, the team ended on fourth place in the first heat and went through to the final with the seventh-fastest time. Zach Apple took his place in the final with the United States winning the event in a new world record.
