The women's pole vault of Athletics 2020 during the 2020 Summer Olympics was held on 2-5 August 2021 in the Tokyo Olympic Stadium . With 31 athletes competing, the event was won by Katie Nageotte from United States , with Anzhelika Sidorova from ROC taking the silver medal and Holly Bradshaw from Great Britain taking bronze.

Format

The competition exists out of two separate rounds. In the qualification, each competitor attempts to jump over a bar set at a certain height, with three failed attempts at the same height ending their competition. After four set heights, the twelve best performance advance to the final, including athletes tied for twelfth place. In the final, the qualified athletes once again attempt to jump over a certain height, but contrary to the qualification, the competition is continued until there is only a single competitor remaining.

Preview

Thirty-one athletes qualified for the event with plenty of medal contenders. Reigning world champion Anzhelika Sidorova seemed one of the big favorites, but Katie Nageotte had performed better in 2021. Defending Olympic champion and 2017 world champion Katerina Stefanidi was also featured in the field, just like defending silver medalist Sandi Morris and Commonwealth Games champion Alysha Newman . Other medal contenders also included Holly Bradshaw , Iryna Zhuk , South American champion Robeilys Peinado , and Olympic silver medalist of 2012 Yarisley Silva .
