The women's mountain bike cross-country of Cycling 2020 during the 2020 Summer Olympics was held on 27 July 2021 in the Izu MTB Course . With 38 athletes competing, the event was won by Jolanda Neff from Switzerland , with Sina Frei from Switzerland taking the silver medal and Linda Indergand from Switzerland taking bronze.

Format

The competition exists out of a single race, in which all competitors conduct multiple laps on a course of 4,100 metres. The competitor with the fastest time is the winner of the event.

Preview

Thirty-eight athletes with Pauline Ferrand Prevot as the woman to beat. The Frenchwoman had won the previous two editions of the world championships, respectively beating Eva Lechner and 2017 world champion Jolanda Neff for the gold. Rebecca McConnell won bronze at both events, with the silver medalist of the most recent European championships Anne Terpstra also in the field. Other big names and medal contenders included the 2018 world champion Kate Courtney , the under 23 world champion Loana Lecomte , Sina Frei , and Eva Lechner .
