Koros were small villages inhabited by mostly Matoran , with a Turaga to lead the village.

After the Great Rescue , the Toa Metru chose the locations of the Koros where the Matoran would live until they would return to Metru Nui .

Each Koro on Mata Nui had a Suva for its Toa . All Koros on Mata Nui were destroyed by the Bohrok .

Known Koros

Mata Nui

  • Ta-Koro was located on a lake of lava, in the shadow of the Mangai Volcano . This was where the Ta-Suva and the Wall of History were located. It also had a stone bridge which could descend into the lava if Ta-Koro was in danger. It was led by Turaga Vakama and guarded by Toa Tahu . It was later destroyed by Rahkshi .
  • Ga-Koro was a floating koro, near the coast of Lake Naho . The Ga-Suva was located there. It was led by Turaga Nokama and guarded by Toa Gali .
  • Po-Koro was located in a desert in Po-Wahi . It was where the sport Kolhii was invented. The Po-Suva was also located in Po-Koro. It was led by Turaga Onewa and guarded by Toa Pohatu .
  • Le-Koro was located in the trees of Le-Wahi, near the lake Kanae. It was led by Turaga Matau and guarded by Toa Lewa . The Matoran also formed a Gukko Force to protect themselves.
  • Onu-Koro was an underground Koro. The Matoran living in Onu-Koro were miners and mined in the Great Mine . It was led by Turaga Whenua and guarded by Toa Onua .
  • Ko-Koro was located on Mount Ihu . It was led by Turaga Nuju and guarded by Toa Kopaka .

Elsewhere

  • De-Koro was a koro located on the Tren Krom Peninsula on the Northern Continent . It was home to a group of De-Matoran and surrounded by a jungle.
  • Ba-Koro was a koro on the Southern Continent . It was home to a small population of Ba-Matoran . At some point, the population disappeared.
