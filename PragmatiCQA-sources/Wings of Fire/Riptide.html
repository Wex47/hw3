Riptide is an adult male SeaWing who was introduced in The Lost Heir . He formerly served as a border guard in the Kingdom of the Sea and is currently the leader of the Talons of Peace . He is in a long-distance relationship with Tsunami .

Appearance

Riptide has been mentioned several times to have dark blue horns and metallic sky-blue scales, that are several shades paler than Tsunami's. He has a pale underbelly and dark blue eyes that are nearly black. He is large, fully grown, and well-built, with sharp, hooked claws and a massive tail. He has several scars, including an old bite mark on his tail, claw scars along his underbelly, and slashes across his snout. He is quite handsome, with a friendly-looking face.

Personality

Riptide is strong, brave, sheepish, dignified, wry, and secretive. He once stated that he would never be able to boss anyone into doing what he wants, and he is a good actor. Even after being imprisoned by Queen Coral , he was determined to protect his home with his life.

He dislikes his reputation as a lowly traitor because of his father, Webs 's crimes, yet he still respects the royal family's authority and does not often argue with them. He is intelligent and observant, noticing that Tsunami looked sad instead of guilty during a misunderstanding in The Lost Heir and concluded that she probably was not lying to him. Riptide is protective and loyal towards those he loves, although simultaneously respectful of their independence. He does not necessarily want to be a cunning or manipulative dragon, but is a rather good liar, and will use this skill when he feels it is necessary. He is often willing to offer his help and gave Tsunami sufficient Aquatic lessons. Riptide demonstrated being light-hearted, calm, and open-minded on numerous occasions, such as when he accepted the dragonets of destiny as allies after Tsunami explained that they were not trying to harm the SeaWings. Riptide seems to prefer to hear the hard truth rather than an easy lie, demonstrated when he asked for Tsunami's true opinion on his father. He is also courageous, as he fought against an enemy SkyWing group to help his tribe, despite being imprisoned by Queen Coral only hours earlier. He is fairly empathetic and does not seem to hold grudges very easily.

Biography

Pre-Series
When Riptide was two years old, Webs fled in the middle of a battle and later returned in secret to the Deep Palace to steal Tsunami's egg from the Royal Hatchery for the Dragonet Prophecy . Webs quickly became infamous for his treachery throughout the SeaWing tribe and consequently created an unfortunate situation for his son Riptide, who was also unfairly believed to be a traitor at heart because of his father's crimes. Queen Coral in particular hated the young SeaWing, and assigned him to the lowest of duties: guarding the border of SeaWing territory, as far away from the palaces and SeaWing civilization as possible. Despite the bad rumors, Riptide always wanted to know his father and eventually joined the Talons of Peace in hopes of seeing Webs again. The Talons would only ever tell Riptide that his father was safe, even though Riptide passed them important information about the SeaWings for years, likely ending when the war did.

The Dragonet Prophecy

The Lost Heir
Riptide first appeared on patrol duty in the Kingdom of the Sea. After he briefly spoke with Nautilus in Aquatic, Tsunami revealed herself to Riptide and he fled, but accidentally smacked into the side of a passing whale. After she pinned him down, Riptide flashed his scales at Tsunami, saying something in Aquatic, and Tsunami responded by flashing some of her scales. Then, Riptide lunged at her and Tsunami, thinking that Riptide was trying to attack her, swam to the surface after slashing her claws across his snout. It was then revealed that when Tsunami flashed her scales to show Riptide she was also a SeaWing, she had unknowingly flashed that she liked him in Aquatic, saying: "Hey, sparkling teeth, I totally love three of your claws but not the others, and I wish your snout was a herring so I could eat it, and also your wings sound like sharks snoring." After learning about her inability to speak and understand Aquatic, he became suspicious, but noticed Tsunami's sad expression and assumed that she was not lying about being a real SeaWing. Clay flew over, but Riptide, attempting to protect Tsunami from Clay, threw Tsunami down into the water, which Clay thought was hostile. After seeing Clay, he attacked him (as MudWings and SeaWings were enemies during the war ), getting into a fight with Clay, but Tsunami angrily separated them and explained that they were the dragonets of destiny; Riptide ceased his attack after her intervention. Clay realized that Riptide was not trying to hurt Tsunami as he protested, confused.

After blindfolding Clay and Sunny , Riptide reluctantly agreed to take Tsunami and her friends to the Summer Palace as Tsunami pointed out what her mother's reaction would be if he did not bring her back. They were confronted by the advance guard, although Tsunami eventually convinced Shark to let them through. After they entered the tunnel that led to the palace, Riptide removed Clay and Sunny's blindfolds and led them forward with glowing scales. After their arrival, Riptide told Tsunami a summary of the palace's architecture and history and took the dragonets of destiny to the top pavilion, where they could wait for Queen Coral to return from the Deep Palace. After Queen Coral and Anemone arrived, Riptide went back to guard duty under the queen's annoyed orders.

As Tsunami and several other SeaWings swam to an island near the Summer Palace to investigate a dead SkyWing (who was later revealed to be Kestrel ), Tsunami saw Riptide following her from a distance, and they waved to one another.

Later, as Tsunami was resting outside the Summer Palace in a cave near the sea, Riptide approached her. Riptide expressed that he'd like Tsunami to tell him about Webs, and although Tsunami listed several good things about her former guardian, Riptide asked to hear about his father's flaws as well, insisting that it'd be okay. Riptide also taught Tsunami a small portion of Aquatic, including the phrases 'squid-brain' and 'I will protect,' as well as how to indicate different types of danger. After Tsunami realized her friends may be in danger from Shark and his guards, she quickly left for the Summer Palace.

The following day, Webs had been found at the Summer Palace and Coral decided she wanted to execute him for his crimes. Soon after, Riptide was found sneaking around outside the palace as well. Coral accused Riptide of working with his father and threw them both in prison, despite Tsunami's protests. With the help of Blister , Coral was convinced that Webs was the assassin that had been killing off her heirs for years, and she wanted her last egg (which was being guarded by Tsunami) to be put back into the royal hatchery. However, Tsunami was convinced that Webs was not the assassin, and the egg was still in danger. She made a deal that if she caught the assassin, she had to let Riptide go. Queen Coral, thinking that she already had the assassins, agreed.

Later, Blister tricked Queen Coral into putting the dragonets in her prison, where Riptide and Webs were as well. Anemone, Tsunami's younger sister, slipped away from her mother and freed the dragonets. The dragonets then released Riptide and Webs from their cage and then prepared to leave. Riptide then revealed to Tsunami that he had joined the Talons of Peace to learn more about his father, though they told him nothing. Tsunami was mad at Riptide for lying to her, but at the same moment, the SeaWing palace was attacked by the SkyWings and the MudWings, who had followed Webs to the Summer Palace. The dragonets and Webs escaped, but Riptide stayed to fight against Burn 's alliance. Tsunami was not sure she had forgiven Riptide for lying to her, but she did hope to see him again.
The Brightest Night
Riptide was seen when Sunny, Tsunami, and Ochre went to the Talons of Peace's camp. There, he explained a few of the significant members of the Talons of Peace, such as Flame 's mother, Avalanche . He is also present at Burn's stronghold near the end of the book, and helps to hold Clay down while Peril burns the venom from the dragonbite viper out of Clay's leg.

The Lost Continent Prophecy

The Dangerous Gift
Riptide is first seen when the Pantalan dragons arrive at Sanctuary . Tsunami and him awkwardly try to hug each other. He told Tsunami that he was glad to see her and that he'd been hoping to show her all of the changes they'd made to Sanctuary, but he trailed off when he noticed the new arrivals with shock. He later stated that they can stay there for a few nights while they figure it all out. He later leaves with Tsunami to choose the messengers to call each of the queens to Sanctuary for a meeting. Riptide is later shown backing Tsunami up when Sundew claimed that they have all been hiding their magic from the Pantalan dragons — he confirmed that animus magic was very rare and only a few dragons possessed the power. He announced that the queens were arriving, and he was later seen in the council.

Relationships

Tsunami

Riptide is supportive of Tsunami, and he is physically affectionate towards her. Riptide's whole face lit up when Tsunami was mentioned, and Moonwatcher said that Tsunami thinks of Riptide all the time, and wants to hear from him. Riptide is supportive of Tsunami, and he is physically affectionate towards her. She makes him nervous, but she genuinely likes him, and hoped to see him again after their initial meeting. She likes having him around her, and although her first instinct was to yell at him for lying about the identity of his father, he looks at her without any expectation of her becoming queen or any judgement about her murder of Gill ; he only sees her as a dragon whom he likes very much. After she reassured him physically, Tsunami felt something tingle in the air between them. He helped her to forget about Gill's death for a while, and he took her front talons in his before they parted. He told her to be careful and that he would be close, if she needed him. Tsunami considered him to be someone that she could trust, but after discovering that he had lied to her about his affiliation with the Talons of Peace, her trust was broken in him, and she wondered if she could believe anything he said. After he apologized to her, she was unsure about her forgiveness towards her, but she still wanted a chance to decide. She wanted him to survive the War of SandWing Succession, and she wanted to meet him again after it was over. They are currently in a long-distance relationship.
