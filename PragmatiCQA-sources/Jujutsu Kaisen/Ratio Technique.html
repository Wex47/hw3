Ratio Technique ( 十劃呪法 , Tookaku Juhō ) is Kento Nanami 's innate technique . It grants him the ability to divide his target with lines and forcibly create a weak spot at the ratio point of seven to three.

Description

The user of this technique can mark their target's body with lines divided into tenths. If the user is able to strike one of these lines on exactly the seven-to-three ratio point, they deliver a critical hit to the subject. This allows them to deal significant damage on tougher adversaries and cleave weak opponent's in two in a single hit.

The lines don't have to measure up to the target's full length or wingspan. Their head, torso, biceps, and forearms are all among applicable limbs for division. This technique can be used on inanimate objects in addition to living beings.

Usage

Kento Nanami honed his innate technique to a masterful degree while working his way up to the status of a grade 1 sorcerer . He usually used it in tandem with his blunt sword but could activate it while fighting with his fists as well.

With his cursed technique activated on weaker targets, Nanami can easily cy them apart with his blunt sword despite its dull edge. On the roof of Kinema Cinema , Nanami divulged his technique and performed it for Yuji , instantly cutting off all the limbs of a transfigured human.

Against Mahito , Nanami first used his technique to break the special grade curse's arm. Mahito believed he reinforced his guard with cursed energy , but the critical hit still snapped his forearm in half. However, Mahito's Idle Transfiguration allows him to reshape his own soul and regenerate wounds in seconds. Additionally, Nanami is at a disadvantage when Mahito transfigures his miniature humans into long-ranged attacks. He openly admitted to himself that Mahito's innate technique is a bad matchup for his own.

Nanami planned accordingly in the jujutsu battle and divulged the specifics of his Ratio Technique to Mahito. Coupled with his "Overtime" binding vow , Nanami's cursed energy output rose exponentially. This boost in power allowed Nanami to unleash Ratio Technique: Collapse , an extension technique that can be applied to a wide area rather than just a single target. By destroying the underground battlefield using Collapse and trapping Mahito under the rubble, Nanami was able to escape with his life intact. Following this encounter, Mahito nicknamed Nanami the "7:3 Sorcerer".

While fighting alongside Yuji, who can hurt Mahito's soul directly, Nanami admitted openly his attacks didn't work on Mahito but added that he could still stifle the curse's movements. Working together, Nanami was able to immobilize Mahito by delivering critical hits while Yuji to landed attacks and dealt damage to his soul.

During the Shibuya Incident , Nanami dominated the curse user, Haruta Shigemo in their fight by creating weak points on his body and striking them with cursed energy enhanced punches. A simple backhand was enough to send Shigemo flying across the room and the blow broke his nose as well. Shigemo admitted this attack would have killed him if not for his cursed technique . Nanami's third and final punch sent Shigemo crashing through a wall, utterly defeating him and smashing his face in the process.

Fighting against Dagon , Nanami used Ratio Technique to create a weak point in the levitating cursed spirit's defenses, sending him crashing down into the ground. Inside Dagon's Domain, Nanami was able to use critical hits to take out the man-eating fish shikigami .

Extension Techniques
Collapse ( 瓦落瓦落 , Garagara ) : An extend extension technique that creates a weak point in the surrounding environment, allowing the user to deliver a critical hit to their surrounding area and destroy it.
