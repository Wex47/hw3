Reggie Star ( レジィ・スター , Rejii Sutā ) is a character in the Jujutsu Kaisen series. He is a jujutsu sorcerer from the past who was incarnated to participate in the Culling Game . Reggie lead his own group of sorcerers and served as an antagonist for the Tokyo No. 1 Colony Arc .

Appearance

Reggie is a man of relatively average height and build with a toned physique. He has light-colored hair that has likely been dyed while his eyebrows and goatee are both darker colored. The tattoo on his left arm is three kanji that translates to "indomitable" in English.

Reggie doesn't wear conventional clothing other than his boxer briefs. Instead, he attaches numerous paper tags to his body. Enough so to cover his entire torso all the way down to his upper thighs. His arms, legs, and feet are all left completely bare.

Personality

Reggie was an intelligent individual with a playful attitude. He adapted to the violence and cruelty of the Culling Game quickly, focusing solely on gathering strong allies while eliminating the weak or anyone who opposed him. Reggie used Remi , a cute girl, to lure in potential allies or targets to gain points from. He claimed he would never kill her for points and then immediately turned around and treated her as completely disposable in battle the next moment.

Reggie's callous attitude extended to his other allies as well. The associates he was looking to collect were just tools he could use to ensure his own progression through the Culling Game. When Chizuru Hari perished, Reggie was embarrassed that his teammate would be killed without even utilizing their cursed technique . He only wished to avenge Hari to make up for lost points.

While Reggie wanted to find allies, he enjoyed conflict and would dispose of anyone he deemed unworthy. More than willing to engage in a fight, Reggie acknowledges his opponent's strength while remaining supremely confident in himself. After seeing Megumi pull his punches, Reggie warned the young sorcerer to fight earnestly and to kill otherwise he'd end up dead.

Reggie believed that sorcerers are con-artists by trade. This outlook informed not only his attitude but Reggie's fighting tendencies as well. While fighting Megumi, Reggie claimed he didn't have any big moves purposely to mislead his opponent. His belief that sorcerers were just scammers was only reinforced when Megumi eliminated Reggie by outsmarting him in a similar manner. Reggie took his defeat well and respected his "executioner" despite being from different time periods. He handed over all his points to Megumi and asked that he let fate toy with him before dying like a fool.

Synopsis

Tokyo No. 1 Colony Arc

Reggie partook in the Culling Game after being revived in the present day. Operating out of Shinjuku he aimed to build up a team of sorcerers, using Remi lure potential allies to his base in an apartment building and killing those he considered too weak. One of these unsuspecting targets was Megumi Fushiguro who was offered a chance to join Reggie after displaying his Divine Dog technique. During negotiations the two try to bluff each other with lies and misdirection. The conversation leads to Reggie and Megumi discussing Kenjaku and the purpose of his game. Reggie believed that the strong players in the different colonies were killing the weak competitors too quickly considering how the Culling Game's rules were designed to account for a lengthy contest. It was his belief that Kenjaku would reveal the game's true purpose and introduce a major change to the game when only the strong were left alive. Reggie's plan to build a team of strong allies and stockpile points was his attempt to prepare for whatever this revelation is. Megumi refused to join and a fight broke out.

Chizuru Hari ambushed Megumi but is quickly thrown aside while Reggie stabs Divine Dog: Totality with several knives. Protecting Remi from Iori Hazenoki 's explosions left Megumi injured but the sudden announcement a new rule has been added convince him to fight more aggressively and kill Haru. Chasing Megumi to street level resulted in Reggia and Hazenoki being accosted by comedian-turned-sorcerer Fumihiko Takaba who allied with Megumi. Megumi's attempt to avoid further conflict fails after Reggie refuses. Takada fought Iori while Reggie battled Megumi with their differing summoning skills. After realising the boy cycled through his Shikigami to let them recover from damage Reggie chased Megumi into a gymnasium believing his Divine Dog was too damaged for use.

Reggie proves his deceptive nature after claiming neither he nor Megumi could use any big attacks, only to summon two trucks to crush Megumi. Despite a successful string of attacks Reggie underestimated his opponent's skill and is quickly caught in Megumi's domain expansion . Reggie attempted to protect himself with Hollow Wicker Basket but failed to account for Chimera Shadow Garden expanding Megumi's other abilities tremendously and suffers a brutal beating from the boy's clones, shikigami and shadow abilities as a result. With the situation being much worse than he expected a domain to be, Reggie realised that Megumi's ability to store objects inside his shadow is limited by the weight he is able to carry. By expanding his shadow into a domain, Megumi had added everything in the gymnasium to the total mass he was forced to carry. To exploit this Reggie flooded the Shadow Garden with water in addition to summoning cars using Contractual Re-Creation , this dramatically increased the weight bearing down on Megumi and pined him to the floor. Despite being forced to focus his cursed energy into strengthening his legsto avoid being crushed under the pressure. Grinning, Megumi turns the tables by dropping Max Elephant onto Reggie. While Megumi was able to strengthen his body with Cursed Energy to avoid immediate damage, catching the Elephant broke Reggie's leg bones and placed him at a disadvantage in this sudden life-or-death endurance match turning the battle into an endurance test. Reggie is eventually crushed and forced into the water-like depths of Megumi's shadow. He escaped by using a car to reach the surface and unleashed his biggest attack: dropping a house on Megumi to either crush him directly or with the weight inside his domain.

Megumi countered by dispelling his domain and the house crashed through the gymnasium floor, sending both fighters tumbling into the underground pool beneath the gym where Megumi grabs Reggie underwater. Realising he can't escape the boy's grip without reducing the crushing weight still left in his shadow; Reggie dispelled his summoned objects still inside Megumi's shadow and broke free to avoid drowning. The fight became a fist fight where Reggie was convinced Megumi would risk leaving himself open to summon a shikigami and waited for the perfect time to strike. He is struck down by a sneak attack from the Divine Dog: Totality , an attack Reggie failed to account for after an entire battle of tricks and deception caused him to forget the creature he assumed was too damaged to fight. As he died, Reggie transfered his 41 points to Megumi under the belief it would make the game more interesting. His dying request was for Megumi to be fate's plaything and to die like a fool.

Abilities and Powers

Overall Skill : Reggie is a powerful jujutsu sorcerer who commands respect from his group of Culling Game players, who are all strong sorcerers in their own right. He was able to overpower Divine Dog: Totality , one of Megumi's most powerful shikigami while its caster was withholding some of his strength. Even though Megumi was holding back, Reggie was able to pierce Divine Dog's mouth with two kitchen knives before its master even knew what happened.

Reggie works well together with Iori Hazenoki . He is capable of throwing the paper tags that cover his body as projectiles soaked in gas. Combined with Iori's body-part bomb technique, the two sorcerers can produce massive explosions.

Enhanced Speed : Reggie was able to outspeed and react to Max Elephant easily and counter Megumi even though the first-year had caught him off-guard.

Master Hand-to-Hand Combatant : Reggie is an extremely capable and experienced hand to hand fighter. He was even able to briefly overpower Fushiguro in a simple fistfight.

Expert Swordsman : Reggie is a skilled swordsman and was able to use it with his cursed technique to injure and overpower Megumi Fushiguro and force the first-year on the defensive.

High Tactical Intellect : Reggie is an intelligent individual who plans ahead to make sure situations that may come up favor his survival. He used Remi to lure in potential allies or victims to gather points in the Culling Game. This was successfully lead Megumi into a trap in Shinjuku where Reggie and his allies ambushed the Tokyo Jujutsu High student.

By using this strategy, he was able to survive the early stages of the Culling Game and deduce that Kenjaku's main goal for the event is not a ritual for the people of Japan. Instead, Reggie believes that Kenjaku will reveal something once only strong jujutsu sorcerers remain, and then the Culling Game will have served its purpose.

Jujutsu

Cursed Energy Manipulation
Great Cursed Energy : Reggie possesses a large amount of cursed energy and is able to continuously use his cursed technique without exhausting himself in the slightest.
Innate Technique
Contractual Re-Creation ( 再契 ( さいけっ ) 象 ( しょう ) , Saikesshō ) : Reggie's innate technique allows him to recreate contracts, usually receipts. This allows him to manifest what is written in the receipt in real life, even if it is not a physical object . Objects that he manifests behave like Shikigami , to which he is able to give commands to them .
Barrier Techniques
Hollow Wicker Basket ( 彌 ( いや ) 虚 ( こ ) 葛籠 ( つづら ) , Iyako Tsuzura ) : This is an esoteric technique and the predecessor to Simple Domain . Like Simple Domain it sets up a barrier around the user which protects them from the sure-hit factor of a Domain Expansion .
