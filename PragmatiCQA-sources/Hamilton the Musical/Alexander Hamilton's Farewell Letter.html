Alexander Hamilton's Farewell Letter was the last piece of correspondence Alexander Hamilton ever wrote. It was written to Elizabeth (Eliza) Schuyler Hamilton , his wife, prior to his duel with Aaron Burr .

The Letter

The full, original letter can be found . The letter does not show Alexander to be very hopeful of his survival, which speaks true to how historian's describe his attitude at the time, said to be close to suicidal. It can also be noted that he signs off by asking Eliza to "embrace all my darling children", when in his document, The Reynolds Pamphlet, he writes "Mrs. Hamilton with her children being absent on a visit to her father", showing his change of ownership of his children depending on his attitude.

The letter was written a week before the duel, not the morning of, as depicted in Hamilton .

Musical

Alexander is seen writing the letter in Best of Wives and Best of Women while Eliza lies in bed beside him. The title of the song and the last line is taken from Hamilton's sign-off line in the letter

The writing of this letter follows sixth rule in The Ten Duel Commandments

While he doesn't take this rule literally, wherein he doesn't literally tell her where he's been, but he makes it clear what happened and why it happened.
