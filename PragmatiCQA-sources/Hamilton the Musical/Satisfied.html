"Satisfied" is the 11th song of Act One of Hamilton .

Angelica Schuyler realizes that though she pushed away her feelings for Alexander for the sake of her sister, she will never be satisfied or find a way to make her desire for what she cannot have disappear.

Trivia

  • The song uses music that Lin-Manuel Miranda originally wrote for Karen Olivo to be used with lyrics she had written. When Miranda was writing "Satisfied", he realized the music he had written two years earlier would be perfect for the song. He called Olivo and begged to use the music. Coincidentally, Olivo ended up playing Angelica Schuyler, the primary character in "Satisfied", in the Chicago production .

Historical Inaccuracies

In Satisfied and part of Helpless, Angelica is shown to be distraught and regretful over her part in Eliza’s marriage with Alexander. She felt that she may have ended up married to him, when this is not true. Angelica was actually married to one John Carter, (later revealed to be John Church) at the time. The musical conveniently skips over this detail for the sake of a dramatic continuity.
