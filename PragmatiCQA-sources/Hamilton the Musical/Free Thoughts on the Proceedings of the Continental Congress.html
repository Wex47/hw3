Free Thoughts on the Proceedings of the Continental Congress was an open letter to the farmers of New York , written by Samuel Seabury

History

The Letter was printed in 1774 as a protest toward to rising revolution, and a defense toward the Constitution.

Musical

The Protesting Letter was referenced in " Farmer Refuted " by Samuel Seabury himself. The title of the song referenced the fact that Samuel wasn't a politician, but a Royalist Farmer who didn't wish to see his way of life upturned. It also references Alexander Hamilton's refute paper The Farmer Refuted .
