Allegra Garcia (born February 23, 1999) is a reformed criminal and meta-human with the ability to manipulate electromagnetic energy. She is also a close friend of Iris West-Allen , Frost and Chester P. Runk as well as Chester's crush. After clearing her name from being framed by her late cousin, Esperanza Garcia , she became a writer of The Central City Citizen and a member of Team Flash and Team Citizen .

After Allegra got freed from the charges, she got a job as a writer in The Central City Citizen . She found evidence Harrison Wells was alive but later discovered it was Nash Wells instead. Later, Allegra helped Nash, and he revealed about The Monitor , the multiverse , and the Flash's secret identity .

Allegra joined Team Flash and helped out in their missions. After Nash sacrificed himself for Barry to get his powers back, Allegra helped rebuild Central City and tried her best to help Frost . Later, Allegra looked for Esperanza. After multiple battles with her, Esperanza died in Allegra's arms. After Esperanza's death, Allegra became the supervising editor for the newly evolved Central City Citizen Media .

Biography

Original multiverse

Early life

Allegra Garcia was born on February 23, 1999 in Central City , into a family of criminals. Her father walked out on her when she was a baby. Following in her family's footsteps, Allegra became a juvenile delinquent and was frequently in and out of juvenile facilities for committing various crimes. At the age of 13, she was once arrested by detective Joe West for robbing liquor stores. Joe often visited Allegra in juvie, but she would refuse to speak with him.

On the night of December 11, 2013, Allegra got into a fight with her cousin, Esperanza , and her gang at the Juvenile Facility when she wanted out of the gang. Suddenly, the Particle Accelerator exploded and its energy surged across Central City . Allegra and Esperanza were blasted with the waves of the dark matter , giving them the same powers of electromagnetic manipulation, though Esperanza was presumed dead from the blast. When Allegra's powers were discovered, she was eventually transferred to the Meta-human wing of Iron Heights Prison , despite being underage.

Villainous cousin

Allegra believed that her cousin was still alive, due to a series of murders that involved people being burnt to death with pure UV radiation. A witness of one of the murders noticed the tattoo on Esperanza's neck which Allegra had a matching ratio of; as a result, Allegra was mistakenly arrested by CCPD . Given no one would believe her hunches about her cousin being alive, she decided to plead guilty. She was almost charged with the max sentence of all crimes committed until Cecile Horton sensed she wasn't guilty with her empathic abilities and held off further prosecution and told Judge Hankerson she had new evidence to stall until she knew the truth. Allegra eventually made bail.

Shortly after Allegra made bail she broke out of her meta cuffs and later went to the witness who claimed to have seen her at the scene of the crime due to Allegra and her cousin sharing the same tattoo. She went to the witness' apartment only to find him dead with ultraviolet burn covering his face. She was later seen by Iris West-Allen and Ralph Dibny in the apartment when they were also visiting the witness for an interview. Allegra in a panic accidentally blasted Ralph in the face with UV Blast and ran but was later caught by Joe West and arrested for a parole violation. Allegra later tried to explain to Joe that she didn't murder the witness and was only trying to talk to him in an attempt to prove her innocence. She was later approached by Cecile who demanded to know who killed the victims but Allegra refused as she believed they wouldn't believe her if she told them and asked for her lawyer.

During interrogation Cecile opened up to Allegra about her empathetic abilities and as a result, Allegra finally opened up to Cecile about the night she got powers and revealed that her cousin was also hit and that she was convinced she was dead but also thinks she may have committed those crimes as the witness ID'd her off a tattoo she and her cousin shared though she was in doubt and said it was impossible. Cecile later contacted Iris and Ralph to check the juvenile records they had and see if they can pull up any file on Esperanza and they discovered she had been taken out of the custody of the juvenile facility and transferred to a very redacted organization revealing she had been alive the entire time. Shortly after Iris tried to warn Cecile their phone call was interrupted by an EMP causing the power at CCPD to go out. Esperanza attacked the officers are CCPD demanding they bring her Allegra or they would all burn. Cecile and Allegra later tried to escape but was later stopped by Esperanza. Allegra tried to convince Esperanza to stop and that she wasn't being herself. Esperanza confirmed her statement and said she only goes by the name Ultraviolet. She was unable to get through to her cousin. Cecile guarded Allegra and confronted Esperanza and told her she wouldn't let her hurt Allegra. They were almost blasted by Esperanza until The Flash showed up and fought off Ultraviolet and eventually was able to overpower her.

Personality

Allegra was originally a criminal during her youth. As an adult, she is now a regular citizen trying to get her life back on track. Allegra sees herself as the light to her cousin, Esperanza Garcia 's darkness and is afraid to become like her.

Allegra liked Iris West 's articles in her Flash blog, since they were an inspiration for her in her convict days, and this led her to join The Central City Citizen . Allegra is adamant about being viewed as an individual; so she dislikes Harrison Nash Wells , because he constantly sees his adopted daughter, Maya when he views her. However, after the events of McCulloch Technologies, where they fought against Eva's Team and Nash saved her life, for the first time she was grateful to him again, and she started to warm up to him and accept him as a friend.

Allegra is insecure about her job as the supervising editor for the newly evolved Central City Citizen Media , since the writers don't listen to her and ignore her ideas. She told Iris that she wasn't ready and had no more experience than them, but Iris neglected the comments and told her she had a voice, since she has lived her entire life in Central City.

Powers and abilities

Powers

  • Meta-human physiology: After Allegra was blasted by the dark energy of the particle accelerator explosion, this altered her DNA and supercharged her cells, giving her access to her abilities.
    • Bioluminescence : Allegra was able to use her UV radiation harnessing to surround herself with glowing energy.
    • Electromagnetic wave manipulation : Allegra is able to manipulate a range of different frequencies and wavelengths of radiation on the electromagnetic spectrum. Allegra was able to also jam radio communication between the Godspeeds.
    • Electromagnetic vision : The entire electromagnetic spectrum or most of it is visible to Allegra. She can tune her vision to interpret the various ranges of energy. She can see radio waves (which include AM, FM, and TV frequencies), radar, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, power waves, x-rays, gamma rays, and even cosmic rays.
    • Eye transformation : Allegra is able to change her eye color, as seen when she was fighting Ultraviolet .
    • Radiokinesis : Allegra is able to generate multiple forms of radiation.
      • Electromagnetic pulse emission : Allegra can emit a surge of electromagnetic energy which can disrupt all technology nearby.
      • Ultraviolet blast : Allegra can release blasts of UV radiation from her hands.

Abilities

  • Bilingual: As a native Mexican, Allegra speaks Spanish fluently, in addition to English .
  • Keen-level intellect/Expert investigator: As a reporter of The Central City Citizen , Allegra is an exceptional investigator, as she follows every lead and rumor to discover the truth behind any story.
  • Expert hand-to-hand combatant/Martial artist: Allegra possesses impressive fighting skills as she was taught to fight and defend herself at a young age as she was able to handle her own against Esperanza Garcia who was a highly trained assassin.
  • Stealth: Allegra possesses a high degree of street-smarts as she was able to escape and always leave the scene as well as she is able to get around Central City and was able to hide and escape the cops when she was framed by Esperanza and was able hide from her during the police lockdown when Esperanza was after her.

Equipment

  • Protective suit : Allegra uses a black tactical suit when in the field.
  • Ruby: Before a battle with Eva McCulloch 's team , Nash Wells gave Allegra this red stone, which induces nightmarish hallucinations in its victims when combined with her powers.
  • Frost jacket : Before Frost left Central City with her mother , she let Allegra borrow a jacket from a previous version of the Frost suit.
  • Harrison Nash Wells' techno fanny pack : An equipment bag with Nash's name tag that she wore over one of her shoulders with a strap that winds around the chest.
  • Harrison Nash Wells' gauntlet : Allegra wears a gauntlet on her wrist which she uses for various purposes.
  • Smoke bombs : Allegra used a smoke bomb to catch up to Barry, and to teleport them back to S.T.A.R. Labs.

Behind the scenes

  • In the DC comics, Allegra Garcia is the daughter of the supervillain Wavelength and a criminal with the power of electromagnetism. She debuted in Titans #28 .
    • Allegra was from Brazil in the DC comics and she was changed to be Hispanic on the show, despite her name still being spelled the same way. In Spanish, two Ls together make a Y sound, so with the spelling on the show it would be pronounced "Ayegra". This wouldn’t be the case if the show had retained her Brazilian origin.
    • Allegra was co-created by Eric Wallace , the executive producer of The Flash .
  • Allegra and Esperanza are the second pair of family members in the series who were struck by a dark matter wave simultaneously during a S.T.A.R. Labs particle accelerator explosion, resulting in developing the same powers. The first time was in season 1 of The Flash when brothers Mark and Clyde Mardon were hit by the dark matter at the same time, resulting in them both having atmokinetic abilities just as Allegra and Esperanza developed radiogenetic abilities.
    • However, in the Mardon brothers' case, Mark's powers were far more precies and potent than Clyde's, whereas Allegra's and Esperanza's powers appear to be virtually identical.
