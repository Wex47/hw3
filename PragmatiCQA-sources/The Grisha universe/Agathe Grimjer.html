Agathe Grimjer is the Queen of Fjerda . She is married to the King of Fjerda and is the mother of Prince Rasmus and her younger son. She is a very devout queen who prayed day and night for the health of her chronically ill son.

Biography

Pre-Series

The queen experienced six miscarriages before she was, with the help of a Grisha Healer , able to give birth to Rasmus. For moral support, she kept her best friend and lady-in-waiting Linor Rundholm with her in court during the course of her pregnancies. Agathe even refused to let her travel with her husband to the front; he eventually died in service. Once her son was born, she sent the Grisha Healer to be put to death. Despite the healer's assistance through the birth, Rasmus was born in a state of poor health that persisted to adulthood.

Six of Crows

The Queen of Fjerda is present at the party in the Royal Palace on the White Island during Hringkälla . She is seated at a throne, surrounded by other party guests.

Rule of Wolves

Agathe is seated on her throne beside her husband during Heartwood . When Rasmus collapses, she shouts for help, as royal guards pull Hanne and Nina , disguised as Mila Jandersdat, away from the prince. After Maidenswalk, Nina is summoned by the queen to the old audience room where she is questioned. Agathe wonders why such an unknown woman would dare to take her son's hand and pray over him. She claims that she believes Nina is a spy and is sending people to investigate her old village. As Agathe begins to recount her challenging pregnancy, Nina seizes the opportunity to take back power. Nina uses her parem altered powers to act as a prophet of Djel . She exposes her six miscarriages and use of a Grisha Healer to give birth to Rasmus. Agathe denies it at first claiming that he would be cursed if that were true. Nina then responds that he is not cursed and that he will grow strong and heal. Sending Nina away, the queen collapses on her knees and wails.

After Nina's prophecy, Queen Agathe watches Nina at every event and party she can. She also begins to pray more often, claiming that she has worn her dresses thin from kneeling.

During the royal hunt, Agathe is seated in the far corner of the tent. When she catches sight of Nina, she claims to want to make a trinket with the materials of her choice. In reality, Agathe wishes for a way to speak to Nina. Agathe tells Nina that Rasmus is getting stronger. Nina replies that Djel is not fond of the war. Just as Rasmus was about to leave for the hunt, Agathe commands him by his side to watch the riders instead. This does a blow to his confidence and reputation at court.

After The Bombing of Os Alta , Hanne uses her powers to make Rasmus sick. Two days later, Agathe sends a litter to the Brum family apartments for Nina, as she claims to have fallen ill of food poisoning. Nina is brought to the old audience room once more where Agathe asks if she needs to see her personal doctor. Nina stands up, freshly tailored and saint-like, as Agathe sends the guards away. Agathe tells Nina of Rasmus' dire condition: he has not risen from bed and is coughing blood. Nina tells Agathe that Djel is angered by the bombing, specifically because Fjerda bombed the Ravkan Royal Chapel where people worship the Djel's children- the Saints and Grisha. Agathe claims this idea as heresy to which Nina rolls her eyes back in her head, recounting details of Agathe's tough pregnancy before Pavlina , the Grisha Healer, was sent to her death. Nina further claims that Djel will forgive Agathe for Pavlina's death but, not the murder of more Grisha. Agathe sits aghast in her throne as Nina turns her back to the queen and walks out.

Physical description

Queen Agathe is described, with the rest of her family, as sylph-like. She wears the traditional white color of the royal family, always in the form of a gown. At the maidenswalk , she wore a circlet of opals on her head, her hair the color of pearls. Her posture is straight and her figure is described as elegant.
