The Ravkan religion , although organized, does not appear to have an official name and lacks a central deity, mainly relying on the worship of Saints . It is sometimes referred to as the Ravkan church or the Worship of the Saints .

History

The worship of the Saints is older than Ravka itself. Yaromir Lantsov , the first king who united Ravka, enlisted the help of monks, founded the First Altar and the Priestguard led by the Apparat , and established Ravka's state religion. The royal chapel in Os Alta is believed to be built on top of the site of Ravka's First Altar.

Worshippers have been persecuted in Fjerda .

Practices

Afterlife

In the Worship of the Saints, they believe in an afterlife called the bright lands, where the Saints greet you and escort you into death.

Holidays

  • Saints Day
  • Feast of Sankt Nikolai , held in winter

Trivia

  • Despite terms and organizations such as churches, saints, monks, etc, Leigh Bardugo has said the Ravkan religion is closer to Slavic paganism than Christianity. There is no Christ-equivalent in this world.
