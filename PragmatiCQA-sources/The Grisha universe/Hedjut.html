The Hedjut , also stylized as Hedjüt , are a people in northern Fjerda .

History

The Hedjut had a symbiotic relationship with the sildroher in the distant past. The Hedjut taught the sildroher human language and customs and and kept the secrets of the sea folk; the sildroher prevented storms from harming Hedjut boats and helped them fish. When the sildroher came to land, the Hedjut provided them with human clothes and helped carry the transformed sildroher out of the water. After Ulla fractured the northern coast into the Kenst Hjerte , the sildroher retreated permanently into the sea.

In contemporary Fjerda, the Hedjut are considered the "divine people of the north." People with Hedjut ancestry usually have tawny brown skin and thick, curly hair.

Culture

They were referred to as "fishermen of the north" by the sildroher.

The Hedjut speak a dialect distinct from Fjerdan . The word ulfleden means 'wolf-blooded' and refers to a child who behaves oddly; it is a compliment among the Hedjut but not among other Fjerdans.

Behind the scenes

Given their location in Fjerda, the Hedjut are likely based on the Nordic , as well as other Arctic indigenous groups.

Trivia

  • The name is stylized as Hedjüt in When Water Sang Fire and Hedjut in the King of Scars Duology .
