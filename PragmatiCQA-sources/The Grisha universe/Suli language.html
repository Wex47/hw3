History

Marie and Nadia Zhabin studied Suli at the Little Palace , where Suli was considered useful in the west and northwest. Sergei Beznikov claims that Suli is a dead language.

Influences

Although the Suli people were based partly on the Ashkenazi Jews , Romani , and various South Asian cultures , the real-world inspiration for the Suli language is unclear. Few Suli words are known in the Grishaverse, making it difficult to find derivations for words and similarities between Suli and Romani , Yiddish , and South Asian languages .

In Shadow and Bone , the "Suli dialect" is very similar to real-world Croatian . The Suli words in Shadow and Bone translate directly the same way from English to Croatian. However, the Suli in Six of Crows and Rule of Wolves no longer maintains this similarity.

Trivia

  • In Shadow and Bone , Suli is referred to as a dialect of Ravkan . However, in all subsequent books, Suli is treated as an entirely separate language rather than a dialect.
  • The Suli language does not have a word for "I’m sorry." When they do something wrong, instead of apologizing, they say that they will make amends.
