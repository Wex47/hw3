Tamurazaki Midori ( 田村崎緑 , Tamurazaki Midori , Jerry in the English version of the 2001 anime series) is a fictional character in the Shaman King manga and anime series. He is one of the Oyamada Family's employees.

Appearance

Midori wears glasses and a formal black suit with a white shirt and a necktie.

Personality

Midori does not believe in the afterlife and thinks shamans are people messing around with ghosts. He was very loyal to Oyamada Mansumi and stays with Mansumi's son Oyamada Manta to gather information on shamans for his employer.

History

Shaman Fights in Tokyo

Midori drives Oyamada Mansumi to the hospital where Manta is recovering. When Mansumi asks if his son has learned his lesson, Midori tells him that Manta refuses to transfer schools because the boy is too focused on his best friend, Asakura Yoh , as well as informing Mansumi about the investigation into Yoh's background. While visiting Manta, Midori apologizes to Manta for the investigation and then informs Mansumi that he has a meeting in Moscow.

Traveling to America

In a company helicopter, Midori flies Manta, Kyōyama Anna , and Tamamura Tamao to Mesa Verdede in America, where they are met by Asakura Hao . Later, Midori does not accompany Manta to the Patch Village.

Shaman Fight Second Round

In order to prevent Hao's followers from attacking the fleet of ships, Midori informs Peyote Diaz about Hao's ability to read minds and then coordinates with him to take down the others. When Peyote begins to attack his teammates, Midori shoots Mohamed Tabarsi in the face with a pistol. Kanna Bismarch attempts to use the Forbidden Killing Curse on Midori, but it does not kill him because he does not believe in the afterlife. He shoots down the girls of Team "Hana-Gumi" with an M134 Vulcan and watches as Peyote kills the others and himself. Midori then contacts Mansumi to tell him that they were all annihilated and the Shaman Fight will end soon. For the rest of the Shaman Fight, he watches the matches and observes Yoh through his van.

Invading the Plants

Midori later gives Mansumi information on the continent of Mū , saying it is a "world class discovery". However, Mansumi informs him that they did not come here to make a discover or seek the legend but to seek the wisdom of the Great Spirit . Manta later finds out that Midori has been contacting his father about the Shaman Fights and of the Great Spirit. In addition, Anna destroys Manta's phone, revealing that Midori put a bug on it to spy and gather information to track down the shamans.

After Hao's attack on the ships, Tamurazaki manages to disappear after Mansumi is captured by Anna.

Anime/Manga Difference

In the 2001 anime series, Midori is only seen once when he is taking Manta to America. In the English version of the 2001 anime series, he is named Jerry and he is Manta's uncle.

His role in the 2021 anime series more closely follows the events of the manga.
