The Mud Bowl is an annual event played by a team of seven 6th graders from P.S. 38 called the Bobcats , against seven sixth graders from Jefferson Middle School called the Jefferson Cavaliers. The Mud Bowl is a game of Ultimate Frisbee , but is called the Mud Bowl because the rain turns the playing field into mud and slop. It has been a tradition to play in rainstorms ever since the official 1st Mud Bowl.

History

1st Annual Mud Bowl

The Mud Bowl was invented by Martin Wright , Nate Wright 's dad. One day, Martin, along with his friends, challenged some sixth grade Jefferson students to a game of Ultimate Frisbee to win their Frisbee back. It rained hard during the game and Martin Wright was the hero of the game when he made a diving catch during overtime after a 50-yard-pass made by Simon Birch . Because of Marty's winning move, P.S. 38 won the Ultimate game 13-12. The fans soon called the game the Mud Bowl because of the rain and Mud. Jefferson demanded a rematch and the Mud Bowl has become an annual event since then. Unfortunately, after the 1st game, P.S. 38 lost for the next 36 years in a row, until Nate, Randy, and the rest of the gang won.

37th Annual Mud Bowl

During Nate Wright and Randy Betancourt 's peer counseling, it is revealed by Francis that the P.S. 38 students playing the 37th game lost to Jefferson 25-6.

38th Annual Mud Bowl

The team consisted of Nate, Francis, Teddy , Chad , Dee Dee , Ruby Dinsmore , and later Randy Betancourt was added to the team, which took some time for his fellow players to get used to. Once the game started, they were losing right away. After a quick team meeting, they used the Jefferson's player's height against them, and bumped it up to a tie. In the last ten seconds, Randy heaved the Frisbee across the entire field and Nate ran as fast as he could to catch it as the time ran out, defeating Nolan in the process, thus achieving a come-from-behind victory of 20-19.
