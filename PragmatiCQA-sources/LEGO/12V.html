Class 4

12V was a voltage standard for LEGO Trains sets from 1969 until 199 0. In contrast to the older battery powered 4.5V standard, the power of the 12V system came from a mains powered DC Transformer delivered by add-on center conductor rails.

In order to reach the newer 12V standard, the older – but still co-existing – 4.5V Trains system could be upgraded by using different 12V Trains accessories.

The second generation "gray" system introduced in 1980 brought much more functionality and expansion options, with the system able to support functioning lights inside the trains, remote controlled signals, power operated points/switches, decouplers and level crossings.

No further 12V sets were released after 1986, and the 12V powered trains were replaced by 9V trains in 1991. Spare parts and accessories for the 12V system (but not complete sets) remained on sale via LEGO mail order service until 1993.
