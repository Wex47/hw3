Elizabeth Weir is an accomplished and acclaimed American diplomat. After a personal request from U.S. President Henry Hayes in 2004 , that career came to an abrupt end so that she could assume the role of the first ever of civilian commander of Stargate Command , essentially trading international diplomacy for inter planetary diplomacy. Soon afterwards, she took on the mantle of original commander of the Atlantis expedition in the Pegasus Galaxy , a role she held up until 2007 when she was captured by the Asurans with Weir's physical body later dying when her consciousness was assimilated by the Asuran collective.

She came to lead the remnants of Niam's faction in their quest for ascension. She returned to Atlantis in 2008 to seek help after a failed attempt at Digital Ascension, but the Asurans proved dangerous, and she sacrificed herself to freeze the others in the cold of space.

However, she was later ascended by the Ascended Asgard Ran before being "unascended" later after she interfered by saving Rodney McKay . After returning to Earth, she was sent to stay on Atlantis by the IOA until they were convinced she was indeed the real Elizabeth Weir. Although allowed to return to working with the expedition, Weir was not allowed to lead it again, instead falling under the command of John Sheppard , her former military commander and the current interim leader of Atlantis.

Background information

Elizabeth Weir was very close with her late father and brought the pocket watch that he gave to her to Atlantis . Not much is known about her mother Katherine Weir other than that she appeared to her in her dream while being infected by nanites in 2006 , indicating that she was alive at the time. At some point, she became engaged to Dr. Simon Wallace and they lived together near Washington D.C. . ( SGA : " Rising ", " Home ", " The Real World ")

Before becoming involved with the Stargate Program , Weir was a skilled civilian diplomat who worked behind the scenes in the U.S. government for several years. An expert in international politics, Weir was well known for mediating for the United Nations . She brokered a dozen of the most sensitive international accords in modern history. She has two Ph.D.s, taught political science at Georgetown University , served in embassies all over the world and speaks five languages, including Russian and Latin . Though she began her career as a political activist lobbying against government spending on the military, she later decided to change things from the inside and entered the realm of government. She concluded that the best way to stop weapons proliferation was to try and end the need for them. Dr. Daniel Jackson referenced her work while drafting the Tau'ri-Tok'ra Treaty . ( SG1 : " Lost City, Part 1 ", " Lost City, Part 2 ")

She was handpicked by President Henry Hayes to replace Major General George S. Hammond at Stargate Command , as part of his restructuring of the program. Hayes indicated that her diplomatic background, and ideological problems with the military, would make her both an effective leader of the SGC and a suitable public face for the program if and when it was made public. Vice President Robert Kinsey also attempted to manipulate Weir as part of his own agenda, and the circumstances of her arrival led some members of SG-1 initially to distrust her. She allowed Colonel Jack O'Neill to go to Praclarush Taonas in search of the Lost City of the Ancients . In the ensuing confrontation with Anubis , however, she rebuffed Kinsey and made a good impression on the staff at the SGC, and seems to have struck a very quick friendship with Dr. Jackson. ( SG1 : " Lost City, Part 1 ", " Lost City, Part 2 ")

For most of her tenure, travel through the Stargate was discontinued as part of Hayes' reorganization, though Major Samantha Carter did successfully convince Weir to authorize a mission to the Othala galaxy using a recovered Goa'uld Tel'tak , so that Carter and Teal'c could seek help from the Asgard in saving the life of Colonel Jack O'Neill who had been placed in statis in the aftermath of the Battle of Antarctica .

Shortly afterward, the System Lords contacted Stargate Command for help in defeating Ba'al ; Weir and Dr. Daniel Jackson entered into negotiations with them. In order to prevent the System Lords from suspecting Earth 's vulnerability, she attempted to stall the talks, to the point of demanding Ba'al's territory in exchange for helping defeat him. The attempt failed, but she did grant political asylum to Camulus , whose domain had already been destroyed and released Yu and Amaterasu . After SG-1 returned from saving O'Neill, she informed him that she would be transferring to the Antarctic outpost as the new leader of the Atlantis expedition and that Jack himself was being promoted to Brigadier General and as such, would become the commander of Stargate Command . ( SG1 : " New Order, Part 1 ", " New Order, Part 2 ")

Alternate timelines

In an alternate timeline , Elizabeth Weir's expedition team including Dr. Rodney McKay and Colonel Marshal Sumner were all killed when the shield around Atlantis collapsed. Weir, along with Major John Sheppard and Dr. Radek Zelenka , managed to board a Puddle Jumper , but were thrown back in time to the siege of Atlantis by the Wraith . Sheppard and Zelenka were killed when the Jumper they were in crashed. Weir was healed by Janus , who built the time machine , and later secured her an audience with the Ancient Council , asking them to allow her to travel back to her time with a Zero Point Module that would prevent Atlantis from being flooded. While Melia was sympathetic to her plight, High Counselor Moros refused, fearing for the timeline, and offered her a chance to return to Earth with the Lanteans . Instead, Janus calculated that three ZPMs rotated sequentially would allow the city to survive ten thousand years. Janus programed a stasis pod for Weir to use, as well as included a Failsafe mechanism into the city. Janus told Moros that Weir went through the Stargate , when in fact she stayed behind. Every 3,300 years, Weir would awaken to rotate the ZPMs. Ten thousand years later, Weir was revived by the Atlantis expedition — coincidentally, on her own birthday—and told her story to them. However, she was already weak, and died by day's end. Elizabeth Weir poured her ashes over the city in a pot that Major John Sheppard had gotten her that day. ( SGA : " Before I Sleep ")

Powers

Hybrid/Asuran

While both a human- Asuran hybrid and a full-fledged Asuran, Weir displayed several abilities common to Replicators:

  • Wireless connection to Replicator subspace network : After her nanites were reprogramed and activated, Weir was able to achieve a wireless connection to the Replicator subspace network once Rodney McKay initiated the link. While connected, Weir displayed the ability to access any and all information known to the Replicator collective, including being able to see through their eyes, granting her a kind of omniscience. Weir was able to remain undetected while doing this as the Replicators didn't know to look for her. ( SGA : " Lifeline ")
  • Replicator mind probing : After her nanites were fully initialized, Weir displayed the same mind probing ability the Replicators possessed, even proving capable of using this to link directly with Oberoth , the most powerful Replicator. ( SGA : " Lifeline ")
    • Replicator Collective control : By linking directly with the collective through another Replicator, Weir displayed the ability to take full control of the Replicator collective and freeze them in place. However, due to still being part-human, Weir lacked the endurance to hold this control long. While Weir needed to connect directly with a Replicator to establish her control, she was able to maintain it briefly even after Oberoth removed her hand from his head, but lost control once her concentration was broken. ( SGA : " Lifeline ")
    • Illusion : While connected with Oberoth, Weir displayed the same ability as the Replicators to implant an elaborate illusion of her own making into the being she was directly connected with's mind. Oberoth, despite being a full Replicator, was unable to see through Weir's illusion right up until the moment she dropped the act. ( SGA : " Lifeline ")
    • Replicator termination : Unique to her, after having been turned into a full Replicator, Weir displayed the ability to terminate the life of another Replicator by linking with them then shutting down the bonds between their nanites. ( SGA : " Ghost in the Machine ")
  • Super strength : As a full Replicator, Weir is able to shove fellow Replicator Koracen up against a wall and pin him there with just one arm while she terminates him. ( SGA : " Ghost in the Machine ")
  • Invulnerability : After becoming a full Replicator, Weir possessed the same invulnerability to harm as the other Replicators possessed. ( SGA : " Ghost in the Machine ")

Ascended

While briefly Ascended , Weir gained all of the powers on Ascended being:

  • Dream control : Weir displayed the ability to enter the dreams of Rodney McKay and Teyla Emmagan to communicate with and guide them discreetly. She could also manipulate their dreams. ( SGA : " Legacy ")
  • Teleportation : In order to save McKay's life, Weir displayed the ability to teleport him from a Puddle Jumper to the George Hammond . ( SGA : " Inheritors ")

Behind the scenes

  • Weir was originally played by Jessica Steen in the Season 7 finale, but was soon replaced by Torri Higginson by Season 8 and Stargate: Atlantis .
  • Elizabeth Weir was originally named "Teresa Wright," then "Teresa Weir."
  • Torri was originally contracted to appear as a regular for six years. However, she was later given a recurring status after only three years.
  • When Weir was declared dead, Torri Higginson had been contracted for four episodes of Season 4. As of This Mortal Coil , she had only appeared in three episodes. This led to speculation that Weir was still alive, a theory given credence by Joseph Mallozzi , who mentioned on his blog that RepliKeller may have been lying. Confirmed deaths turning into survivals are nothing new to the Stargate franchise (the various deaths of Dr. Daniel Jackson and Apophis being prime examples). Since Weir appeared on the next episode, it is probable that RepliKeller was working for her all along and lied at her command.
  • Along with Rodney McKay , John Sheppard , Teyla Emmagan , Samantha Carter , Radek Zelenka , Chuck and Walter Harriman , she is one of only eight characters to appear in all five seasons of Stargate: Atlantis . However, Carson Beckett appears in Season One to Three while his clone appears in Seasons Four and Five.
  • Holly Dignard (stand-in for elderly Weir) previously played Holly in the Stargate SG-1 episodes " Inauguration " and " Lost City, Part 1 ".
