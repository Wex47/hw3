Terry (November 17th 1933 - September 1st 1945) was the dog who played Toto in The Wizard of Oz . She was a cairn terrier who belonged to Carl Spitz, a well-known movie dog trainer. She made her debut in with Shirley Temple in 1934, and played in sixteen films in total; after The Wizard of Oz, her most notable movie was (1941). She reappeared with Margaret Hamilton in the 1942 movie .

For the film, Terry earned $125 per week for her owner. She was injured during filming, when one of the Wicked Witch of the West 's Winkie guards stepped on her. She was out of action for a time, and replaced with a stand-in. Also for lighting and color tests that preceded filming, a stuffed animal was used.

She was buried in Spitz's backyard, but unfortunately her grave plus the rest of the property were razed to build a highway. In 2011 a monument statue dedicated to her was placed in the garden of the Hollywood Forever Cemetery.
