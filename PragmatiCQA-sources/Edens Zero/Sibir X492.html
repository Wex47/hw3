Sibir (シビル Shibiru ) is an elderly man and an older version of Sibir from X442. He was working for Drakken Joe as an in-debt slave.

Appearance

Sibir is a frail and relatively short elderly man in comparison to his younger self. He now has a mustache, wears a shirt, and his earrings are smaller. The only features he kept from his younger age is the tattoo that spells his name on his bald head and the piercings on his chin.

Personality

In contrast to his past self, Sibir has become kind and modest. Despite these changes, he still involves himself in dark operations as he feels he can not go back from it all, but will express regret for his actions. He is even willing to defy Drakken Joe, and help the Edens Crew in both timelines when they attacked Drakken's base of operation .

Synopsis

Belial Gore arc

World No. 29

When Shiki and his friends were in trouble with Laguna and Fie of the Element 4 , Sibir put up a smokescreen and told them to follow him where they take shelter at his small bar away from prying eyes and he helped apply first aid on Weisz ' wounds.

Suddenly, Fie breaks into Sibir's Bar to collect Weisz and informing him that his friends and medicine aren't coming, and told Sibir that there's punishment for harboring fugitives. When Sibir begs Fie to spare Weisz, the Flame Sniper tells him that he isn't killing anybody, unless they're a traitor to Drakken Joe. Fie then pulls out a pistol, aims it at Sibir and fires, killing him for treason.

A week after his and Shiki's death, the news covers his slaying, discovering that he was executed for treason against Drakken and asks people on the street about his death.

World No. 30

After Drakken Joe's defeat and arrest, Sibir became worried about the Interstellar Union Army 's investigation on the inhabitants of Belial Gore, explaining that everyone has their secrets as debt victims of Drakken Joe. Weisz reassures the old man that Amira will smooth things out for them.

Sibir reveals to Weisz the pendant in his hand which an upset Weisz thought he stole it all along. But Sibir reveals that the older counterpart of Weisz let him hold the pendant and explained it was one of the young gang members who actually stole the pendant. He tried to return the item to the other Weisz but the latter saw it as proof he could not trust his former friend and they drifted apart.
