" I Love Rock 'n' Roll " is a song by American recording artist Britney Spears . It severed as the fourth single from her third full length studio album, Britney . It was released on May 27, 2002, by Jive Records . The song was used in her 2002 movie Crossroads . Spears' character, Lucy, performs it in a karaoke bar. It is a cover of the Arrows' song of the same name.

Background

Spears said of the song, "They asked me to sing karaoke in the movie Crossroads and I've actually sung I Love Rock 'n' Roll in a lot of clubs that I've been to." Spears has publicly admitted the original song to be one of her favorites. Spears listened to the Arrows' original Mickie Most-produced version just before she recorded the song, according to her Jive A&R man Steve Lunt. The scratches performed on this song were performed by Corey Chase, at Hit Factory Miami. When promoting the single's release she infamously attributed the hit version of the song to Pat Benatar instead of Jett, although she claims this was a remark made in sarcasm rather than a mistake. The song is a pop rock and dance-pop song.
