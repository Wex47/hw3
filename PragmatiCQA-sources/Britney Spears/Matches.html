" Matches " is a song by American recording artist Britney Spears . It severed as the fifth single from her ninth full length studio album, Glory . It was released on December 18, 2020, by RCA Records . The song features additional vocals from American boy band Backstreet Boys.

Background

"Matches" was produced by Michael Wise and Ian Kirkpatrick and co-written by Asia Whiteacre and Justin Tranter. Even though that both Spears and Backstreet Boys had a prime in late 1990s and early 2000s, "Matches" marks their first collaboration, however both artists previously worked together in 2001, by contributing their vocals to cover of "What's Going On" as a part of Artists Against AIDS Worldwide. The song was recorded early in 2017 solely by Backstreet Boys for their ninth studio album DNA (2019), but didn't make the cut. However, Spears heard it and loved it, so she recorded it, but keeping Backstreet Boys on a song. One of boy band members, AJ McLean, was positive about song's release and feeling it gives off, and he said he would like to record a music video in "quarantine style".

Composition

"Matches" is a fast dance, glitch pop and electropop song containing glitchy and electronic sound effects and heavily treated vocals. Song's lyrics describe dangerous love and they have been described as "sultry" by USA Today' s Sara Moniuszko. Lyrics of "Matches" contain many reversals; in pre-chorus Backstreet Boys are singing "Our fire is killing me / The good kind of killing me", when Spears starts her verse with line "I like the way you dress" later adding "And the way you undress".

Release

"Matches" was first released on December 4, 2020, by being included in physical deluxe re-issue of Spears' ninth studio album Glory . A week later, on December 11, a re-issue was released to digital download and streaming media. In the track listing, the track is placed after two previous singles, "Mood Ring" and "Swimming in the Stars". With that release, both Spears and Backstreet Boys tweeted about it, with Spears calling the boys her friends and asking fans about their opinion of the song, while the Backstreet Boys focused on being "asked about the possibility of a Collab like this for the past 20 years". The song impacted Italian contemporary hit radio stations on December 18, 2020, making it Glory' s fifth single.

Critical Reception

Writing for Rolling Stone Jon Blisten called "Matches" a "delightful hit of contemporary club pop" with strings that are "reminiscent of the Max Martin-style songs that made Spears and BSB superstars". Tom Breihan from Stereogum called the track "catchy" and" "fast, glitchy pop". He also added that "'It's Backstreet back, bitch' on the intro feels like a missed opportunity". In opposite to Rolling Stone s Blisten, Wren Graves of Consequence of Sound stated that "Matches" "isn't a callback to the glory days of denim dresses and Max Martin productions; instead, it's a trip through the last decade of horny EDM". Idolator s Mike Wass called collaboration "sexy" and said that it "doesn’t quite reach those heights [as "Swimming in the Stars"]", but still being a "worthy addition to both discographies".

Commercial Performance

"Matches" did not spawn huge commercial success, however, it charted on the US and Canada Digital Song Sales charts, placing at number 7 and 11, respectively. In Europe, the song peaked at number 22 on Germany Digital Song Sales chart and Hungary Singles chart and 48 on UK Download Chart. The song also reached number 40 on New Zealand Hot Single Sales chart.
