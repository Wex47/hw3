Rebellion is an unreleased song by Britney Spears . It was intended for her fifth studio album Blackout . It's her most famous unreleased song to date.

Background

'Rebellion' was first teased way back in 2006 when Spears posted a short, fifty second snippet of the track on the main page of her website, accompanied by a very 2000's animation of her transforming into a Bengal Tiger and a cacophony of roars.

The song speaks about her rebellion against her management, This marks the second time she rebelled against the label Jive, after the release of the demo version of " Mona Lisa " and announcing the mysterious scrapped album Original Doll on KIIS FM. The original Rebellion snippet is less than a minute long, but it was later confirmed that there is a full version of the song. The full version has not yet leaked and it's unknown of it's whereabouts. Jeff Dandurand claims that he has attempted to purchase and release the track officially, even going so far as to state that he uploaded the entire song to YouTube at one point without fans noticing before being forced to take it down.

Using Wayback Machine, a website that allows you to view the contents of a website on a specific date, it's possible to see that a full set of lyrics was actually uploaded to AZLyrics on the 24th of June, 2006, alongside an audio track that's no longer available.

In 2019, Sam Lufti uploaded a photo on Twitter of CD's and one gold disc has "Rebell" written on it, which indicates that Lufti is the only one that has the complete song.

In 2020, a fanmade extended version appeared on Spotify upon the rise of the #FreeBritney movement, however it was taken down by RCA.

Trivia

  • In 2006, Britney was spotted driving in LA playing the song in her Mercedes on full blast.
  • When Britney posted the teaser of the song on her website, JIVE forced her to take it down.
  • Britney wanted the song on the album that eventually became Blackout, JIVE threatened to have the album on hold and wanted her to forget about the song, which she did in order for the fans to listen to the body of work she was proud of.
  • The song is registered twice on ASCAP as Rebellion and Rebellion Part 2.
