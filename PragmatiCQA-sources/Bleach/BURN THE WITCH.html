BURN THE WITCH is a manga series authored by Tite Kubo , initially consisting solely of a one-shot released in the July 16th, 2018 edition of Shonen Jump before later being followed by a 4-chapter serialization set a month after the events of the one-shot. A "season 2" was announced to be coming soon.

BURN THE WITCH follows the adventures of Noel Niihashi and Ninny Spangcole , two Witches working for Wing Bind in Reverse London , the western branch of Soul Society that is populated by Dragons and kept secret from the Human World 's London .

The first 4 chapters were adapted into an animated film released on October 2, 2020 in Japanese theaters and on-demand.

Introduction

BURN THE WITCH is the first work Tite Kubo has released since the last chapter of Bleach was published in Shonen Jump on August 22nd, 2016, and takes place in the same world as his previous work, set 12 years after the Quincy Blood War and 2 years after the events of chapter 686 . The one-shot details a day in the lives of two Witches working for the Western Branch of Soul Society , located in Reverse London . The limited serialization takes place a month later as Noel and Ninny carry out their duties dealing with various Dragons and the issues surrounding them while Balgo Parks is targeted for elimination due to his new Dragon-attracting disposition.

Character Types

  • Witches and Wizards : Witches and Wizards are agents of Wing Bind. Having passed a special exam to join the organization, they protect, tend to, and in some cases exterminate Dragons . Though generally assigned to one division of the agency, a Witch or Wizard can be called upon to fulfill other duties in emergencies, such as when a Dragon is rampaging and no one else is present to stop it. They are rewarded for carrying out their duties with both money and achievement points, the latter of which allows for promotion within the ranks in sufficient quantities. They are led by the Top of Horns .
  • Dragons : Having existed in London for centuries and dangerous to ordinary citizens, Dragons were once responsible for 72% of the deaths in London in medieval times, which led to the city's Coat of Arms bearing their image. However, they are largely regarded as a myth by the general populace, who cannot see them, and in modern times are protected and controlled by Wing Bind. Wing Bind, and by extension Reverse London, domesticates many of them for the production of resources, but others, like Dark Dragons and Disguisers , are malevolent and violent threats to Humans , leading Wing Bind to designate them solely for eradication.

Setting

BURN THE WITCH is set in London , a fictionalized version of the real-world city in the United Kingdom that is divided into two versions of itself, nearly identical in architecture to each other but with drastically different ways of life and knowledge of the other.

  • Front London : Located in the Human World , Front London is populated by ordinary Humans who cannot see Dragons and largely believe the "other side" of the city to be a myth, leading them to live normal lives with modern technology. Though unable to interact with or protect themselves from Dragons, the inhabitants of Front London can still have a dramatic effect on them: for example, in the 1800s, Front London drastically cleaned up its streets, indirectly reducing the population of Disguiser Dragons by removing many of the corpses they could have inhabited. Noel Niihashi attends school and Ninny Spangcole is a famous pop singer here.
  • Reverse London : Spanning across the "hidden side" of London, Reverse London is the western branch of Soul Society and serves to contain, protect, and domesticate Dragons through Wing Bind , allowing them to use Dragons and the materials they produce for a variety of different purposes and products. The residents can see Dragons and are fully aware of Front London, leading them to work to protect the residents of the latter when necessary. Noel and Ninny work as Witches here.

Though Front and Reverse London are completely separate from one another, they can be traveled between using methods like the Coin Gate . Additionally, the two are inextricably linked: should a building be destroyed in Reverse London, its counterpart in Front London will be destroyed at the same time in the same manner.
