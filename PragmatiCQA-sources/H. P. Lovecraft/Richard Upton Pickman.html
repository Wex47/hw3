Richard Upton Pickman is a fictional character created by H. P. Lovecraft for his short story " Pickman's Model ". He was a renowned Boston painter and photographer notorious for his graphic works.

Fictional Biography

Pickman was well-known for the execution of his artwork, but the images he created were so graphic that the Boston Art Club revoked his membership, an act which caused him to be ostracised by his peers.

Later events brought to light the fact that the subject of his work - a species of grotesque beasts living amongst and preying upon humans - was in actuality based on photographs taken by Pickman himself, and not merely the result of a fertile imagination. Shortly afterwards he mysteriously vanished, and was implied to have been one of the creatures from his paintings all along.

In the modern day, Pickman runs with his former models, and appears to be treated as something of a king by them ( EXP : " They Thrive in Darkness , Ron Shiflet ).

Known Paintings

  • "Ghoul Feeding"
  • "The Lesson": Ghouls teaching an apparently-human child how to feed like themselves.
  • A cross section of Beacon Hill with ghouls living in galleries.
  • "Subway Accident": Ghouls attacking a crowd in the Boylston Street subway by coming though a crack.
  • "Holmes, Lowell, and Longfellow Lie Buried in Mount Auburn": Ghouls in a vault reading and laughing at a Boston guide book.

Trivia

  • Pickmans' four-times-great-grandmother was hanged during the Salem witch trials of 1692, with Puritan minister Cotton Mather in attendance.
  • The title of the painting "Holmes, Lowell, and Longfellow Lie Buried in Mount Auburn" likely refers to physician Oliver Wendell Holmes Sr. and poets Amy Lowell and Henry Wadsworth Longfellow, all interred at Mount Auburn's cemetery.
  • "Pickman" and "Upton" are, in actuality, old Salem names. ( EXP : Lovecraft Remembered )
