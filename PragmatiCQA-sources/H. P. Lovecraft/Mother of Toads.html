Synopsis

A young apothecary's apprentice, sent to the abode of a witch in a swamp, is ensorceled, seduced and ultimately killed by a giant toad that can take the form of a human woman, or a human witch with an atavistic spirit of a gigantic toad. The toad-witch-woman seduces the young man then unleashes a horde of toads upon him which force him down into the muck of the swamp and carpet him as he drowns.

Behind the Mythos

Unlike Lovecraft who emphasized heredity, miscegenation and his horror of racemixing, Smith in this short story emphasizes the seductive power of the glamour and illusion magic at the witch's command, opposed to the horrifying reality of her physical form. The sexual aspect of the story is much more mature and balanced than the Lovecraft coldness or the Howard adolescent adventurousness of his compatriots and it stands quite apart and unique among the as a whole.
