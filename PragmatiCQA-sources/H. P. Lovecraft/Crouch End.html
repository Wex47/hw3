Synopsis

Young couple Lonnie and Doris Freeman are on their way to a dinner party when they find themselves lost in the London suburb of Crouch End, a place where the barriers between dimensions has been rubbed thin. As they make their way through an increasingly-bizarre world, Lonnie is taken by a nightmarish creature, while Doris barely manages to escape with her life.

Characters

  • Leonard "Lonnie" Freeman , a young lawyer.
  • Doris Freeman , Lonnie's wife.
  • PC Robert Farnham , an officer at the Crouch End police station.
  • PC Ted Vetter , a senior officer assigned to the station.

Publication History

First published by Arkham House in New Tales of the Cthulhu Mythos in 1980, "Crouch End" was later included in a slightly edited version in King's own collection Nightmares and Dreamscapes in 1993.

Mythos Connections

  • Several Mythos entities are made reference to, including Cthulhu Kryon ( Cthulhu ), Yogsoggoth ( Yog-Sothoth ) and Nrtesn Nyarlahotep ( Nyarlathotep ). Lonnie is heavily implied to have been taken by The Goat with a Thousand Young ( Shub-Niggurath ).
  • Cthulhu's resting place of R'yeleh ( R'lyeh ) is mentioned.
  • A sign on a decaying warehouse reads Alhazred , a clear reference to Abdul Alhazred , the crazed author of the Necronomicon .

Adaptations

  • An audiobook version of Nightmares & Dreamscapes was released with a reading of "Crouch End" by actor Tim Curry.
  • The story was adapted into an episode of Nightmares and Dreamscapes: From the Stories of Stephen King by television network TNT

Trivia

  • H. P. Lovecraft himself is mentioned by PC Vetter when he is explaining the nature of dimensions to Farnham.
