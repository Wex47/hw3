The Horror in the Museum and Other Revisions is a collection of short works which were either revised by H. P. Lovecraft , or ghostwritten by him for other authors.

Publication History

First published by Arkham House in 1970, the collection ran to 383 pages and was edited by Lovecraft's friend and contemporary August Derleth . A revised edition was released in 1989 by S. T. Joshi , featuring a number of additions, with this issue running to 450 pages. A trade paperback was then released in 2007, this time by Stephen Jones , and featured a new introduction by Jones along with a potted biography of Lovecraft.
