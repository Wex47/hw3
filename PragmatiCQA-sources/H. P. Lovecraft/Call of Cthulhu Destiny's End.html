Call of Cthulhu: Destiny's End was one of two planned follow-ups to Call of Cthulhu: Dark Corners of the Earth , the other being Call of Cthulhu: Beyond the Mountains of Madness .

Overview

Announced in November 2002, Destiny's End was developed by Headfirst in parallel with their Dark Corners of the Earth release. It was ultimately cancelled in 2006 after Headfirst were unsuccessful in securing a publisher; this cancellation was a major contributing factor in the bankruptcy of the company.

Somewhat unusually for the survival-horror genre, it was designed to be played as a two-person cooperative game, although it could still be played by a single player.

Premise

The story involved two characters, Emily and Jacob, searching for clues to their past in the derelict town of Innsmouth, solving problems and fighting Mythos monsters along the way.
