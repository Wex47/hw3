The commonplace book is notebook of 221 entries for characters and stories written by H. P. Lovecraft between late 1919 ( HPL : Selected Letters 1.61) until his death, typed up by R. H. Barlow and published after Lovecraft's death along with some essays.

The first 201 entries were typed by Barlow from the original notebook in May 1934 with Lovecraft annotating previous and writing new ideas in the typed copy.

For use in a revised Supernatural Horror in Literature , Lovecraft also wrote "Weird Story Plots ; Suggestions for Writing Story" in 1933. It was also typed by Barlow for Lovecraft and partially reproduced and conflated in the posthumous publication of the Commonplace Book.

27 Life and Death

Death—its desolation and horror—bleak spaces—sea-bottom—dead cities. But Life—the greater horror! Vast unheard-of reptiles and leviathans—hideous beasts of prehistoric jungle—rank slimy vegetation—evil instincts of primal man—Life is more horrible than death.

28 The Cats of Ulthar

The cat is the soul of antique Ægyptus and bearer of tales from forgotten cities of Meroë and Ophir. He is the kin of the jungle's lords, and heir to the secrets of hoary and sinister Africa. The Sphinx is his cousin, and he speaks her language; but he is more ancient than the Sphinx, and remembers that which she hath forgotten.
