The Thing in the Moonlight is a short story written by J. Chapman Miske and published in 1941.

Background

Based on a letter from H.P. Lovecraft to Donald Wandrei in 1927 it was first published in the January 1941 issue (Volume 4, Number 1) of Bizarre Magazine.

Plot Summary

The narrator recounts the story of Morgan, an illiterate man alone in his home, who suddenly began to write a letter under the name Howard Phillips of 66 College Street, Providence, Rhode Island, detailing a dream from November 24 1927.

In the text the dreamer finds them self in a reedy marsh and feels compelled to climb a cliff to the north. Reaching a moonlit plateau they they look around and discover a rusting tramway. Some way down the track they find a yellow 1900-10 tram car numbered 1852. He climbs aboard and though the car stands empty the conductor and motorman approach from the left before the latter begins to howl and the former charges on all fours. Seeing that the motorman's face is in fact a white cone with a single white tentacle the dreamer flees. Though he knows he's sleeping he is trapped in the dreamland, involuntarily returning to the ancient tram car and the howling creature each night. He signs off by asking "God! When will I awaken?".
