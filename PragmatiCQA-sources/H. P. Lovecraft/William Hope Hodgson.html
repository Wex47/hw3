William Hope Hodgson (15 November 1877 – 19 April 1918) was a prolific British author who wrote in a number of genres. His works are known to have inspired the likes of H. P. Lovecraft , Clark Ashton Smith and Henry S. Whitehead , and he was responsible for the creation of supernatural detective Thomas Carnacki, a character who has been expanded on for many years by many authors.

Hodgson was killed in action during the First World War at the fourth battle of Ypres, April 1918.
