Kay Challis 's alters* are members of Kay's DID system. They inhabit the Underground . Each seems to have a specific purpose, most of them designed to protect Kay, though not all of these are known.

*Please note: while the title of the page calls Jane's system "personalities", this is an outdated and incorrect term. "Alters", "System Mates" and "Headmates" are the most common terms used by those who have Dissociative Identity Disorder.

History

Origin

As a little girl until her early adolescence, Kay Challis was sexually/physically/emotionally abused by her own father. The experience and continued traumas she suffered caused her to develop Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID) and manifest 64 different alters, all of whom reside within The Underground . Each of the alters have their own personal Station within the Underground that is their designated living area.

Characteristics

Each of the alters have their own "role" or "job" within the hierarchy of the Underground, a reason for existing that ultimately assists in maintaining the "equilibrium" within the Underground, as stated by the Secretary and Lucy Fugue , and further elaborated upon as the story unfolds. Crazy Jane is the designated primary alter, the one who is "surfaced" the majority of the time and in control of Kay's body when the other alters wait for their turn. Jane, as primary is charged with caring for the system and making vital decision in the "surface" to protect them and most importantly, Kay. It is revealed that Jane wasn't the first "primary", to exist. A previous alter named Miranda was once the primary until she abdicated, following a failure to protect them.

Whole most of the roles of the alters are to protect Kay and the system, there are some who do not seem to share this role. The role of Driver 8 seems to be to transport and navigate the Underground bringing each of the alters to the "surface" when needed/wanted. The Weird Sisters ' role seems to be to "advise" the alters, having done so for Miranda in the past and Jane in the present, Penny Farthing has stated her role is to "run away from danger", while Hammerhead and Driller Bill seem to also act as law enforcers within the Underground. Hammerhead in particular seems to take up the role of reigning in rebellious alters, confining and imprisoning them until they had "cooled off" before presumably returning them to their individual Station, as she did with Karen . Black Annis 's "role" seems to be to stand guard on the entryway to the Well and prevent any man from passing. The roles of the alters was further made clear in an argument between Hammerhead and Jane, where Hammerhead angrily told Jane their job wasn't to protect ordinary people but to instead protect the girl . To this end, alters like Flaming Katy and Doctor Harrison are shown to explicitly only surface when Kay is endangered to a specific kind of threat, as seen where Katy appears when Jane is being harassed. Dr. Harrison was implied to only surface only when they faced torture or psychological abuse. Dr. Harrison also acts as a mediator when interventions between system alters is necessary.

In order to better fulfill their mission of keeping Kay safe, some of the alters are shown to also posses the job to calm down Kay when in stressful situations: Karen is known to only surface whenever Jane becomes deeply depressed and appears to cheer her/them up by "finding her one true love". The Hangman's Daughter has stated that her role was to simply paint in peace and quite in order to clam themselves down. The Balladeer was also implied to have her purpose be to mourn whenever an alter is killed.

Although unconfirmed, due to their status as psychological constructs of the original Kay Challis, it has been stated and theorized by Jane that they themselves cannot be killed, with her later revealing that sometimes a few of the alters within the Underground simply go dormant within her mind only to later resurface later completely fine years later. This was later proven to be somewhat true upon Miranda reemerging from the Well despite the alters' belief that she destroyed herself. It was later revealed however that this was not Miranda but instead Daddy , pretending to be her.

As revealed in " Dad Patrol ", alters do indeed truly "die" when their bodies are thrown in the Well, as seen where Baby Doll , Scarlet Harlot , Lucy Fugue and Miranda 's bodies were seen floating lifelessly in the Well.

The overall leadership or even hierarchy of the alters is unknown, but it is shown that the alters themselves consider themselves as nothing more than protectors of Kay Challis . As such, the system believes that it is their first priority that they should pursue any situation that is either in Kay's best interest, or whether their actions fall into what she wants. With Hammerhead , Crazy Jane and the majority of the alters agree that what they need personally is second to Kay's own needs.
