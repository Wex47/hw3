Biography

Early Life

In London, England during the year 1927, amongst a travelling circus, Dorothy is being kept in a cage and refer to as the "Ape Faced Girl" who was captured in the Yukon and is now forced to perform as part of a freak show by conjuring creatures. However, things go a bit awry when she gets distracted by someone in the audience and lashes out. An ominous voice tells her that he can help if she makes a wish. A candle then appears in her hand, so she proceeds to make a wish, blows it out and chaos ensues. When it's over, everyone is dead except for her, her creature, and Niles Caulder.


At this London Carnival in 1927, Niles finds himself in the audience to confirm that the “Monkey-faced girl” attraction was his daughter. What he also bares witness to in the process is how The Candlemaker , one of Dorothy's most powerful imaginary friends killed everyone else at the carnival that night.

A couple of years later, Niles sends Dorothy to hide inside Danny, who was “a smart alley” at the time, and set out on his journey to gain immortality. All so that he would always be around to protect his daughter from others and from herself. His investigation eventually led him to Paraguay, where he stole a talisman in the form of a rabbit’s leg that provided him with immortality. Unfortunately, this was not a permanent solution, so he began to test out his unethical experiments of immortality on what is now the team.

In London during 1978, Niles visits her who is still residing with Danny , but in a room below the actual street, the young girl doesn't want him to leave. Ignoring how lonely she seems, Niles instead cautions her not to ever make a wish. When Niles inevitably leaves, Danny tries to remind him that the girl needs her father and that she can't stay forever.

A New Family

Despite multiple attempts to return them to their normal size, this latest venture into fixing their size also fails.

Later, Dorothy and Jane bond a bit over both of them having "friends" inside their heads. Niles interrupts as he wants the serum she's been using so he can use it for Dorothy. Jane refuses and says she's used it all. Eventually, Dorothy tries to spend time with Cliff as well who has been "feeding" the rats they end up taking one of the cars around the little race track. Powers and abilities

Powers

  • Metahuman physiology : Through her mother, Dorothy is a meta-human, inheriting various unique powers.
    • Longevity : Dorothy hasn't aged since the 1910's, retaining the physical appearance of a child despite being over a century old. Given her mother's own immense life-span, its highly likely that Dorothy herself also possess similar longevity.
    • Imagination Manifestation : Dorothy has the ability to warp reality and bring to life the monsters of her thoughts.
      • Imaginary Friends : Dorothy's reality warping powers allow her to conjure her Imaginary Friends into the real-world. Although they mainly appear in order to act as Dorothy's playmates, they can also appear in order to defend her when she feels strong emotions and even influence her mind.
        • Darling Come Home : A motherly-like Imaginary Friend that appears to take the form of a adult woman with golden 70's hairstyle and a mirror for a face with light bulbs for eyes.
        • Herschel : A gigantic spider Imaginary Friend that possesses the ability to phase through walls.
        • Candlemaker : A gigantic wooden-flame demon that appears whenever Dorothy grants or makes a wish. As seen during her fight against Baby Doll, Dorothy primarily uniquely summons Candlemaker by conjuring a candle and blowing its fire out to "make a wish". The Candlemaker seems to manipulatively whisper to Dorothy in order to influence her into summoning him.

Former powers

  • Manny : Given to her by her mother , Dorothy can also manifest the same bear/wolf monster hybrid with deer antlers to defend herself. This imaginary friend was soon killed by Flaming Katy when Dorothy summoned him in a panic by Baby Doll 's antics.

Abilities

  • Singing : Dorothy is a talented singer with a beautiful voice, she was able to perform a song to resuscitate Danny .

Weaknesses

  • Temper : When Dorothy is mad she loses control of her powers to the point they can influence her to do things like make a wish out of anger.
  • Panic Attacks: As seen during the incident with the mother rat and her runt, when Dorothy sees or experiences something particularly upsetting that makes her feel scared, panicked, confused and frightened, she would immediately begun losing control of her powers and unconsciously summon her imaginary friends to her side in order to "defend" herself and if she does not regain her composure, they soon simply begin to rampage uncontrollably until Dorothy makes them go away.
