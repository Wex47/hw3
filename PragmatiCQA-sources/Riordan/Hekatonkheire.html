The Hekatonkheires (also known as the Hundred-Handed Ones ) are a race of humanoid beings, each with a hundred arms and fifty heads. They were one of three sets of children born to Gaea and Ouranos , the others being the Titans and the Elder Cyclopes . They were the primeval gods of violent storms, while their brothers, the Elder Cyclopes, held power over thunder and lightning.

By the Second Olympian War , Briares is the only Hekatonkheire left after his two brothers faded over time. Tyson , a cyclops, saved Briares from his imprisonment in Alcatraz .

History

Before the First Olympian War , the Hekatonkheires were imprisoned in Tartarus by Ouranos alongside their brothers: the Elder Cyclopes . They were guarded and tortured by Kampê until Zeus killed her and Poseidon freed the brothers by breaking their chains with his Trident . The Hekatonkheires helped the Olympians win the First Olympian War. Two of them, Cottus and Gyges, later served as guards to Tartarus' entrance. Briaries now lives in the underwater forges where Tyson works.
