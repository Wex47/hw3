The Movie

Synopsis

The dreaded thief Diabolik (Law) wreaks havoc in Europe for his own financial gain and amusement. He shares an extravagant underground lair (and a giant bed of money) with his curvaceous, superficial girlfriend (Mell), who helps Diabolik to kill innocent people and steal billions from the government.

Trivia

  • The movie is based on Diabolik , one of the longest running—and most successful—Italian comic books. In the paper version, Diabolik is much more sinister than his cinematic counterpart—he's a criminal fighting evil with evil, often resorting to murder to "punish" the evildoers he meets. The movie was made assuming some knowledge of the comic book, thus explaining the negative reaction it gets outside Italy.
  • Footage from this film was used in a Beastie Boys music video for their song "Body Movin'". The video is a parody of the movie and roughly follows the film's sequence of events, with a more deliberate comedic twist.

The Episode

Host Segments

  • Prologue: Mike & the Bots discover the SOL employee handbook, which seems not entirely progressive.
  • Segment One: Mike toys around with the handbook; Crow asks about dental coverage. "Shut up, you have no right to ask." Pearl , meanwhile, has a new joystick to control the SOL, but it breaks, accidentally causing the SOL to begin landing procedures. Mike & the Bots are thrilled (though Crow T. Robot is nauseous); Pearl is less thrilled.
  • Segment Two: Crow and Mike are packed; while Servo begins disposing of the many extra hims on the ship.
  • Segment Three: In Castle Forrester , everybody is lining up new gigs: Pearl has accepted an offer to be dictator for life of Qatar , Bobo has a great new position at the zoo and Brain Guy has less desirable (to him) prospects.
  • Segment Four: Crow has become fearful of returning to Earth, so Mike sings a little reassuring song, which helps, but also succeeds in scaring Servo.
  • Segment Five: The SOL nearly disintegrates during re-entry, but Mike & the Bots survive the crash. Mike, Crow, Servo and Cambot ( Gypsy has launched ConGypsCo) are last seen happily sharing a small apartment, and settling in to watch a bad movie-- The Crawling Eye —on TV. As they begin to riff on it, it occurs to Crow that the movie seems familiar.
  • Stinger: "Is that stud coming?"

Trivia

  • The final show of the Sci-Fi Channel era. At the time, it was unclear if the series would ever continue, and it seemed unlikely to do so.
  • While this is the series finale of MST3K (on cable TV), the last original episode that aired was Merlin's Shop of Mystical Wonders , on Sept. 18, 1999 (the airing was delayed by problems with rights clearances).  The final episode to air in reruns was The Screaming Skull , on Jan. 21, 2004.
  • In a sense, this is the second series finale, as the final episode of the Comedy Central era ( Laserblast ) was written to serve as the final episode if the show was not picked up by another network.
  • Crow's comment about The Crawling Eye looking familiar is a reference to the first broadcast episode the show did for The Comedy Channel . However, neither Michael J. Nelson , Kevin Murphy , nor Bill Corbett had yet taken over their respective on-screen roles when the episode had originally aired (though Nelson and Murphy were part of the writing staff).
  • Tom Servo gives the last riff in the theater ("This has been the official biopic of Larry Fine").
  • Mike gives the last riff on the show ("Oh, Forrest Tucker, he's the guy that makes sure the trees' shirt tails are in"), while the gang is watching TV in his apartment.
  • The TV station they are watching is WTMJ in Milwaukee, Wisconsin.
  • Crow's wire mother from Invasion USA makes her final appearance, despite being dumped into space with the rest of the trash on the Satellite in Code Name: Diamond Head . Crow is pleased to see her.
  • In the early days of YouTube someone (the entire theater session without host segments or commercials). Immediately following the scene where Diabolik talks on the phone with Valmont, there follows an assemblage of memorable moments from the movies riffed during the Sci Fi Channel seasons. This appears to have been a genuine surprise to Mike, Kevin, and Bill, who convulse with laughter at the clips.
