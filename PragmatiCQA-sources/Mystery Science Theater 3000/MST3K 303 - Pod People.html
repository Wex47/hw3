The movie

An alien slaughters the poachers and campers it encounters in the woods, while a boy befriends its offspring.

The episode

Host segments

Prologue: Crow does a segment from his one-man show, "Robot on the Run", and Tom does the intro to his own show.

Invention Exchange: Joel demonstrates a new guitar chord that blows up in his face, while the Mads wail along with their new royalty-free Public Domain Karaoke Machine.

Segment Two: Joel and the Bots sing their own version of the song from the movie, " Idiot Control Now ". While the Mads like it, Joel declares that "It stinks!"

Segment Three: Joel teaches Crow about creating new age music, leading to a new segment, "Music From Some Guys in Space"

Segment Four: Trumpy's magic causes mayhem on the SOL, confusing the Mads and thrilling Joel.

Segment Five: As Joel and the bots pack up, they conclude the show with the sincere song " Clowns in the Sky ", but the Mads don't like it.

Stinger: "It stinks!"

MST3K crew

Production

  • That's makeup lady (and occasional writer) Faye Berkholder as the recording studio assistant in Deep 13. For years, many fans mistakenly believed that it was Bridget Nelson (whose first on-screen appearance wouldn't occur until Gamera vs Zigra ).
  • Joel's old grey jumpsuit from the KTMA season makes an appearance on a clothing rack during the host segment.
  • According to Kevin Murphy in The Amazing Colossal Episode Guide , the producers of NPR's Music From the Hearts of Space series enjoyed the "Music From Some Guys in Space" host segment and even sent them a few CDs of new age music.
  • Rare instance where Servo directly apologizes to the audience for the film's convoluted plot.
  • Ranked 5th in the Top 100 Episodes as chosen by backers of the Bring Back MYSTERY SCIENCE THEATER 3000 campaign.
  • This episode aired eleventh during Turkey Day '91 , fifth during Turkey Day '14 , and second during Turkey Day '16 .
  • One of Crow's riffs mentions the film Mac & Me , which would later be the subject of an episode . Jack Perkins also mentions it during the last of this episode's The Mystery Science Theater Hour bumpers

Goofs

  • When Joel says, "Trumpy, you can do STUPID things!" in the theater, he’s referencing a line by Tommy, who says, "You can do magic things!" However, Joel does that riff about ten minutes before Tommy says the original line.
