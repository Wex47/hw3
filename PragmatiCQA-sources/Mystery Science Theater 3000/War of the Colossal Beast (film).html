War of the Colossal Beast is a 1958 science fiction film directed by Bert I. Gordon . It was co-written by Bert I. Gordon and George Worthing Yates . It is a sequel to Gordon's 1957 film The Amazing Colossal Man .

Plot

A mysterious series of food-truck robberies prompts government officials to doubt that the 60-foot tall Colossal Man is dead. Manning is discovered in a desolate mountain range in Mexico, insane and disfigured. The military drugs him and transports him back to America. Bureaucratic callousness exacerbates the situation, while Glenn's sister attempts to calm and protect him. Glenn escapes and wreaks havoc on a city before electrocuting himself to death.

Notes

  • This was the only film that Bert I. Gordon directed that was a sequel to one of his previous films.
  • This film is the origin of the line "Glen was fifty feet tall!".
  • The climactic scene of Glenn's electrocution is in color.
  • In The Amazing Colossal Man , it is stressed that Glenn’s fiancée is the only ‘family’ that he has. In this sequel it is revealed that Glenn has a sister. The fiancée and the sister are not played by the same actress, and the sister is not meant to be the same character as the fiancée, though they look alike.
  • In this film, Glenn Manning is played by Duncan “Dean” Parkin. Glenn’s grunts and groans were performed by Paul Frees .
  • Actors Russ Bender and Jack Kosslyn had also appeared in The Amazing Colossal Man , but as different characters.
