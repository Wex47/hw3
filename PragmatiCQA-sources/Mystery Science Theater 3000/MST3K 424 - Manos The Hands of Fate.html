The Short

Hired! Part 2 (1940)

Synopsis

Continuing from the first part , managing salesman is inspired by his handkerchief-wearing father on how to motivate and instruct his young, inexperienced car salesman on how to close sales.

The Movie

Synopsis

A family becomes lost while driving and finds themselves at a mysterious secluded lodge. There, they encounter an evil cult leader and his minions.

The Episode

Host Segments

Prologue: Joel has installed a chip in the Bots that makes everything he does seem brilliant, but it quickly gets on his nerves.

Segment One (Invention Exchange): The Bots are disgruntled over the chip. In Deep 13 , the Mads invent the Chocolate Bunny Guillotine (which Frank despises), while on the SOL Joel introduces the Cartuner to combine two boring comic strips into one funny strip, Like and .

Segment Two: Joel and the Bots recreate the opening driving scene from the movie when they are pulled over by Redneck Officer Gypsy , but Cambot 's failure to stop the blue screen footage pushes them over the edge. Frank apologizes for the movie.

Segment Three: Joel and the Bots discuss exaggerated features that make for a horrifying monster, but can’t agree if Torgo counts as one. They propose scarier looking monsters based on exaggerated or missing body parts.

Segment Four: Joel dresses as the Master and Crow is his hellbeast, but Tom isn’t impressed; he thinks Joel looks like Maude and his face is "too friendly". Dr. F apologizes for the movie.

Segment Five: The Bots reenact the wives' wrestling game from the movie to cheer Joel up (though he is not quite pleased over this). In Deep 13, Forrester complains about their pizza being late; Frank admits that he wanted Togo's Pizza, but they were busy, thus having him order from Torgo's Pizza two hours ago. Torgo finally shows up, but accidentally leaves their pop in the car. Frank disturbingly notes the pizza is still pretty warm, resulting in both him and Forrester spitting it out in disgust. A dismayed Torgo remarks, "They always do that..."

Stinger: Girl in car saying "Why don’t you guys leave us alone?"

Trivia

  • The silhouettes of Joel, Tom, and Crow obscured the few shots where MST3K viewers might have been able to see Torgo’s goat feet.
  • Ranked 1st in the Top 100 Episodes as chosen by backers of the Bring Back MYSTERY SCIENCE THEATER 3000 campaign.
  • Unusual credits : Torgo's theme music plays instead of "Mighty Science Theater."
  • This episode aired third during Turkey Day '93 , second during MST3K Anthology , and the sixth & last during Turkey Day '16 .
  • This is usually referred to by the cast and writing staff as officially the worst movie they ever riffed. Monster A-Go Go is cited as a close second due to its total lack of coherent narrative, but Manos is considered more loathsome in terms of subject matter.
  • During a 2007 interview with Mike Nelson and Kevin Murphy by the Free Beer and Hot Wings Radio Show! , Mike remarked that his wife Bridget was creeped out so much by "Manos" The Hands of Fate that she still could not stand to watch it.

Goofs

  • Servo uses the phrase "this is a bug hunt!" during his imitation of Hudson panicking in the film Aliens . In fact, the phrase "bug hunt" comes in an earlier scene when Hudson is talking with Apone on the Sulaco . The implication is that "bug hunt" refers to a difficult mission (akin to "a needle in a haystack") but not necessarily a dangerous one.

Video Releases

  • Commercially released on VHS by Rhino Entertainment in Feburary 1998, it was later released as part of a three-pack along with Gunslinger and Poopie! in March 1999.
  • Commercially released on DVD by Rhino in November 2001, the DVD includes the entire contents of the Poopie! video as an extra.
  • The Manos DVD was re-released unchanged as part of MST3K: The Essentials , a double-disc set which features this episode and newly released Santa Claus Conquers the Martians . As a limited time special offer, if ordered off a specially created Rhino website, a bonus disc of Shorts Vol 3 was also included with the set.
  • Shout! Factory re-issued Manos as a 2-disc "Special Edition" on September 13, 2011. Disc 1 features the episode, The Mystery Science Theater Hour wraps, and a featurette, Group Therapy , in which Joel , Trace , Frank , and Mary Jo discuss the episode. Disc 2 features the original, unriffed movie; the documentary Hotel Torgo ; the Ballyhoo Motion Pictures featurette Jam Handy to the Rescue , where Joel discusses the shorts used on MST3K ; and Hired!: Parts 1 and 2 , presented back-to-back for the first time.
