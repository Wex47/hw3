The Movie

Synopsis

Giant grasshoppers threaten the midwest. A scientist and a news photographer join forces to stop the giant insects before they destroy Chicago.

The Episode

Host Segments

Prologue: Mike and the Bots attempt to sing some classic standards is repeatedly interrupted by a woman on the hexfield who keeps dialing the wrong number.

Segment One (Invention Exchange): Still getting calls from the wrong number woman on the SOL . Down in Deep 13 the Mads have come up with the Re-comfy bike, even more comfortable than the recumbent bike. Mike & the Bots finally present new playing cards, like Todd and the Eight of Chris Lemmon .

Segment Two: While the Bots advise against it, Mike calls the Mads and catches them acting very "unmanly". Mike says he won't be calling them again for a while.

Segment Three: Crow has written a screenplay: Peter Graves Goes to the University of Minnesota . It gets somewhat repetitive.

Segment Four: Tom's standup routine at the Improv (with its trademark red brick wall) is heavy on jokes comparing grasshoppers and locusts.

Segment Five: Mike unlocks the secret to Bert I. Gordon special effects. Meanwhile in Deep 13 , the Mads attempt to re-assert their masculinity by boxing (with Frank eventually gaining the upper hand).

Stinger: "Alright, men. Into the woods!"

Trivia

  • This episode premiered as the fifteenth during Turkey Day '93 .
  • Unusual credits : Before the copyright, an additional credit was added: "Shot entirely in Minneapolis, home of the University of Minnesota".
  • No title slate has ever been included before the opening credits of this episode.
  • At the time of this episode's original broadcast, Chris Lemmon's most recent film credit was 1991's Firehead , which became a RiffTrax presentation in 2013.
  • The tune Tom hums during the reading of Act 1 of Crow's screenplay is the Minnesota Rouser .
  • Mike encourages Tom to send a tape of his stand-up act to The Chevy Chase Show , but the short-lived talk show had already been canceled by the time this episode first aired.
  • Beginning with this episode, Mike 's green jumpsuit gains a Wisconsin patch on the right arm.

Obscure References

  • "Let's see what Benny and Joon are doing!"

A reference to the 1993 psychological romantic comedy Benny & Joon .

  • "Miss Mansfield?"

Actress Jayne Mansfield was killed in a vehicle collision in 1967. Urban legend said (mistakenly) that she was decapitated, most likely due to the fact that her wig was found some distance from the crash.

  • "Why is Walter Winchell in this scene?"

Walter Winchell was a radio commentator and gossip columnist. His fast-paced speech pattern is best remembered in his work as the announcer on The Untouchables TV series.

  • "Must get Vivarin!"

Vivarin is a brand of over-the-counter .

  • "Jimmy Jam does his thing!"

Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis are highly successful R&B and pop music producers.

  • "File on Dalton Trumbo, sir. "

Screenwriter Dalton Trumbo was one of the , being after refusing to answer questions about his involvement with the Communist Party.

  • (Singing) "This is the army, Audrey Aimes...!"

A riff on the title song to the 1942 musical " This Is the Army " by Irving Berlin.

  • " It's a star city."

Refers to a Minnesota rural economic development initiative.

  • "Suddenly it's turning into Topper !"

Topper is a 1937 comedy film about a pair of ghosts.

  • "This was no boating accident!"

An oft-misquoted line from the 1975 movie Jaws , spoken by Richard Dreyfuss . (Actual quotes: "This was no boat accident." "Well, this is not a boat accident!")

  • "Women on the Verge of an Atomic Breakdown."

A reference to the 1988 Spanish film Women on the Verge of a Nervous Breakdown .

  • "Coincidence? Read the book!"

A quote from commercials for the Time-Life book series Mysteries of the Unknown .

  • "Is this a snipe hunt?"

A snipe hunt is a type of practical joke that often involves sending the person on the receiving end to "find" a nonexistent animal or object.

  • "Exterminate with extreme prejudice!"

A reference to Apocalypse Now , in which Capt. Williard is ordered to "terminate" Col. Kurtz's command "with extreme prejudice" (that is, assassinate him).

  • "America responds to the new Robert Ulrich/Faye Dunaway sitcom!"

It Had to Be You premiered on CBS in September 1993. It only aired 4 episodes.

  • "Animals trained by Rudd Weatherwax."

Rudd (and brother Frank ) Weatherwax trained animals for movies and TV. Most famously they handled the training of dogs for the Lassie movies/TV series and Old Yeller .

  • "'One weekend a month' my ass!"

" One weekend a month, two weeks a year " was the former recruitment slogan for the US National Guard, noting the requirements to be a Guardsman with benefits.

  • "...Just like they handled the Chicago convention."

The 1968 Democratic National Convention , held in Chicago, was marked by an aggressive and violent response to protests by the Chicago Police Department and the Illinois National Guard against demonstrators and others, both outside and inside the convention center.

  • "Bad Lieutenant!"

Bad Lieutenant is a 1992 neo-noir crime drama film starring Harvey Keitel .

  • "And this is the 1910 Fruitgum Company!"

The 1910 Fruitgum Company is a pop band founded in 1966. After disbanding in 1970 and a brief reunion in 1979/80, they reformed permanently in 1999.

  • "Hey general, where ya goin'?" "Decatur! I'm gonna shoot that paper-hangin' sonofabitch!"

A quote from the movie : "...And when we get to Berlin I am personally going to shoot that paper-hanging son of a bitch" (meaning Hitler).

  • "Mister Mitchell!"

The newsreader has an uncanny resemblance to Henry Mitchell , the father in Dennis the Menace .

  • "The Cubs don't win the pennant! The Cubs don't win the pennant!"

A double reference, to call of Bobby Thomson 's home run to win the National League pennant for the New York Giants in 1951 ("The Giants win the pennant! The Giants win the pennant!") and also the historical futility of the Chicago Cubs: they had not won the World Series since 1908 or a NL pennant since 1945 before they won both in 2016.

  • "They've got a picture of Russ Bender!"

Russ Bender played Richard Kingman in The Amazing Colossal Man and Dr. Carmichael in War of the Colossal Beast .

  • "I gotta have those Glengarry leads!"

A reference to the play and film Glengarry Glen Ross , in which the "Glengarry leads" are coveted premium "leads" (i.e. names of interested buyers) that the salesmen compete to get. The reference is suggested by the sight of a man pacing nervously around an office.

  • "This is what it sounds like when grasshoppers cry!"

A reference to the Prince song " When Doves Cry ".
