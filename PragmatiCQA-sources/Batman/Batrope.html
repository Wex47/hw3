The Batrope (also referred to as the Bat-Line ) is a tool used by the Bat Family for climbing and swinging from buildings primarily. The traditional method was for Batman to attach a line to one of his Batarangs , which would serve as a grappling hook after being thrown. In modern times, Batman has more commonly used Grapple Guns and other zip-line gadgets: the Batrope sometimes being part of them.

Functionality

The Batrope (or Bat-Line) is a length of monofilament wire attached to a spool concealed within a compartment on Batman's utility Belt . It is used primarily for scaling the sides of buildings and ensnaring targets. Many of Batman's allies also use Batropes including Batgirl, Nightwing and Robin. Although Batman's lines are famously durable, exotic weaponry can cut through it, including Batarangs.

History

The Batrope first appeared in Detective Comics #28 as Batman's "silken cord". It was used by the Dark Knight to swing away from a crime scene as the GCPD arrived to arrest him. Later in the story, he tied the rope around Frenchy Blake 's leg and threatened to drop him though a window unless he confessed to his role in the jewelry thefts. The Batrope stayed in Batman's utilities, with him often throwing or attaching it to object to climb it.

Beginning in Detective Comics #32 , Batman would also attach the Batrope to his batarangs to act as grappling hooks. The Batarangs also increased the range which it could be thrown, with Batman beginning to make use of this to function regularly. Though Batman originally carried the Batrope from his Utility Belt , he eventually modified it to store the rope within the belt itself.

On a few occasions, Batman would attach the Batrope to other weapons or gadgets to create climbing ropes, such as the Batpoon in World's Finest Comics #9 and Suction-Cap Climbing Ropes in Batman #183 . However, these were often abandoned after several usages, often after proving ineffective. By Batman #414 , Batman had stopped attaching Batarangs to the Batrope and replaced them with a grappling hook. This proved more effective in latching onto objects and allowing him to climb them.

Eventually, Batman created a new gadget called the Grapple Gun which, in addition to firing a rope-attached grappling hook, also allowed him to ascend the reel with a mechanized rotor. This quickly replaced the Batrope as his primarily method of scaling buildings, thought the rope itself was still used occasionally.

Media Depictions

Batman has often made use of the Batrope in other media, ranging from live-action to cartoons. However, its appearances have dwindled since Grapple Gun 's implementation into the comics.

Live-Action

  • The Batrope features often in 1960s Batman series and Batman: The Movie . Like the comics, the rope is stored in Batman 's utility belt and is often attached to Batarangs to act as grappling hooks. The rope can also be tied to other objects to pull from a distance. Batman also uses a grappling hook mortar to fire the rope up high buildings.
  • In the 1989 Batman film , Batman is depicted using a "spring-action reel" to store and fire the Batrope. The gadget also attaches it to various additional equipment, including a grappling hook launcher and speargun. In an early scene, Batman also uses a rope-attached Batarang to subdue a mugger .

Animation

  • Batropes feature in The Adventures of Batman and The New Adventures of Batman . Like the comics, they are often used in conjunction with Batarangs.
  • The Batrope occasionally appears in Batman: The Animated Series . Due to its implementation of the grapple gun, it often features as a rope-attached Batarang used to subdue foes. However, some episodes show Batman and Robin using it to swing from areas.

Video Games

  • In the arcade tie-in to 1989's Batman , the spring-action reel is identified as the Batrope. The gadget can be used as both a traversal option and weapon.
  • The Batrope features in Batman: Dark Tomorrow as one of Batman's gadgets. Unlike most depictions, Batman can only use it to swing between buidings, with ascension only given to the Grapple Gun. In a notoriously difficult early section, the player must use the Batrope to navigate the rooftops of Gotham.
