Shells ( Cartridges in Serious Sam Advance and Shotgun Ammo in Serious Sam: Double D ) are a common ammo type that serves as ammo for shotguns.

  • 2 Appearances
  • 4 List of appearances

Overview

Shells are a type of ammo pickup that supplies shotguns that use 12-gauge rounds, such as the 12 Gauge Pump-Action Shotgun and Double Barrel Coach Gun .

The amount of ammo in each box of shells depends on the game and (in some cases) the difficulty level. In most games, each box supplies 10 shells on Normal and Hard difficulties, and 20 on Tourist, Easy and Serious difficulties, while in Serious Sam 2 it gives the player 20 shells per box regardless of the difficulty.

In Serious Sam 3: BFE and Serious Sam 3 VR: BFE , there is a small shell pickup shaped like the pump-action shotgun's magazine that gives 5 shells or Normal, Hard and Serious difficulties, 10 on Tourist and 7 on Easy difficulties.

In Serious Sam's Bogus Detour , there is a small pickup that gives 5 shells.

In Serious Sam 4 , there are four shell pickups: one that supplies a small amount of shells that are dropped from enemies with the Looter Shooter upgrade, one that supplies 10 shells, a box that supplies 20, and a drum magazine that also supplies 20. The 20 shells box also supplies 5 grenades for the shotgun's grenade launcher if it's equipped. The drum magazine pickup only supplies ammo for the Autoshotgun .

Appearances

Shells are the commonest ammo type in the series, often appearing in almost every level in the game they appear in. They can also sometimes be found in secrets. Shells for the autoshotgun are not as as common as normal shells, but can sometimes be found in secrets that contain ammo depots.
