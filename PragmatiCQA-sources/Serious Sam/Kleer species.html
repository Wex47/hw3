Biology

The Kleers were a primitive race of sentient beings that lived on the planet Kleer , the third planet of the Sirius system. They had a similar appearance to kangaroos due to their strong hind legs with hooves, which they used for jumping, and the long tail served for balancing in jumping. Their long forepaws reached the ground, and the Kleers used them for running and walking. In general, they were gentle creatures covered with thick, soft fur, although their bodies were muscular. The Kleers had small straight horns.

History

Before Mental attacked their planet, the Kleers lived a peaceful existence. The Sirians never bothered the Kleers and they just let them live their lives, and the Kleers never showed any desire to leave their planet or learn anything about technology. The Sirians had many other uninhabited planets that they planned to use, and therefore did not see the need for contact with the Kleers.

When Mental attacked Sirius, he first attacked the Sirians, not the Kleers, because the Kleers seemed to be completely harmless. The Sirians contacted the Kleers for help, because the supernatural powers of the Kleers could help stop Mental, but the Kleers were too selfless and happy on their planet to understand the complexity of the situation. Since they had never fought, the very idea of ​​war was incomprehensible to them. Soon, Mental destroyed all of the Sirians, leaving not even a single child alive.

When the Kleers finally realized what the last desperate cry for help from the Sirians meant, it was too late. Suddenly, they realized that they could not help their kind neighbors. And they got completely confused, began to enthusiastically argue about what they could do, why they did not do it, and why they were so blind in relation to the impending threat. Some began to speculate about how to avenge the Sirians, but the idea seemed so out of the ordinary that most did not understand what was meant by revenge. There was no such word in their lexicon. Most of the Kleers did not understand why Mental would attack them. The Kleers had no business with the Sirians, never bothered Mental, and never saw him as prey.

Using telepathy, Mental found out what the Kleers were thinking about. He didn’t think they might actually attack him, but he decided that it would be nice to destroy them all for the sake of caution, or just for fun. Mental sent an automated military fleet to their planet, which dropped seven million tons of burning napalm into the evergreen forests. One terrible day, all land on the planet was set on fire. The Kleers had nowhere to run, some were burned on the spot, some tried to escape, but this was not just a wildfire. Wherever the Kleers fled, fire was everywhere. Everyone died, leaving behind only ash and mountains of bones. Since then, the planet of the Kleers remains a dark planet surrounded by a layer of thick smoke and dust that does not settle.

The ghosts of the Kleers still roamed the dead deserts of their planet and could never rest. And their remains laid under the burned forests, and would never see sunlight again. Mental's twisted mind spawned a brilliant idea. With so many bones and ghosts wandering around the planet, he decided it was great material for necromancy. So, using his high intellect, Mental resurrected the Kleers as skeletons to serve in his army. Since then, the skeletons from Kleers have been among Mental's most vicious servants. They are not alive, but making them die completely was not so easy. They do not care about anything other than the orders of their master, and they are ready to throw out their anger on anyone who Mental orders to attack.

Powers and abilities

As Kleers had lived for millennia without the need for technology, they had developed supernatural abilities such as telekinesis, telepathy and the materialization of objects with the power of thought. They can also materialize a weapon consisting of two joined balls, which they use to toss at their target.

Kleers are very fast and can outrun a running human, allowing them to get in close and attack them with their claws. The bones of Kleers are held together with magical links that makes them quite durable. However, these magical links are weak, and the skeleton can be shattered with a strong blow from a shotgun or heavy object, such as a sledgehammer.

Culture

Kleers were the only intelligent race on their planet, and since nature was merciful to them, they never had to use technology. They lived in large groups in the forests and had a rich culture, sang songs, danced and told each other stories of great hunts. They never built houses or wrote books because they had no fingers. They just lived in the woods and hunted. But, nevertheless, they were a very developed race, with a voluminous cultural heritage. The clerics considered hunting an art, and performed the use of their chainball ability according to a certain custom.
