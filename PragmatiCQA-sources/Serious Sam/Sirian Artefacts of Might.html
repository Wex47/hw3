The Sirian Artefacts of Might , stylized as S.A.M. , is a game mechanic and skill tree introduced in Serious Sam 4 and Serious Sam: Siberian Mayhem .

Overview

As the name implies, the S.A.M. system consists of artefacts that were made by the Sirians . They appear as egg-shaped or spherical objects made from an unknown pink material covered in a gold metallic casing. Their purpose and how they are used is not completely understood, but it is known that they contain nanoparticles, and from within they can grant the user superhuman abilities if the egg is crushed and inhaled. These abilities include being able to dual-wield weapons, reload weapons faster, use certain objects as melee weapons, and even being able to ride large creatures in Mental's Horde , such as Khnums and Sirian Werebulls .

Every time the player obtains a S.A.M., Sam will crush it in his hand and inhale the pink fumes. After this, the skill tree window will pop up and the player will gain 1 skill point, which can be used to unlock a skill. This window can be accessed at any time by invoking NETRICSA .

There are 17 skills to choose from on the tree, but there are 10 S.A.M. eggs in Serious Sam 4 , and x eggs in Serious Sam: Siberian Mayhem . The player should pick wisely with what skills they want to unlock. To unlock a skill, the player needs to press and hold down the primary fire key on the skill they wish to unlock. The skill can be unlearned by pressing and holding the key down on it again. As the player gains more skill points, they can unlock more upgrades further diagonally down the skill tree.

In Serious Sam: Siberian Mayhem , the skill tree becomes somewhat easier to obtain new skills as more paths are connected to the middle row allowing easier access to skills that are further down the tree.

Appearances

S.A.M.s are quite rare objects, appearing only a handful of times throughout the campaign. They are usually found in Earth Defense Force outposts contained within small wooden crates, and can sometimes appear at the location of a side quest. Most of the time, only one S.A.M egg appears per level.

Tactics

  • Find as many S.A.M crates as possible to maximize the chances of unlocking upgrades.
  • The Looter Shooter upgrade can be somewhat useful if the player is low on health, armor or ammo. However, as the amount of health and armor the player receives is miniscule, it shouldn't be seen as helpful if the player needs to recover any lost health or armor after a big fight.
  • The Berserker upgrade can be a bit of a gamble, depending on the situation the player is in. It can useful in small to medium skirmishes, but the player risks being killed if they have this upgrade unlocked and are low on health when facing a big horde of enemies. This is especially true when playing on higher difficulties.
  • The Rodeo Time upgrade can be a useful asset to the player if they want to use enemies like Khnums or Werebulls to run down hordes of weak enemies such as Octanian Commanders , Octanian Gamma Troopers and Processed Anthropolyps . The player should avoid riding into enemies like Beheaded Kamikazes , Major Bio-mechanoids or Scrapjacks , though, as this can quickly deplete the health of the creature they are riding or even kill it if runs directly into Kamikaze explosions or rockets fired by the Major or Scrapjack.
  • The player can unlearn skills for a different skill needed depending on what combat situation they're in.
  • In Serious Sam: Siberian Mayhem , the Healing Punch skill has been significantly buffed as certain enemies heal more health than in Serious Sam 4 .
