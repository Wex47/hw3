Professor Hermann Stein was a scientist who worked for the Earth Defense Force , who assisted them with his research into Ancient Egyptian and alien artifacts, most notably his work on figuring out how to activate the Time-Lock .

He appears as a minor, yet pivotal unseen character in Serious Sam 3: BFE , Serious Sam 3 VR: BFE and Serious Sam VR: The Last Hope , and is indirectly mentioned several times in Serious Sam 4 .

Overview

Little was known about professor Stein. From his name, it can be assumed that he originated from Germany and decided to end up as an archaeologist, as he took a great interest and passion in science and history, particularly the history of Ancient Egypt and the Sirians . During some point in his life. he joined the Earth Defense Force and became one of its many scientists and devoted a great deal of time and research on historical and alien artifacts, such as the Time-Lock and the Time Warp . When he was working there, he met professor Gottlieb Kiesel and the latter assisted Stein with his research.

Appearances

Serious Sam 3: BFE

In the following years, professor Stein had been working hard on his research and, fortunately for everyone, he finally got an answer for what he was looking for. Using all the knowledge he got from the research he held, Stein finally comes to the conclusion that to make the Time-Lock work, someone had to activate two generators that fuel the device, though their precise locations were yet to be uncovered. The professor decides to keep all of the important data on his phone, and, when Quinn calls him to upload the data to headquarters, so other scientists can help, he refuses until he is rescued from the Egyptian Museum in Cairo , where he resides at the time, as the building itself has already been besieged by the aliens.

In order to get Stein back safely, Bravo Team is sent with the task of extracting Stein, but around 0900 hours upon entering the museum, they are ambushed and lose contact with HQ, though the professor himself was fortunate to survive. Upon losing contact with Bravo Team, the command center send in Alpha Team to aid them. The group consists of Jones , Rodriguez , two pilots, one being Miller , and Sam Stone , a Captain in the Earth Defense Force. Upon arriving at their destination, their helicopter comes under attack by missiles and is shot down. As Sam battles his way back to his comrades, he discovers that they have been already killed, thus, Sam decides to go forward with the mission on his own. Sam makes his way inside the museum to find professor Stein, while Quinn contacts him asking to find cover, until his arrival.

After all of the members of Alpha Team, save for Sam, are wiped out, Sam manages to reach the museum where Stein is hiding. Unfortunately, he is too late, as Stein is killed and eaten by a pack of vicious Antaresian Spiders before he can be rescued. However, Sam finds his phone, which has the data he was looking for. With the professor's phone in hand, Sam uploads the photos and data to HQ and then leaves the museum to head to the extraction point.

Serious Sam VR: The Last Hope

At some point in the Mental War , professor Stein contacted the main headquarters of the Earth Defense Force and told them that he believed that in one of the temples in Egypt , there could be tablets which could be deciphered and thus aid them in their quest to activating the Time-Lock. He informed the commanding center that he wished to hold an investigation there, but he required protection due to the fact that Mental's servants were not just hanging around for fun, and would never let Stein make his plan work without a fight. Having understood the importance of this mission, the headquarters made a decision to bring back on Earth their best man, a true symbol in war against the invaders, the commander of the Battlecruiser Saratoga , Captain Sam Stone, also known as Serious Sam.

Not long after that, Saratoga approaches the planet and Stone finds himself back on Earth. Having met Stein, both of them make their way to the mentioned temple in Egypt, but are greeted by waves of Mental's servants as they approach their final destination - the Monolith of Time . Sam orders Stein to get into the temple and do his research while Sam keeps the monsters at bay. As always, Sam crushes the aliens using everything at his disposal, as well as small orbital bombardment provided by Saratoga.

While Sam was slaying Mental's alien forces, Stein was already beginning his work. During this time, Stone manages to finish the first wave of monsters off and follows the object of his protection, but finds something interesting and gets lost inside the temple, coming face to face with another wave of aliens waiting for him in the Tomb of the Forgotten. Finally reunited, the two men try to make their way out of the temple, as professor Stein was eager to tell Sam about the tablets he found. According to his words, these findings may play a vital clue in powering up the Time-Lock. He seems very passionate about them, but Sam himself takes almost no interest in those tablets, as all his thoughts are only about how to kill more aliens.

Such opportunity arrives immediately, as Sam and Stein are ambushed in the Court of Contemplation - one of the temple's courtyards. Stone tells the professor to take cover, as he unleashes another portion of his firepower against everything that dares to head their way. In the end, Sam manages to defeat them.

Following this attack, Sam and Stein have finally leave the temple with the professor's research being successful, but another group of Mental's hordes are waiting for them in the Desert of the Lost with one of their generals - the unholy Space Mummy of Gamma Pegasi. While Stein takes cover, Sam, eager to fight, heads directly at the enemy. The horde are no match for Sam and are eliminated. Finally safe, now that Stein has gained more knowledge and acquired the tablets that he presumably took pictures off, returns to Cairo to continue his research one step closer to the answer, while Stone returns back to the Saratoga.

Soon afterwards, the EDF report that Mental is testing a secret superweapon somewhere on the planet Shaanti . The professor has to stop his work on the Time-Lock for a moment and deal with this new matter. Having conducted some research, Stein manages to find out the precise location of the superweapon and make notes for Sam, who is tasked with locating and destroying the threat. The Saratoga is then immediately sent to Shaanti and Sam, using the professor's notes, though hardly understanding anything, battles his way up to the flying islands of the planet where he encounters Mental's superweapon - a dragon . In the following fight, Stone manages to destroy the creature.

Serious Sam 4

Stein does not appear in Serious Sam 4 , but he is mentioned several times by professor Kiesel.

Trivia

  • If you listen closely to Stein's muffled screams and cries for help, you can hear gunshots before he is killed. This implies that he was using the SOP38 Pistol (or perhaps a similar weapon) to defend himself from the Antaresian Spiders, before being overwhelmed by them.
  • Stein, along with Quinn , are the only two characters not seen in Serious Sam 3 .
  • According to Sam, Stein had terrible handwriting, as it was almost impossible for him to understand anything from the professor's notes during his quest for locating Mental's superweapon.
