Overview

The Aurigan Haemovore is a skinny, levitating humanoid creature that wears a black cape. They originate from a planet in Beta Aurigae , where the species primarily inhabits a vast system of subterranean caves. By a strange twist of fate, the ancestors of these beings happened to resemble a creature not unlike a vampire that appeared in the folklore of the Aurigan sentients. As this trait constituted an evolutionary advantage, making the sentients fear the Haemovore, over the millennia the Haemovore evolved and gained more vampiric-like traits, such as acquiring a thirst for blood, the ability to levitate objects, and super-fast movement by camouflaging themselves as a swarm of bats.

Aurigan Haemovores are fairly common enemies in the campaign, first appearing on the second level Death from Below , who attack Father Mikhail , Kenny and Sam . In the later portions of the game, they are encountered in medium to large groups. Their main purpose is to harass the player when they are dealing with other enemies.

Behavior and skills

A Haemovore has a number of abilities and attacks, depending on how far away it is from the target. When they spot the target, they will transform into a swarm of bats and get closer to them until they are within range. They will also teleport should the player attack the Haemovore with weapons that fire projectiles.

At medium range, the Haemovore will fire a screaming projectile at the player. This projectile has weak homing capabilities and will cause the player's screen to flash showing a display of the creature for a few seconds, which can obscure their vision. At close enough range, the Haemovore will scratch the player with its claws, dealing minimal damage. Occasionally when attacking, the Haemovore will swoop down towards the player and restrain them as it drains their blood. This attack can quickly deplete the player's health and they will be unable to use their weapons unless they get the Haemovore off of them, which can be achieved by pressing the 'Use' key when prompted.

When a Haemovore has been killed, they will be set on fire and their body will crumble away into dust.

Tactics

  • The Haemovore's screaming projectile can be avoided by sidestepping or hiding behind cover.
  • The 12 Gauge Pump-Action Shotgun , Double Barrel Coach Gun and Military Knife work well on lone and few Haemovores, but for groups, the M29 Infantry Assault Rifle or XL2 Lasergun are better options.
  • A direct hit from a grenade fired by the pump shotgun's grenade launcher can instantly kill a Haemovore, but it's necessary to predict its parabolic trajectory first.
  • For larger groups of Haemovores, strong weapons like the A-24 Devastator , Autoshotgun , FRPCL , RAPTOR Sniper Rifle and XM214-A Minigun are effective on them.
  • Haemovores make a distinctive shriek when performing their blood-sucking attack. This attack can be easily dodged by strafing sideways when the Hamemovore prepares to strike.
  • Haemovores tend to instantly teleport upon seeing incoming slow-moving projectiles. Because of that, one should avoid using the XPML21 Rocket Launcher against them.
  • Groups of Haemovores can overwhelm the player if they are not killed quickly on higher difficulties, and should be treated as medium-priority targets.

Trivia

  • A sleeping Haemovore appears as a secret in Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse called Count Dracula. Disturbing him will have him say a quote to the player that is a play on Dracula's lines from Castlevania: Symphony of the Night . He has black hair and much more health than a normal Haemovore.
  • The Haemovore inhabits the same planet as the Aurigan Cave Demon .
