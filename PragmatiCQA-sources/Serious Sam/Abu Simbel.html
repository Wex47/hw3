History

Many millennia ago, the Sirians visited Earth and greeted the local Egyptian people, who viewed them as gods. The Sirians built various constructions underground and in many temples and complexes all throughout Egypt, including Abu Simbel. Inside the temples, the Sirians placed a key, the seal of Set, inside the Great Temple of Ramesses the Great , which served an important purpose for activation of the Time-Lock fail-safe mechanism.

During the 22nd century, during Mental War , many of Earth's cities, towns and other settlements came under siege from Mental 's forces. Abu Simbel suffered the same fate, as abandoned vehicles, garbage and debris laid everywhere. The village was lifeless, which indicated that most, if not all the inhabitants were wiped out. Evidence of this were the propaganda posters, which tempted people to join Mental's army. Before the war took place, Abu Simbel was home to several thousand inhabitants.

Abu Simbel temples

The main features of Abu Simbel were the temples. The first temple was the Great Temple of Ramesses II. Inside the temple was a small statue of Ramesses and a sealed door, which contained the seal of Set, a key that was needed to access another tomb to activate the Time-Lock mechanism. According to legend, placing the statue of Nefertari next to the statue of Ramesses would "open the door to eternity". The statue of Nefertari was never found, thus archaeologists were never able to find it.

The Small Temple

The temple of Hathor and Nefertari, also known as the Small Temple, was built about one hundred meters northeast of the temple of pharaoh Ramesses II, and was dedicated to the goddess Hathor and Ramesses II's chief consort, Nefertari.

Inside the temple, there were four puzzles that needed to be solved to reveal a hidden switch. It didn't matter what order the puzzles were solved in. When all four switches were activated, it would reveal a secret passageway leading to the hidden chamber in the temple, where the statue of Nefertari laid within.

Military base

South of the temples was an abandoned military base, which indicated that the Earth Defense Force were stationed here at this area during the war.

There were two ASU 7 sentry units located at the front of the entrance to prevent intruders or any of Mental's hordes from gaining access inside the base. These turrets were both connected to switches that could be deactivated from the other side.

Appearances

Serious Sam 3: BFE

Abu Simbel is the second location that Sam visits to find the Time-Lock control room. In the Great Temple, Sam finds the statue of Ramesses II, but has to find the statue of Nefertari so he can unlock the door. In the other temple, Sam solves the puzzles and obtains Nefertari's statue. As he has the Arrow bracelet in his possession, which was needed for revealing the hidden switches, he is successful in solving a riddle that had stumbled even the best archaeologists for the past 200 years.

Upon unlocking the door inside the Great Temple, Sam is disappointed to find that inside the room was another key, and not the Time-Lock control room he was hoping for. Quinn speculates that the key bears the symbol of Set , and may be the key for gaining access to the control room in another tomb. Sam leaves Abu Simbel and goes to his next location.

Abu Simbel, along with every other settlement on Earth, was destroyed after Mental sent the Moon hurtling towards it.
