The Exotech Larva is the second boss of Serious Sam: The Second Encounter , Serious Sam HD: The Second Encounter , Serious Sam VR: The Second Encounter , and the third boss of Serious Sam: Xbox and Serious Sam Classics: Revolution .

Overview

The Exotech Larva is a large cybernetic insect that has been heavily modified and experimented on in an attempt to create an obeying bodyguard for Mental . With the success of creating similar cyborgs, the Bio-mechanoids, he decided to go even further. He created the creature by combining robotic parts with an even simpler organic creature - an insect larva. The Larva was heavily armed and put into a 'strength multiplicator' device to enhance its abilities and endurance. However, during this process, the Larva tried to assassinate Mental at least 17 times. After many hours of trying to teach the Larva the difference between a caterpillar and himself, Mental gave up and decided to give his hideous creation a new job; guarding the time portal inside the Tower of Babel .

Appearances

The Exotech Larva is first shown in a cutscene once the player arrives inside the Tower of Babel , appearing from a large mechanical door. It will then slowly move along its cables and approach the player during this sequence and begin firing at the player.

Behavior and skills

The Larva has two attacks; firing large, bouncy plasma balls, and spawning little creatures called Biopods from somewhere on its body. After taking enough damage, one of its arms will blow up and fall off the Larva, making it weaker. When around 2/3rds of its health are gone, the last arm will fly off. After the second arm has been blown off, the creature will enter its final phase; the wires that used to connect the arms will start firing deadly lasers that will hit something as soon as they are fired. These lasers do much more damage than the orbs, especially considering that they can hit twice, requiring the player to hide behind cover in order to avoid it. The Larva's plasma balls and lasers can gib a player upon killing them.

Tactics

Serious Sam 1

  • As soon as the player enter the Larva's room, they should skip the cutscene and shoot a charged-up SBC Cannon cannonball at every generator in the room. There is one on the center part of each side of the room. One charged-up cannon shot will destroy each generator in one shot, preventing the Larva from regenerating its health.
  • Whilst fighting the Larva, make sure to always be near a pillar. The Larva's orbs, and later, lasers, are very powerful, but they'll have no effect if they hit a pillar the player is hiding behind.
  • If it becomes difficult to hide behind a pillar because of how close the Larva is, switch to another pillar as soon as it's done firing. Being further away makes it easier to hide from its attacks.
  • The Larva has an alarmingly high 75% resistance to cannonball damage, so don't use the cannon against it except as a last resort.
  • Rapid-fire weapons such as the XM214-A Minigun and XL2 Lasergun will work great against the Larva because it lets the player attack both the Larva and the Biopods it spawns. While the damage may not be as high as something like the rocket launcher, it does make it much easier to kill the creatures the Larva spawns while doing damage to the Larva itself.
  • In the Serious Sam Classics: Revolution version of the game, using the XL4-P 'Ghostbuster' Beam Gun against it is ill-advised, as while it boasts a high firerate it is incapable of hitting the creatures it spawns. This is likely a bug.
  • The MK III Grenade Launcher and XPML21 Rocket Launcher work well against the Larva, but they make it much more difficult to kill the creatures the Larva spawns at close range, as killing one as close range puts the player at risk from being harmed from their own rocket or grenade exploding near them.
  • When the last arm is being blown off, the player should immediately run to one of the pillars and make sure the Larva can't see them. The lasers the Larva fires are notably more damaging than its orbs are, and being out when it first starts firing will either take away a lot of health or outright kill the player.
  • During the final phase of the fight, the eerie sound it makes is a big hint that the Larva is about to fire its lasers. As soon as this sound is heard, run behind a pillar and stay there until the zapping sound from its lasers firing is gone. After that, attack it again until its lasers start to fire at the player.
  • There is a useful but cheap trick for fighting off the Larva. Between ball volleys, run up to the center of it with the P-LAH Chainsaw and start attacking it until the chainsaw starts slicing up the creatures it spawns. The player will be too close for the Larva to hit with its orbs and the creatures will be killed as soon as they appear, which makes it completely harmless. This is very useful in co-op, as it lets the other players attack the Larva without having to worry about things like hiding behind pillars. However, this tactic should be abandoned as soon as the last arm is blown off, as its lasers will be able to hit the player while they're chainsawing the Larva.

Trivia

  • In the classic version of the game, the Larva's gibs are shown after it explodes, though in the HD and VR renditions, they are not shown.
  • The Larva's boss music theme is an instrumental rearrangement of "As Above So Below" by Undercode, from their album "Enlightening the World". It was later reused for the Cecil boss fight in Serious Sam 2 .
    • A modern rearrangement of "As Above So Below", titled "Above All Hope", appears in Serious Sam: Siberian Mayhem .
