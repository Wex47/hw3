The Oracle is a minor character who appears in Serious Sam 3: BFE and Serious Sam 3 VR: BFE . He had a major influence in Ancient Egyptian culture, and was perhaps the last known Sirian .

Overview

The Oracle was a Sirian who stood at least 10 to 12 ft tall. He had greyish-brown skin and glowing blue eyes, which shined like crystals. He wore Egyptian pharoah attire with a metallic crown or helmet on his head. On his wrist was the Sirian Mutilator , or more commonly referred to as "the Arrow".

History

Several thousand years ago, the Sirians sent a scouting party to Earth and landed in Ancient Egypt . Among one of these Sirians was the Oracle. The Egyptians were amazed by the Sirians and their superior technology and worshiped them like gods. The Sirians took this into account and had the Egyptians begin massive construction projects, such as the Great Pyramid of Giza .

Like the other Sirians who lived in Egypt, the Oracle was treated as a deity. He, too, like the rest of his people, made many requests, in which he would provide them with gifts of treasures in return. One such request was the tabernacle be built in his favor, then he would show the Egyptians the power to wield the Arrow. Once it was built by Khufu , the Oracle was satisfied. Later on in his life, he bestowed the Arrow to Amun-her-khepeshef , the firstborn son of Pharaoh Ramesses II and Queen Nefertari , as a gift.

Throughout his life, the Oracle had the Egyptians carve hieroglyphs referring to the Mutilator bracelet and the Time-Lock for archaeologists to decipher. By doing so, he hoped that one day, future humans would be able to find out how to power up the Time-Lock.

Eventually, the rest of the Sirians died out, leaving the Oracle the lone survivor of his race. Soon, he died of unknown causes, though he may have just succumbed to old age. Before he died, he made one final request: have the Mutilator bracelet buried with him in his shrine. When he died, he was mummified and laid to rest on a golden throne surrounded by four alien statues within the Great Pyramid; having the gods "watch over him" from the Egyptians' perspective.
