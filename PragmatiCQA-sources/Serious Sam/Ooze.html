Overview

Ooze is a green radioactive or mutagenic liquid. It is highly toxic and unstable, causing anyÂ organic matter that comes into contact with it to become mutated. Once infected, the creature(s) undergo severe mutations and deformities, and become extremely aggressive towards anything that hasn't been infected by the ooze. Whenever Sam or one of Mental 's forces steps onto ooze, it rapidly depletes their health and kills them, which could imply that ooze doesn't always react the same way when it comes into contact with something.

Due to the unpredictable nature of the ooze, it will sometimes come alive by itself, creating small green blobs called Ooze Spawn , which are usually found in barrels. Like wildlife exposed to the ooze, ooze spawn will target non-infected life forms. Ooze spawn is relatively short-lived, and will eventually explode.

History

The ooze was created by the Zorgs using various experiments in an attempt to get their skin back after losing it due to intense solar radiation from the sun on their home planet. It did give them skin back, however, it also hideously mutated them, making them grow larger and made their skin green. This made them look completely different from the unaffected Zorgs, earning themsevles the name of the Orcs .

When the unaffected Zorgs found out about the ooze, they banned it and exiled the Orcs to the planet Orazio, where they began to start a wide-scale planetary mining company, the Altani Corporation. When Mental found about the company, the Orcs, and the ooze they used on themselves, he forged an alliance with the company, renaming it to Mental-Altani in the process.

After Mental discovered the Orcs' company and the ooze, he had the Orcs produce the ooze again on a massive scale, and then had them go to any planet they could find that harbored life, including Earth . Once they arrived on their desired planet, they would test the ooze on the planet's lifeforms, wrecking havoc on the ecosystems. Sometime later, Mental-Altani found Earth and set up their operations on the planet and contaminated its soil and water, infecting its wildlife with the ooze. Some of the creatures that became infected started sprouting legs and gained intelligence, while other lifeforms, such as carnivorous plants , developed an appetite for humans.

Not only did Mental want the Orcs to use the ooze to wipe out life in the universe, he also wanted to use the ooze to enhance some of his own units, such as Gnaars . The Orc scientists experimented on the Gnaars and exposed them to the ooze, creating the Infected Gnaar . Unfortunately for them, this only resulted in the Gnaar becoming aggressive towards its creators, and even turning on their own kind.

Application

Despite being a biohazard, the Orcs are not affected by the ooze (though they can still take damage from it) due to having already been exposed to it. The ooze is frequently stored in barrels, though they are not kept very secure, and tend to leak out and flood the Orc bases and other areas. The Orcs sometimes dump the barrels of ooze into the Earth's oceans to dispose of them, which in turn pollutes the water and mutates anything living underwater.

On the Orcs' Moon base, they kept ooze more securely contained inside glass vat containers, though they were more susceptible to being broken this way, allowing ooze spawn to escape.

Transporting ooze was not always an easy task, considering its toxicity. To counter this problem, the Zorgs came up with an easier method by creating the Oozegun , a utility tool that was used for spewing ooze into containers for shipping. The tool was later modified to be used on the battlefield by the Orcs.
