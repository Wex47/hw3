Overview

The Octanian raider is a small aircraft used by the Octanians, a race of creatures controlled by colonies by polyps. The raider is powered by two engines located on either side of the wing and the body of the aircraft is designed to be nimble and highly maneuverable, which can make it difficult to shoot them down on the ground.

Application

Used extensively throughout the Tunguksa Offensive, raiders were commonly deployed into heavily populated areas or combat zones to ensure air superiority in the skies. Unlike comparable Earth aircraft, the raider is not piloted by an Octanian soldier, but by a colony of cyber-polyps. Due to the "pilots" not requiring training, these easy-to-produce aircraft are used en masse to establish air superiority in the early stages of planetary conquest by Mental’s horde, clearing the way for the Harvester Motherships to begin processing the population. Raiders often fly in groups of up to 5, targeting buildings or random locations and tossing bombs.

After the Alien Artifact Acquisition team succeed in grounding the raider fleet in Italy with the eruption of Mount Vesuvius, raiders are encountered a lot less frequently, though it's probable that they still remained in great numbers in France and other European countries.

Weaponry

Despite being fighter aicraft, raiders seem to play more of a role akin to that of a bomber, deploying their bombs on buildings or targeting areas of strategic value, such as military conveys. These bombs are very destructive, capable of causing severe infrastructure damage or destroying several vehicles, such as when they attacked the Earth Defense Force convey on their way to the Vatican.

Tactics

  • Raiders do not pose a threat to the player unless they are right next to the raider's bomb radius when they explode.
  • Their bombs can deal a lot of damage, so stay clear of Raiders when they are about to release them.
  • As raiders are very fast aircraft, rapid-firing weapons such as the XM214-A Minigun or XL2 Lasergun are needed to take them down.

Behind the scenes

  • An unused laser projectile can be found in the game files, suggesting that the raider was going to fire lasers at some point, but was scrapped before the final version's release.

Trivia

  • Raiders behave just like the Seagull Fighter , a bomber aircraft in Serious Sam 2 . Like the latter, seagull fighters only target specific areas with bombs.
