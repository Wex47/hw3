The TCC2 "Perun" Crossbow is a powerful precision weapon introduced in Serious Sam: Siberian Mayhem .

Overview

Named after the Slavic God of lightning and thunder, the lethal potential of this military-grade crossbow does more than justice to its lofty name

Despite being modeled on a comparatively old design, the Perun utilizes all the latest achievements of modern weapons development. It combines an electronic loading device with a pneumatic launch mechanism and shoots metallic bolts. Its body and strings are made of nickel-steel alloy, allowing for increased durability and strength. The electronic converter increased kinetic energy, resulting in remarkable bolt speeds, in turn leading to an accumulation of electrical energy that is unleashed on the target upon impact.

The sniper scope is the final ingredient to transforming the Perun from normal crossbow into a lethal weapon that will satisfy even the most demanding snipers.

Appearances

The Perun is given to the player in the chapter " Siberiade " by One-Eyed Olga . It replaces the RAPTOR Sniper Rifle and A-24 Devastator in the campaign.

Tactics

  • This weapon should be primarily used to snipe large enemies that are far away from player's position, such as Scrapjacks , Adult Arachnoids , Major Bio-mechanoids , Witch-Brides or Aludran Reptiloids .
  • Due to its penetrating nature, the Crossbow can be used against enemies that tend to line up together such as Kleer Skeletons , Processed Anthropolyps , Gnaars , or Sirian Werebulls .
  • While fighting against Draconian Pyromaniacs , or Burners , one should aim for their fuel tanks to deal increased damage or instantly kill them.
  • The Perun is arguably the best weapon to counter Octanian Sentry Towers ' turrets from medium-long distances.
  • The TCC2 is extremely useful against Octanian Zealots and their stronger brethren, Octanian Legionnaires , due to fact the bolts penetrate their armor and helmets without losing much damage.

Trivia

  • This weapon seems to be visually based on the FN P90 submachine gun with its casing extractor working as a built-in magazine.
