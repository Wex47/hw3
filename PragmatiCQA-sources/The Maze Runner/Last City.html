The Last City appears in the Death Cure film . It is seemingly the last major settlement of humanity following the Sun Flares and the subsequent Flare Pandemic . The actual location of this megapolis is unknown. The city serves as the primary headquarters of WCKD and is its main base of operations from which it coordinates its efforts in obtaining immune children for the production of a cure .

The Last City's equivalent in the original book series is Denver . The film portrayal differs greatly from the books in many respects.

History

The city's general history remains a mystery. In the Maze Runner novels, the city in which most of the events of the Death Cure took place was known as Denver , capital of the U.S. state of Colorado, but in the films, not much is known about the Last City prior to the Sun Flares . The city was likely, but by no means was certainly, a prosperous metropolis up until the impact of the solar storm. The Last City, as it likely had come to be known, would survive the cataclysmic event, albeit with its infrastructure possibly damaged. It is also possible that the city was constructed after the Sun Flares by either the Post-Flares Coalition or WCKD itself. The theory is increasingly plausible due to its many deviations from pre-Sun-Flare cities and lack of any resemblance to Denver.

According to WETA Digital (the VFX company behind the Last City's portrayal within the motion picture), the city was built directly upon Cape Town of South Africa . This theory is ever more plausible due to the fact that the Last City's road layout and some of its landmarks such as Portside Tower are consistent with that of Cape Town. This, coupled with the South African geography (including the inclusion of Cape Town's famous Table Mountain, Signal Hill which is shown to be very close to the City Wall, and the distant mountains) being directly consistent with what is portrayed in the film and the fact that Cape Bay, exactly like the Last City, was historically protected by a natural mountain range which relegated entry within via natural barriers are very clear evidences providing major plausibility to the theory that the Last City was built directly upon the ruins of Cape Town.

Aside from theories, the Last City's general history remains a collection of esoterica. Basing off of the theory that the city existed before the Sun Flares, the Post-Flares Coalition – and later WCKD – took over administrative control of the city, constituting major construction efforts in all areas of the metropolis, including several skyscrapers (most of which, including the buildings known as "Portside" and "YKS Megacorp" are presumed to be privately owned by corporate bodies) as well as WCKD's primary headquarters and research facility.

Sometime after the city's founding, or reconstruction, it increased in size immeasurably as refugees from the rest of the Scorch flocked to the safety, security and resumption of civilization that the authorities governing the area appeared to provide. It is presumed that, during this time, it became the largest post-Sun Flare megapolis in the world.

When the Flare Virus Pandemic began, WCKD created several quarantine checkpoints, strategically established at key choke point locations in the mountains surrounding the Last City in an attempt to prevent the virus from breaching the city limits. During this time Jorge is understood to have visited the city for some unknown reasons, making note of both WCKD's major presence there and the measures the organisation were taking to defend it. Sometime after this, WCKD likely began making preparations for safe zones in and around high density districts in the event that the checkpoints defending the city were breached, including the construction of a titanic protective wall in the 2.5km surrounding WCKD HQ .

Eventually, at least one of the quarantine checkpoints surrounding the city was breached, and shortly after this, many areas of the city likely began falling into chaos. It however must be noted that refugees continued to pour in from across the Scorch, as it is stated that, by the events of the Death Cure film, the slums that had accumulated around the inner-city wall stretched on for a massive 25km . WCKD steadily began retreating its urban operations to the well-defended inner sections of the city, while slowly leaving other neighbourhoods behind as the virus slowly took hold of everywhere beyond the wall. With the quarantine checkpoints now abandoned (and infested with cranks ) the amount of refugees attempting to travel to the Last City itself is presumed to have later starkly declined; however, the denizens of the Last City themselves continued to flock to the safe zone at its centre. WCKD continued to permit entry to the safe zone, but entry procedures quickly became highly stringent so as to avoid a repetition of what caused the outer city to fall. Rules and safe zone policy became extraordinarily strict: curfews were strictly enforced; infection checks in public spaces were imposed; the city's network was integrated into one uniform digital space whose only constant was universal surveillance by WCKD; and other regulations so strict that even appearing to be infected in any way, such as coughing, was cause for deportation.

It is worth noting however that a major civilian presence continued to reside in the outer city, showing that the Flare had not completely wiped out the population of the outer city.

Three months before the events of the Death Cure film , WCKD's Director of Operations, Dr. Ava Paige , ordered the closing of the city's primary entrance (and only entrance to civilians), the main gate. To the dismay of both the safe zone's urbanite inhabitants and the desperate refugees alike, Paige deemed the addition as a "temporary precaution" against the increasing threat of infection from the outside. This, along with the complete cut-off from the safety and supplies provided by WCKD from outside the wall, exacerbated tensions between the lawless survivors of the outer-city and the authorities in the inner-city – to the extent where the consensus among outer-city residents was that the people of the safe zone were careless to the rapidly worsening issues faced outside the city wall.

In the final month prior to the City's collapse, the people living and working in the city had begun to lose faith in WCKD's efforts in finding a cure , burdened with ever stricter regulations and led on pipe dreams alone. In a monthly progress report to WCKD's Board of Directors, Doctor Ava Paige had revealed to them that Subject A7 – Minho , displayed the highest and most promising antibodies to the Flare seen thus far, the BoD unanimously expressed their newfound doubts to what appeared to be just another pipe-dream for a cure. They also expressed their own thoughts about instead protecting people from the threat instead of attempting to fight it and initially rejected Paige's request to initiate a new round of trials on the infected using the antibodies projected from subject A7. Faith was somewhat restored after Teresa Agnes convinced them that a breakthrough to a cure was closer than ever, and were convinced to begin human trials.

Shortly after this, Ava Paige herself began to lose faith in WCKD's efforts, confessing to Janson that cases of an airborne Flare Virus had been detected around the safe zone, and that at the rate it was spreading the city would be in the same state as its exterior in a month. Already affected by the Flare himself, Janson proposed the creation of another safe zone in an alternate location, but Paige, remorseful of WCKD's previous efforts to find a cure at any cost, replied, "It's not about giving up, Janson. It's about knowing when you've lost."

During the latter events of the Death Cure film, Thomas , Newt , and Gally breached the safe zone by traversing an abandoned network of tunnels running beneath the city. While in WCKD HQ attempting to rescue Minho, Gally installed a hacking device on WCKD's firewall, deactivating the city's digital defences and allowing Lawrence 's men to hijack the defensive turrets that had previously precluded any move to storm the wall protecting the city.

Later in the night, Lawrence gathered a mob of armed outer city residents, riled their anger against WCKD and the establishment that had seemingly abandoned them, before blowing up both himself and the now defenceless main gate.

WCKD reacted near instantaneously by evacuating their staff and research, sounding sirens to alert the inner city denizens to the breach and deploying Zone Control at key choke points around the city in a vain attempt to halt any advance made by the thousands upon thousands of refugees now pouring into the safe zone.

WCKD's Zone Control, despite being highly trained and highly equipped, were completely outnumbered by the sheer quantity of people storming the city, and chaos slowly engulfed street after street. A massive conflagration ensued, leading to attacks on the very infrastructure that kept the city online.

By the end of the film, WCKD HQ was destroyed and organised resistance to the attackers slowly diminished. In the city's final shot, it is seen engulfed in flames and anarchy. The majority of the city was aflame.

Government

The Last City was administratively governed by WCKD , headed by the Board of Directors, with the Director of Operations, Dr. Ava Paige , providing the most influence unto their decisions. The city is protected and patrolled by WCKD's "Zone Control" division, mainly overseen by Ava's Assistant Director of Operations, WCKD's Zone Control act as the city's full law enforcement and military army, like an evolved CDC. Their duties are highly varied from patrolling the streets, enforcing city curfew, removing and arresting any infected civilians, guarding the WCKD HQ, and guarding the wall.
