This is about the Freddy Krueger's Glove of the A Nightmare on Elm Street film series. If you are looking for one or more other versions of Freddy Krueger's Glove, please check the Freddy Krueger's Glove Disambiguation .

Freddy Krueger's Glove is the main weapon of Freddy Krueger . It is based on the ninja weapon neko te.

He made it himself, before he was burned to death, out of sheet metal, pipes, fishing knives, rivets, and an old leather work glove in his boiler room . He made it to kill with. According to Freddy's Dead: The Final Nightmare , he made at least several other gloves, with other designs.

Marge Thompson confiscated the original glove, so the one Freddy uses in the Dream World , and gets pulled into the physical world with him on several occasions, is obviously a different glove. So, when Freddy has been pulled into the physical world, two gloves are in the physical world at the same time, and could hypothetically come into contact with each other.

Description

The main glove consisted of fourteen parts: the glove, the backplate, four fingers, four fingertips, and the four blades. Freddy created his iconic glove before he was burned to death. Part of the glove's creation was seen in A Nightmare on Elm Street . Freddy used it mostly to slash or kill his victims. It had four blades, and the thumb was excluded. As a cameo, the glove appeared in Jason Goes to Hell: The Final Friday. The morning after Jason was pulled into Hell, Freddy's hand sprouted up, grabbed Jason's hockey mask, and dragged it down to Hell as well.

It is shown in Freddy's Dead that he'd made other gloves, with different designs than his main glove.

Gloves

A Nightmare on Elm Street (1984 film) and A Nightmare on Elm Street 2: Freddy's Revenge (film)

The glove that was seen in those films, was constructed by Lou Carlucci, and had very short blades. There was also a large rivet on the index finger. Overall, that glove was tiny compared to the others. There was also a scene in Freddy's Revenge where blades grew from Freddy's fingers.

It disappeared from the set of A Nightmare on Elm Street 2.

A Nightmare on Elm Street 3: Dream Warriors (film)

That film featured hypodermic needles and syringes in place of knives in one scene, but overall, the blades were larger and the backplate was weathered.

A Nightmare on Elm Street 4: The Dream Master (film)

Similar to the Dream Warriors Glove, but its overall color became more green and red with messy spills of solder all over.

A Nightmare on Elm Street 5: The Dream Child (film)

Similar to the Dream Master Glove, but the overall color became more bright and red, with bigger blades and fewer and more silver solder spills.

Freddy's Dead: The Final Nightmare (film)

That one was closer to the Dream Child Glove. It featured thicker spills of solder than either one, but also thicker metal fingers. The glove was also lighter in color.

Aside from the main hero glove, there was a flashback sequence where Kathryn Krueger discovered numerous bladed gloves in the basement, all of which had different designs. One glove featured spikes on the backplate and fingers rather than long blades on the fingertips. That type of glove was clearly intended for backhanding a victim.

Freddy vs. Jason (film)

That one was closest to the Dream Warriors Glove, but it had grey color and huge blades.
