Freddy's Nightmares: A Nightmare on Elm Street: The Series is an American horror anthology series, which aired in syndication from October 8, 1988 until March 12, 1990.

Many episodes are black comedy oriented.

From information, including dates, in the episodes themselves, it can be concluded that the series takes place in the same time frame as when it originally aired (1988-1990) and that the episodes take place in chronological order.

A spin-off from the A Nightmare on Elm Street film series, each story was introduced by Freddy Krueger (played, as in the film series, by Robert Englund ). This format is essentially the same as that employed by Alfred Hitchcock Presents , Tales from the Crypt , or The Twilight Zone. The pilot episode was directed by Tobe Hooper. It begins with Freddy Krueger's dismissal of the child-murdering charges due to his arresting officer's lack of reviewing the Miranda warning at the time of Freddy's arrest. A mob of parents eventually corners Freddy in a power plant (his workplace), leading to him being torched by the police officer, dying and gaining his familiar visage.

The series predominantly takes place in and around Springwood , the town that Freddy Krueger terrorized. Although a few stories are about Freddy, most are about other scary events that take place in the ill fated town.

Production

The series was produced by New Line Television, producers of the film series. It was originally distributed by Lorimar Television. However, Warner Bros. Television would assume syndication rights after acquiring Lorimar (New Line is now a subside of Warner Bros.)
