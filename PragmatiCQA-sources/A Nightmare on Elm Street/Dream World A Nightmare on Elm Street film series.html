This is about the Dream World of the A Nightmare on Elm Street film series. If you are looking for one or more other versions of the Dream World, please check the Dream World (disambiguation) .

Note: Logically, this version of the Dream World would have to also exist in the Friday the 13th films previous to Freddy vs. Jason.

The Dream World is a dimension parallel to the physical world. It must be noted that in this context "world", when referring to either the physical world or the Dream World, doesn't mean planet. It means dimension. Hypothetically, the Dream World may contain all the planets, stars, galaxies, and so on the physical world does.

The nature of the Dream World is only vaguely explained.

It is clearly shown that objects can be pulled out of the Dream World into the physical world. It is strongly implied that objects can be pulled out of the physical world into the Dream World as well.

It is strongly hinted that the line between the physical world and the Dream World is inherently blurry. This isn't even counting Springwood in Freddy's Dead: The Final Nightmare .

Both humans and animals can access the Dream World while asleep.

It is post-death Freddy Krueger 's main domain. He both torments and kills his victims there. In the Dream World, Freddy is powerful to the point of invincibility, and is able to withstand any injury that would normally harm or kill a normal person. He is able to manipulate his victims' dreams to whatever he sees fit, mostly in the form of his victims' personality and/or fears.

Any injury that people and animals sustain in the Dream World affects them in the physical world as well, and, if they die in the Dream World, they die in the physical world as well. This is an example of it being hinted that the line between the physical world and the Dream World is blurry.

Although dreamers are able to manifest their own powers in the Dream World, they are rarely, if ever, close to being a match for Freddy's own power. Among dreamers, only Alice Johnson has shown comparable power to Freddy in the Dream World. (Amanda Krueger has shown comparable power in the Dream World, but at the time, she wasn't a living dreamer accessing it through sleep.)
