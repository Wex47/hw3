Dave Barry (August 26, 1918 – August 16, 2001), born David Siegal , was an American actor, voice actor and comedian, who provided Bluto 's voice in six Popeye cartoons between 1942 and 1944, all of them uncredited: 1942 – Kickin' the Conga Round , Alona on the Sarong Seas , and A Hull of a Mess ; 1943 – Seein' Red, White 'N' Blue and Too Weak to Work ; and 1944 – W'ere on Our Way to Rio .

Barry got his start with Famous Studios during a 1942 Miami stand-up performance. He was doing his act at a hotel when a man from the audience (who worked for the Miami-based Famous Studios) approached him at the bar after the show. He said they needed a deep baritone voice for Popeye's nemesis Bluto in a series of animated features.
