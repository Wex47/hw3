Crazy Credits

  • The penguins kick the fishing boy off the "DreamWorks" logo. (The boy's voice is supplied by Jeffrey Katzenberg, uncredited).

General

  • This is the last film for Bernie Mac (who voiced Zuba, Alex's Dad) before his death. This movie was dedicated to him.
  • The sequel Madagascar 3 (2012) was confirmed months before this one came out.
  • Kowalski's abacus makes its first appearance in the films.
  • Moto Moto means 'Hot Hot' in Swahili.
  • The song from this movie, "Time 2 Move It/I Like To Move It", was used in a 2008 Chrysler car commercial.
  • A specially-made segment featuring Julien and Private was used as part of a 2008 Comcast Digital Voice commercial.
  • A specially-made segment featuring Alex , Gloria , Melman , Marty , and the penguins was used as part of a 2008 McDonald's restaurant commercial.
  • Alex's family birth-mark is shaped like the continent of Africa, including the island of Madagascar.
  • The aircraft used in the escape attempt is based on a Lockheed L-10 Electra, similar to one used by Amelia Earhart. the plane also resembles a Mcdonnell Douglas DC3.
  • The hippopotamus who asks if Alex is dancing about a plane crash is voiced by Stacy Ferguson , better known by her stage name, Fergie.
  • Ville Valo, the lead singer of the Finnish band 'H.I.M.' does the voice for "Moto Moto" in the Finnish version of the movie.
  • Jada Pinkett Smith 's daughter, Willow Smith , plays young Gloria in the movie. Ben Stiller 's son, , plays the part of young Alex . This makes Jada and Ben the only Madagascar voice actors any of whose children did their characters' juvenile voices.
    • Surprisingly, in the Latin American Spanish dub Melman is voiced by Ricardo Mendoza while his younger version is voiced by his son, Ricardo Mendoza Jr.
  • The film's original title was Madagascar 2: The Crate Escape . Many publicity items and memorabilia were made with this title before it was changed to Madagascar: Escape 2 Africa.
  • All of the zebras in the film were voiced by Chris Rock .
  • The first Madagascar movie to release in IMAX.
  • The film reveals that Marty once owned an iPod.
  • The "I Like Them Chunky" song was used on Discovery Channel's 2010 "Pumpkin Chumpkin" Commercals.
  • In the beginning of the movie in the flashback, the skyline of New York City was shown, which the Twin Towers (1 & 2 World Trade Center/North & South Towers before their destruction) are clearly visible, this was because the day when Alex came to the central park zoo was on April 8th, 1972.
  • Stock footage from Madagascar was used during the News Report Segment, however there was a scene where the Old Lady from the First Movie and The Pilot Episode of Penguins of Madagascar Madagascar Penguins: A Christmas Caper was asked by the news reporters of what happen during the raid, to which she respond: "He was a very bad kitty". And another scene was shown before the cargo ship scene from the first movie. Which shows that the four animal crates cataining Alex, Marty, Gloria, Melman were being loaded to the ship. And the final scene from the news report was all of the new york inhabiants going to the central park zoo morning the loss of the zoo animals after finding out that they were missing.
  • The newspaper at the beginning of the film with the headline "Lion Cub Fished From Sea" has the date set at Wednesday, 8 April 1972. However, 8 April 1972 was a Saturday.

Movie Mistakes

  • The birthmark on Alex's paw appears and disappears between shots.
  • There is a shot in which the monkey is cutting through the middle frame with a grinder, then in the next shot he actually completes the task on the rear one.
  • In the very end of the movie, Alex glances at Marty's butt, which has a bite mark supposedly made by Alex in Madagascar (2005). However, the mark cannot be seen in any other scene that shows Marty's butt, such as the very first one when they are all shaking their butts with the music.
  • In fight between Nana and Alex on the African road, at the end, we see a shot of the bus directly behind them, but after the fight, the old lady walks quite a ways to get to the bus.
  • In the scene where the penguins steal the tour jeep, the tour guide has no gun yet he has one when the humans pick up the old lady after the penguins leave with the jeep.
  • When they ram the dam, you can clearly see Nana's purse fly into the water and go down with the dam, but they later give it to Makunga.
  • At the beginning of the film when the zoo-keeper places Alex on the newspapers on top of a stone table where Alex performs, the newspaper disappears moments later.
  • The birth mark on Alex's paw was never seen in the first film.
  • After Nana beats up Alex on the road, she gets back on the tour jeep and it drives away. Alex picks up her purse, dumps it out and drops it on the ground. Later, after the penguins hijack the jeep and drive over Nana, her fellow tourist says "Lady, I found your pocketbook," and returns it to her, when they were obviously a lot farther down the road from the initial Alex and Nana fight location.
  • When Kowalski says: "Only two passengers unaccounted for, sir." after the plane crash, he is referring to King Julien and Maurice, who flew out the side door earlier.
  • Nana's exclaim of "Bring it on!" her "Bad kitties." quote is muted in the Latin Spanish dub.

Easter Eggs

  • At one point, during the scene where the penguins hijack the safari tour jeep, Rico can be seen using a dispenser.
  • Many Crayons have been shown with the Penguins in some scenes, with Kowalski using a Crayon, these branded Crayons belong to the Real Life Crayon Company, which produces Crayons for Children to Draw.
  • The Original Title for this movie, Madagascar 2: The Crate Escape, may be a reference to the 1999 Ubisoft Classic. Rayman 2: The Great Escape .
  • Alex's Line, "These Guys, are white with black stripes, You're Black with White Stripes." Is a callback to Madagascar 1, where Marty says "I don't know if I'm Black, with White Stripes, or White, with Black Stripes".
  • Marty's Bite Mark from Madagascar 1 stays intact over of the course of the Movies anyway. And Speaking of Marty's Bite Mark, after Alex saw Mort in the window, Marty says in I Quote: "oh yeah like you bit me on the Butt". Which is a reference to to Madagascar 1, where Alex bitted Marty's butt after turning Wild, which resulted in him seeing a lot of Steaks.

Incorrectly regarded as goofs

  • The title, "Madagascar: Escape 2 Africa," might appear to be misleading since Madagascar could be considered part of the African continent. However, whether Madagascar is strictly part of Africa is perhaps geographically debatable, and certainly politically debatable. Technically, Madagascar is an island east of Africa. Politically, there is no pan-African government, perhaps Madagascar being it's own country is generally debatable. considering the various botched plans involved in the plot, the filmmakers undoubtedly intended the subtitle "Escape 2 Africa" to be somewhat Historically Intentional.

Anachronisms

  • The newspaper headline showing Alex being rescued from his crate is dated 'April 8, 1972', yet the 2nd Twin Tower of the World Trade Center was not completed until 1973. This would also put Alex's current age at around 36.
    • this should be noted that up until the 911 Attacks, the WTC has been around since 1971, but the ribbon wasn't cut to present it to the public till 1973.

Controvesy

The plot of Makunga using Alex to become alpha lion and then things going bad while Makunga is alpha lion and then Alex having to do something to fix was criticize to be similar to the Original 1994 Disney Movie titled The Lion King with Makunga also looking exactly like the Villain Scar, while Zuba and the fact that the Watering Hole was basically the Pride Lands was straight up Mufasa. In fact the entire plot of Makunga 's Evil Plan was similar to Scar's Evil Plan in the Original and the Remake of The Lion King , in which Scar uses Simba to kill Mufasa and become king, then things going bad meaning Simba has to do something about it. The Only Difference, Zuba stays alive throughout the entire Movie, while Mufasa dies during The Lion King. Another Thing is that Alex was Older when he was banished from the Watering Hole, while Simba was a Young Cub and was believed to be dead along with Mufasa, but in reality he was banished believing that he killed his own father.
