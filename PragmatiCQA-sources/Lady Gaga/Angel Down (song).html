" Angel Down " is a song written by Lady Gaga and RedOne for her fifth studio album Joanne . The song is the 11th and final track on standard edition of the album.

Background

The song was inspired by the death of 17-year-old Trayvon Martin, who was shot dead by George Zimmerman in 2012. His shooting death launched the Black Lives Matter movement. Lady Gaga said she wrote the track in response to " the epidemic of young African-Americans being murdered in this country. "

“ I was overwhelmed by the fact that people just stood around and didn’t do anything about it and that the justice system continues to over and over again not seek justice for these families, ” Lady Gaga told Beats 1 's Zane Lowe.

The song's lyrics expressing confusion at the absolute apathy Gaga sees following an "angel" getting gunned down in the street.

Work Tape

Work Tape is an alternate version of Angel Down and is included on the deluxe version of Joanne . The work tape is a more stripped version of the main version of the song, and does not feature as much electronic production. The work tape is also recorded in a higher key and features rawer vocals. This version is produced by RedOne.
