The echeladder is a rating system in the Homestuck universe. By successfully defeating enemies in Strife or completing certain tasks you can ascend Achievement Rungs. By climbing these rungs you may be rewarded with various upgrades and items including Boondollars , Gel Viscosity, Cache Limit, Man Grit, and a feather to place in your cap or a frog to have, which for some reason only appear when leveling up and disappears shortly after. John has reached the top of his echeladder gaining the level of Heir Transparent, and Dave has supposedly hit the top as well, though we do not know any of his rungs. The titles within rungs change depending on the individual.

A common way for players to climb the ladder is through combat, but John is able to gain several levels by creating the paradox clones and doing The Windy Thing, so one can climb the echeladder by doing something related to the "main quest" of each player - Jade breeding Bilious Slick , Dave getting Caledfwlch , Rose learning to , players waking up their Denizens, defeating them, solving riddles tied to temples on their planets (and such boring stuff) could make them climb the echeladder as well. Tavros might have gained levels from leading an army of underlings, despite never killing anything himself, or the underlings themselves might have gained levels from combat.

Echeladders are not necessarily reserved for players; Nannasprite mentions having . Nor are they even limited to living things; many objects are capable of rising in the echeladder when they defeat enemies on their own, such as books advancing when dropped on enemies via the Sburb interface. This does not appear to apply to normal weapons.

The ladder apparently has a major effect on a player's abilities, as players at the end of the game are able to take down the extremely powerful Black King and Queen , and kill enemies that were almost boss-like in the beginning of the game with a single attack. Advancing through tiers seems to increase a character's strength, as Alternate Future John was presumably killed by The Slumbering One because he was too low on the echeladder, and we see the main version of John able to wield larger weapons than the sledgehammer he initially struggled to use in various portions of the comic. It is also implied that Jane's advancements through the echeladder increased her strength . Aside from increasing strength, it is implied that damage done by weapons wielded by characters is also automatically increased, since some of Jade and Jake's ladder tiers make references to the ranged weapons they use. The Boondollars awarded when a character climbs the echeladder are stored within the fragile but beautiful Ceramic Porkhollow . Also, various aspects of Joey's physique, such as her HP and dexterity, are increased when she advances through the ladder.

There are levels higher than the echeladder known as the " god tiers " that can only be reached by dying on a Quest Bed. Of the post-scratch trolls, only Vriska and Aradia are confirmed to have reached these levels (with Gamzee faking it); however, all eight of the kids have achieved this. Even when they reach god tier, they still keep leveling up.

"Echeladder" is a portmanteau of " echelon " and "ladder".
