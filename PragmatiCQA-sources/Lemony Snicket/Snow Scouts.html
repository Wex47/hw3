Description

As explained by Carmelita Spats , each year the Snow Scouts, led by her Uncle Bruce , trek up the Mortmain Mountains to celebrate False Spring by dancing around the Springpole on Mount Fraught and crowning a False Spring Queen. Carmelita is always given that title thanks to the obvious nepotism in the group coupled with her bratty, temperamental personality.

All the Scouts are suggested to belong to wealthy families, when the Man With a Beard, but No Hair and his partner in crime, the Woman with Hair, but No Beard, are heard plotting to seize the Scouts' family fortunes, to finance future crimes.

History

The Baudelaires met the Snow Scouts in a cave in the Mortmain Mountains that was the former habitat of the Volunteer Feline Detectives . In the back of this particular cave, is a , unknown to the majority of The Snow Scouts.

Later in The Slippery Slope , the Snow Scouts, with the exception of Carmelita Spats , who was adopted by Esmé Squalor , were captured by Count Olaf and taken to work in his submarine in The Grim Grotto .

False Spring Traditions

The Scouts would travel to the Mortmain Mountains , where they would celebrate False Spring on Mount Fraught , at the source of the Stricken Stream . On the way over, they would stop in a cave for Snow Scout Story Time, where one Snow Scout tells a story o the others, and then they all ate marshmallows until they felt sick and go to sleep on a heap of blankets.

Carmelita Spats was always crowned False Spring Queen, and they would celebrate by dancing around a Springpole.

Uniform

The uniform of the Snow Scouts consists of:

  • bright white snowsuits with
    • puffy sleeves, covered in emblems of snowflakes in varying sizes and shapes
    • pink lettering on the back listing each of the words of the Snow Scout Alphabet Pledge
  • white headbands with
    • tiny plastic snowflakes sticking out of the top in all directions
    • the word "Brr!" written in icy script
  • dark round masks covered in tiny holes (much like those worn in the sport of fencing) to protect the Scouts' faces from Snow Gnats
