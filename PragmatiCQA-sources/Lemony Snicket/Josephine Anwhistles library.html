Josephine Anwhistle 's library is a large room located in her home over Lake Lachrymose . It is where she keeps her collection of books on grammar for it is her greatest joy in life.

Description

The library is curved in the shape of an oval. One wall is made up a wooden bookshelf, curved to fit the wall, holds encyclopedias and other books, while metallic polished shelves contain thick books on the history of verbs. Cabinets made of glass shelve adjective manuals that are on display.

The opposite wall is a floor-to-ceiling window, which is "an enormous curved pane of glass." Behind it is an amazing view of Lake Lachrymose , giving off a vibe that feels like one is flying above the lake.

In the middle of the room are comfortable chairs, each with their own footstool.

Adaptational Differences

In the 2004 film , the window has a circular blind that can be pulled aside by a chain. It also has an attached office that belonged to Ike Anwhistle , in which he was investigating fires and held evidence of arsons.

In the Netflix adaptation , the library also holds a map of the lake, along with a large statue of Josephine Anwhistle and a sword, which she tosses through the window to fake her death.

Most importantly, the adaptation features a hidden safe behind one of the shelves, unlocked with the password 09-11-05 (I-K-E). Inside are crackers, whistling music, photos of Josephine doing daring things, and The Incomplete History of Secret Organizations .
