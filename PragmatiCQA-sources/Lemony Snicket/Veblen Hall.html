Veblen Hall is a classy building in The City . It is unclear if the building functions exclusively as an auction house or if it can be used as a venue for other organized events such as business meetings or weddings.

Description

Veblen Hall has won the prestigious Door Prize, an award given each year to the best-constructed opening in The City . The award-winning opening door has polished wooden planks, exquisite brass hinges and a gorgeous, shiny doorknob made of the world's second-finest crystal. The door stands above a flight of stairs.

Immediately inside, past the door, is the hall with a very high ceiling, very shiny floor, and massive window that had won first runner-up for the Window Prize the year before the Baudelaire Fire .

The hall is two blocks away from the Baudelaire Mansion .

The In Auction is held at there, where the innest things are auctioned off, with all the money going to Esmé Squalor . During this event, three huge banners are hung from the ceiling, one with the word "In" written on it, the other with the word "Auction," and third with a huge portrait of the auctioneer. At least two-hundred people, mostly rich, seem to attend.

Trivia

  • Veblen Hall is likely named after Thorstein Veblen , an American economist and author of The Theory of Leisure Class , a critique of capitalism.
