Description

Duncan , Isadora , and Quigley Quagmire were heirs to the sapphires. It is unknown how the Quagmire parents acquired the relics, but it is known that the jewels are what paid for the Quagmire Mansion .

The Quagmire Sapphires are similar to the Baudelaire fortune, because it's the inheritance that the triplets will receive when they come of age.

They also attracted the attention of Count Olaf . He strongly desired the Quagmire Sapphires and as is common of many of his other endeavors. Olaf most likely went about getting them by murdering Mr. and Mrs. Quagmire . However, it is unknown whether or not he was responsible for the Quagmire fire. In the Netflix adaptation, a woman uses a laser device to burn down the mansion. This could be Count Olaf in his disguise as Shirley by the pink clothing.

After the death of the Quagmire parents, the sapphires started to be in charge of Esmé Squalor . Why Olaf kept on chasing them after that is unknown.

At the end of The Austere Academy , Olaf set his sights on using the Quagmire triplets to gain access to the sapphires. He kidnapped Duncan and Isadora and hid them in a cage at the bottom of an elevator shaft, then later in a statue of a red herring, in The Ersatz Elevator . They were then hidden in the Village of Fowl Devotees' Fowl Fountain throughout the course of The Vile Village . With the help of the Baudelaire children , the Quagmires were eventually able to escape. Afterward, Olaf's interest in the sapphires seemed to wane, and the current status of the gems is currently unknown.
