The 1989 Formula One season was the 40th season of the FIA Formula One World Championship. It began on March 26 at the Brazilian Grand Prix and ended on November 5 at the Australian Grand Prix .

Alain Prost ( McLaren - Honda ) won the Drivers' Championship at the controversial Japanese Grand Prix , which was noted for an incident which Prost and his team-mate Ayrton Senna collided at low speed in the final chicane.

McLaren dominated another season this year, but could only manage to win 10 races.

Mid-season Changes

Driver changes

  • For Round 2 ( San Marino ):
    • AGS : Philippe Streiff (testing accident) → Gabriele Tarquini
  • For Round 3 ( Monaco ):
    • Ferrari : Gerhard Berger (crash injury) → none (entry withdrawn)
  • For Round 7 ( France ):
    • Tyrrell : Michele Alboreto (sponsorship refusal) → Jean Alesi
    • Arrows : Derek Warwick (off-track injury) → Martin Donnelly
    • Benetton : Johnny Herbert (dropped/recovery from crash) → Emanuele Pirro
    • Lola : Yannick Dalmas (Legionnaires' disease) → Éric Bernard
  • For Round 8 ( Britain ):
    • Arrows : Martin Donnelly → Derek Warwick (recovered)
    • AGS : Joachim Winkelhock (moved to touring cars) → Yannick Dalmas
  • For Round 9 ( Germany ):
    • Lola : Éric Bernard (F3000 commitments) → Michele Alboreto
  • For Round 11 ( Belgium ):
    • Tyrrell : Jean Alesi (F3000 commitments) → Johnny Herbert
    • Rial : Volker Weidler (departed from team) → Pierre-Henri Raphanel
  • For Round 12 ( Italy ):
    • Tyrrell : Johnny Herbert → Jean Alesi (seat return)
    • EuroBrun : Gregor Foitek (departed from team) → Oscar Larrauri
  • For Round 13 ( Portugal ):
    • Tyrrell : Jean Alesi (F3000 commitments) → Johnny Herbert
    • Onyx : Bertrand Gachot (sacked) → JJ Lehto
  • For Round 14 ( Spain ):
    • Tyrrell : Johnny Herbert → Jean Alesi (seat return)
    • Ferrari : Nigel Mansell → none (driver suspended, Ferrari entered only one car)
    • Rial : Christian Danner (replaced) → Gregor Foitek
  • For Round 15 ( Japan ):
    • Minardi : Pierluigi Martini → Paolo Barilla (race opportunity)
    • Rial : Gregor Foitek (departed from team after heavy crash) → Bertrand Gachot
  • For Round 16 ( Australia ):
    • Minardi : Paolo Barilla → Pierluigi Martini (seat return)
