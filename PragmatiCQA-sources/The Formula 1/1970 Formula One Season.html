The 1970 Formula One Season was the 21st season of the Drivers' World Championship and the 13th season of the Constructors' Championship.

It began on March 19th 1970 in South Africa and ended on October 25th in Mexico , and consisted of 13 races.

The Drivers' World Championship was won by Jochen Rindt of Lotus , who became the first (and so far only) posthumous winner of the championship, when he was killed during practice for the Italian Grand Prix . He finished five points ahead of Jacky Ickx of Ferrari . Ickx's teammate, Clay Regazzoni was third.

The Constructors' Championship was won by Lotus - Ford , seven points ahead of Ferrari , and 11 ahead of March , a brand new constructor.

In addition to Rindt, drivers Bruce McLaren (testing accident at Goodwood) and Piers Courage (during the Dutch Grand Prix ) were killed this season, giving this year the sobriquet "The Year of Death" .
