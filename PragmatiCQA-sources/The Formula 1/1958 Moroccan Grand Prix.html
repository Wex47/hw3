The 1958 Moroccan Grand Prix was the final race of the 1958 Formula One season . It was the 13th Moroccan Grand Prix and the second on the Ain-Diab layout.

Mike Hawthorn took home his fourth pole position, while Stirling Moss won his fourth race of the year, but it wouldn't be enough as Hawthorn would finish in second to take home his first and only championship.

The race though would be marked in tragedy when Stuart Lewis-Evans ' engine seized and flew him off the track at high speed, with the car bursting into flames. He was airlifted back to the UK, but would die from his burns six days later.

Background

Heading into the final round of the championship, Mike Hawthorn held a eight point lead heading into the final race at Morocco which was a non-championship race in the previous season. Robert La Caze, a F2 driver, became the first person from Africa to compete in a Formula One race. In the constructors championship, Vanwall held a six point lead from Ferrari . But despite the margin, the dropped score rule meant that Vanwall would win the first constructors championship.

Title Permutations

Drivers' Championship

Mike Hawthorn enters the race with a 8-point lead over Stirling Moss with only one race left, and is likely to secure the title.

The possible permutations are:

  • For Hawthorn to win the Championship:
    • Second place or better.
  • For Moss to win the Championship:
    • Moss needs to win and get fastest lap and hope that Hawthorn finishes 3rd or lower.
