The 1996 Formula One season was the 47th season of the FIA Formula One World Championship. It began on March 10th at the Australian GP in Melbourne , and finished on October 13th at the Japanese GP at Suzuka after sixteen races. The World Champion was Damon Hill , of Williams - Renault , who won the constructors' title.

Changes from last season

Team changes

  • Pacific Grand Prix withdrew from Formula One.

Driver changes

  • Michael Schumacher : Benetton → Ferrari
  • Eddie Irvine : Jordan → Ferrari
  • Jean Alesi and Gerhard Berger : Ferrari → Benetton
  • David Coulthard : Williams → McLaren
  • Pedro Diniz : Forti → Ligier
  • Martin Brundle : Ligier → Jordan
  • Johnny Herbert : Benetton → Sauber
  • Jos Verstappen : Simtek → Footwork
  • Luca Badoer : Minardi → Forti

Mid-season Changes

Team changes

  • Forti were declared bankrupt after the British Grand Prix, taking no further part in the championship.

Driver changes

  • Only a single mid-season driver change happened in the season. Tarso Marques filled in Fisichella's seat for the Brazilian Grand Prix . However, after the British Grand Prix , Fisichella would be replaced by Giovanni Lavaggi for the remainder of the season.
