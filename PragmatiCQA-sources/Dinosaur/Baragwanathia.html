Baragwanathia is a genus of extinct plants of the division Lycopodiophyta of Late Silurian to Early Devonian age (427 to 393 million years ago), fossils of which have been found in Australia, Canada, China and Czechia. The name derives from William Baragwanath who discovered the first specimens of the type species, Baragwanathia longifolia at Victoria, Australia.
