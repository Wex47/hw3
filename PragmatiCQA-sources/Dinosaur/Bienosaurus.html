Bienosaurus
Order: † Saurischia
Suborder: † Thyreophora
Family: † Scelidosauridae
Etymology: Bien's lizard
Historical period: Early Jurassic
Habitat: China
Diet: Herbivorous

Bienosaurus is a scelidosaurid thyreophoran from early Jurassic China, as well as being one of the most obscure dinosaurs to the general public. The exact size of the animal is currently unknown due to a lack of fossil remains.

In the Media

Bienosaurus' so far only appearance in a fictional story is a guest performal/cameo appearance in Dinosaurs: Bite of the Albertosaurus. He is seen next to the sepulchre of Michael Chrichton (who he is named after), author of the Jurassic Park Novelization, crying over his death, as well repining about not appearing in his books.
