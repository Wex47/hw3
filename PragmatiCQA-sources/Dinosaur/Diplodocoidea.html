Diplodocoidea is a superfamily of sauropod dinosaurs , which included some of the longest animals of all time, including slender giants like Supersaurus , Diplodocus , Apatosaurus , and Amphicoelias . Most had very long necks and long, whip-like tails; however, one family (the dicraeosaurids) are the only known sauropods to have re-evolved a short neck, presumably an adaptation for feeding low to the ground. This adaptation was taken to the extreme in the highly specialized sauropod Brachytrachelopan . A study of snout shape and dental microwear in diplodocoids showed that the square snouts, large proportion of pits, and fine subparallel scratches in Apatosaurus , Diplodocus , Nigersaurus , and Rebbachisaurus suggest ground-height nonselective browsing; the narrow snouts of Dicraeosaurus , Suuwassea , and Tornieria and the coarse scratches and gouges on the teeth of Dicraeosaurus suggest mid-height selective browsing in those taxa.[1] This taxon is also noteworthy because diplodocoid sauropods had the highest tooth replacement rates of any vertebrates, as exemplified by Nigersaurus , which had new teeth erupting every 30 days.
