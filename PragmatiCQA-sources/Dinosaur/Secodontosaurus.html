Secodontosaurus was a pelycosaur synapsid from early Permian Texas, USA, and possibly other North American states.

Description

Secodontosaurus belongs to the Sphenacodontidae, and bears the appearance well. It has a large fleshy sail-back, and an elongated snout full of sharp teeth. Its thought that this animal might've waddled around, and gotten its teeth into whatever it found. Theories also suggest it might've lived a more amphibian-like life and fed on aquatic animals hence the elongated snout.

Discovery and classification

Evolutionary relationship to Dimetrodon

It is known that both are synapsids, and belong in the eupelycosaur group. They might've had similar feeding habits
