Pat E. Johnson is an American actor and martial artist. He is a ninth degree black belt in the art of American Tang Soo Do. He is the president of the National Tang Soo Do Congress, which was created by Chuck Norris. In 1984, he held a minor role in The Karate Kid film trilogy as a referee.

Biography

Not known what day and month Johnson was born. Johnson was born in 1939 in Niagara Falls, New York. He began training in traditional Korean Tang Soo Do Moo Duk Kwan in 1963, while stationed in South Korea as a chaplain in the U.S. Army. While under the tutelage of a Korean master named Kang Lo Hee, Johnson earned his black belt in just thirteen months. After his army service ended, Johnson met and formed an association with Tang Soo Do instructor Chuck Norris. Johnson soon rose to the rank of chief instructor at Norris' school in Sherman Oaks, California in 1968. That same year, he formulated the penalty-point system still used by karate tournaments.

From 1968 to 1973, Johnson was captain of the undefeated Chuck Norris black belt competition team, which won 33 consecutive national and international titles. In 1971, he became the National Tang Soo Do Champion. In both 1975 and 1976, Johnson was awarded the prestigious Golden Fist Award for best karate referee in the United States.

In 1973, Norris founded the National Tang Soo Do Congress (NTC), and named Johnson as executive vice president and chief of instruction. In 1979, Norris disbanded the NTC and formed the United Fighting Arts Federation (UFAF), again naming Johnson as executive vice president.

In 1980, Johnson had a small supporting role in the feature film The Little Dragons (later known as The Karate Kids U.S.A. ). In the film, Johnson played the karate instructor to a pair of young brothers (portrayed by Chris and Pat Petersen) who use their martial arts skills to foil a kidnapping plot.

In 1984, Johnson served as Stunt Coordinator on The Karate Kid . Johnson also featured in the movie as the chief referee in the All Valley Karate Tournament. He was one of only four cast members who knew any Martial Arts before shooting began. The others being Chad McQueen ( Dutch) , Ron Thomas ( Bobby ), and Darryl Vidal , who was featured in the Semi Final against Johnny Lawrence . Vidal invented the infamous Crane Kick that sees Daniel win the tournament .

In 1986, Johnson was promoted to ninth-degree black belt. That same year after a difference of opinion with Norris, he would leave the UFAF and reform the NTC

Filmography

Johnson was known for outside of The Karate Kid trilogy such as Enter the Dragon, The Little Dragon, The Big Brawl, Golden Needles, Force: 5, The Ultimate Warrior, I Am Steve McQueen, and Cruise Missile.

Character

The Karate Kid

Pat is present at the 1984 All Valley Under-18 Karate Tournament during sign ins. He later breaks up a near fight between Dutch and Daniel LaRusso in the locker room. Pat later officiates many of the matches throughout the tournament. He is firm and fair in his officiating, threatening to remove a point from Daniel in his initial bout for running out of the ring, and disqualifies Bobby Brown in the semi-finals when he lands an illegal kick on Daniel.

The Karate Kid Part II

Pat appears in the aftermath of the 1984 All-Valley Under 18 Karate Tournament, along with the ring announcer, Bruce Malmuth. Both of them offer praise to Daniel for the Crane Kick that he lands on Johnny to win the tournament.

The Karate Kid Part III

Pat leaves a message on Cobra Kai's answering, informing Kreese that because Cobra Kai has failed to pay the dues that each dojo owes to the All Valley Karate Tournament, that Cobra Kai was being dropped from the rolls of eligible dojos. Pat officiates Mike Barnes' match in the semi-finals that puts him into the finals against Daniel LaRusso. He also officiates the final match between Daniel LaRusso against Barnes. He constantly is warning Mike Barnes for his actions and continues to deduct points from Barnes for his flagrant rule violations. As a member of the All Valley Karate Tournament committee, he would have had a hand in implementing the lifetime ban on Cobra Kai Dojo for the actions at the 1985 Tournament by Terry Silver, John Kreese, and Mike Barnes.

Trivia

In the Karate Kid Special Edition DVD, Pat Johnson narrates and is the focus of the featurette BEYOND THE FORM, where he gives the entire history of the choreography that went into the Karate Kid.
