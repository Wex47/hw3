Lieutenant Jack Pierce is a minor character in The Karate Kid franchise.

He was deceased as of the events of the film, but he was an important character in The Next Karate Kid , as he was mentioned numerous times.

Biography

Jack Pierce was an officer with the 442nd Infantry Regiment during the second world war. Pierce was very close friend to one of his subordinates, a Staff Sergeant known as Mr. Miyagi . Over their time in the unit, Pierce learned karate from Miyagi. After the war, Pierce and Miyagi stayed in touch, although Miyagi lived in Los Angeles and Pierce lived in Boston.

Over the years, Pierce married a woman named Louisa and the two had one child named Susan . Susan eventually married and had a daughter named Julie . Julie and Jack were very close and Jack taught Julie the same style of karate that he had learned from Mr. Miyagi all those years ago. However, unfortunately, Jack passed away. Some time later, Susan and her husband died in a car crash, leaving Louisa to watch over Julie.

The Next Karate Kid

In 1994, Miyagi goes to Boston to attend a commendation for Japanese-American soldiers, who fought in the 442nd Regimental Combat Team during World War II. He meets Louisa and is invited to her home where they catch up on old times and war stories.

Mr. Miyagi realizes that Jack and Louisa's granddaughter, Julie, is struggling after the death of her parents. Miyagi invites Louisa to stay at his house in Los Angeles to enjoy peace and quiet tending his garden while he stays in Boston as Julie's caretaker. Miyagi eventually learns that Julie learned karate from her grandfather, and he teaches her in karate so she can find peace and balance in her life.
