Suzanne Lockridge (born October 17, 1950) is an American costume designer and co-founder of Irene Corey Design Associates . She is most notable for her work helping to design and construct the costumes for Barney and Friends.

She discovered her love for sewing at a young age when she spent her vacation time learning to sew from her grandmother, Vallie Lockridge ( Irene Corey's mother). Later on she attributed her artistic development to the mentorship she received from her aunt and grandmother

Career

Suzanne graduated from the University of Texas at Austin in 1979 . After spending some time in freelance painting , her aunt, Irene Corey approached her wanting to know if she was interested in starting a costume design shop together. Each contributed $1000 to the business and Irene Corey Design Associates was born.

Suzanne has designed and constructed full-body mascot costumes for many high profile clients including: Hershey's, Paramount Pictures, Chick-Fil-A, Fire Fox and HP, among others.

After Irene Corey retired in 1995, Suzanne continued running Irene Corey Design Associates until her own retirement in 2015.
