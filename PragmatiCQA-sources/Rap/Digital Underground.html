Digital Underground was a Rap group from Oakland, California. Their members included Shock G, Money-B, DJ Nu-Stylez, Cleetis Mack, 2Fly Eli, DJ Fuze, Chopmaster J, Kent Racker, Nzazi Muhammed, Schmoovy Schmoov, rAN/D (Randy Brooks) Michael Webster Pee Wee, Aaron Parsons, Saafir, Esinchill, BINC and Tupac Shakur . The group appeared in "Nothing but Trouble" with Dan Akroyd. Digital Underground is also notable for launching the career of member Tupac Shakur, whom died prematurely.
