Super God Hand (スーパーゴッドハンド, Sūpā Goddo Hando ) is a catch hissatsu technique.

Users

Anime only

  • Endou Mamoru

Info

Back at Tonegawa Tousen , Endou practiced with Sakanoue on this technique. They used a wrecking ball to swing at Endou, and Endou tried to used this new technique to stop it, however he didn't manage to complete it, and as a result gets hit by the ball.

During the match with Muteki no Giant , Endou completed and used this technique, trying to stop Clario Orvan 's Diamond Ray . However, he failed to stop it, making the score 1-0 for Spain. When he later faced Diamond Ray again, Endou used the technique again, but this time, by channeling his inner feelings about the former match between Raimon and Barcelona Orb , the technique evolved into Diamond Hand , stopping Diamond Ray.

Usage

The user turns their body slightly, with their right hand clutched into fist and covered in a golden aura. The user turns around and raises their right hand in the air, summoning a large fist, before it opens up into yellow hand. The user crouches, their left hand clutching their right forearm, with the yellow hand pulled down closer to the user's right hand. As yellow hand is pulled down, it shrinks smaller and reverts back into a fist. The user swings their right arm back, with a trail of yellow aura and lightning following the fist. The user then thrusts their hand forward, summoning a small yellow hand.

Trivia

  • In Sakanoue's explanation of Super God Hand, this technique reduced the radius and size of the hand to maximize catching power. It can be considered an upgrade to God Hand , but due to the narrow radius of the hand, this technique and its evolution, Diamond Hand, may only be used to stop shoots that travel in a straight line, like Clario's normal Diamond Ray.
  • There is no typography when this move was used due to it evolving into Diamond Hand the second time Endou used it.
  • In the Cantonese dub, the name was changed to "超級黄金神掌", using the Mandarin dub of saying God Hand, "黄金神掌" (Golden God Palm) instead of "上帝之手” (God Hand).
