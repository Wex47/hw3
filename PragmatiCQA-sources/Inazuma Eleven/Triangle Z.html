Info

Original series

Triangle Z was first used by the Kidokawa Seishuu 's strikers, the Mukata triplets. This technique is the third to break through God Hand , even with Endou using both of his hands. The technique was later stopped by a double-handed God Hand with the help of Kurimatsu and Kabeyama , which urged Endou to make a better technique .

Triangle Z was used again in the match between Inazuma Japan and Neo Japan , in its 改 form, and was used by the players of Neo Japan. However, the shoot lost its power by the air shift created from Tobitaka 's incomplete Shinkuuma , and was easily caught by Tachimukai .

Ares no Tenbin series

Triangle Z was used in the match between Seishou Gakuen and Kidokawa Seishuu . The Mukata triplets used the technique from a standing position to create an Override technique with Gouenji 's Fire Tornado , creating Bakunetsu Storm . This technique scored Kidokawa Seishuu's first goal. Later in the match, they used it again, though this time as a normal shot, as Gouenji drew the defenders to him, to create space for the triplets. They shot it towards Gouenji, faking another Override, but due to an effect on the ball, it changed direction to the goal, scoring Kidokawa Seishuu's second goal.

Obtainment

Inazuma Eleven 3: Sekai e no Chousen!!

The manual for Triangle Z can be bought for 10000 Nekketsu Points at Cotarl's Hissatsu store.

Usage

In the original anime series, the first user kicks the ball up in the air. The second user kicks the ball back using a bicycle kick. The third user jumps on top of the first user's shoulders and jumps up in the air, kicking the ball towards the goal. The third user quickly comes down, with the first two users extending their hands out, holding the third user up, posing, and creating a yellow triangle behind them.

In Inazuma Eleven Ares no Tenbin , there are two variation of Triangle Z. In the first variation, three users cross their arms, with their backs facing the ball. All three users turn around and kicks the ball at the same time, creating a large yellow triangle that surrounding them. The ball flies into the air, leaving a trail of yellow triangles behind it.

The second variation, three users run forward in a triangle formation. One user kicks the ball in the air. The three users then kneels down on their knees before jumping up into the air. The first user kicks the ball up higher in the air. The second user follows up and kicks it higher. The third user catches up and kicks it further up, repeating this several times, lighting the ball in a yellow aura. The three users then back flips until they catch up with the ball. They all perform a back kick at the same time, creating a triangle that surrounds the ball. The shot flies toward the goal with a trail of yellow triangles.
