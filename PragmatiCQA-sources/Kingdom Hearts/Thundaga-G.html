For general information and history on gummi blocks, see gummi block .

Gummi blocks in Kingdom Hearts and the Final Mix version are items used to construct gummi ships . They are used by both the player character, and the Heartless , to travel through interspace . The more blocks the player has, the more powerful the ship they can build in the gummi garage , as well as construct specific ship blueprints they collected.

The most common blocks are acquired by destroying objects and enemy ships while traveling to worlds, and picking up the remains. The majority can also be purchased at Cid 's Gummi Shop in Traverse Town , moreso when he fully expands his stock. Cid's shop is unlocked after bringing two Navi-G Pieces to him, from which he constructs a Navi-Gummi , letting Sora , Donald , and Goofy 's ship travel through warp holes. Although technically a gummi block, the Warp-G is not actually placeable.

The more obscure and powerful blocks on the other hand can only be acquired through finding them in treasure chests (the normal version of the game also had a lot of common blocks, but Final Mix replaced all of these), or as a reward for completing an optional objective or minigame. Returning all 99 puppies to Pongo and Perdita also grants a 100% complete set of all blocks, making collecting them otherwise obsolete. Final Mix changed this set to only contain one of the basic blocks, instead of a maximum amount of everything.

Final Mix also included the addition of gummi ship missions , which reward new design-type gummi blocks, as well as new blueprints.

Mechanics

Each gummi block has an effect on the ship it's placed on. The core of a ship is the cockpit, of which it can have only one, and which will have a powerful . Together with an engine gummi, which affect flight speed, they allow ships to fly through interspace. The most common blocks, Protect-G, Shell-G, and Dispel-G all serve to add armor (although almost all blocks will add some degree of armor), act as filler, and aid in the basic shaping of each ship, moreso thanks to their large shape variety. The wing gummies affect a ship's handling and the weapon gummies serve as the damage output.

The aforementioned design-type blocks have no actual function, unlike the special-type blocks, which have a selection of more esoteric functions, like shielding, a larger item grab range, radar, and a boost option.

System upgrades for the gummi garage are required for a handful of ship functions, such as 'Spray' and 'Palette' for coloring blocks (the latter simply adds a larger variety), SYS. UP1 and 2 for a large assembly space, and COM. LV1, 2, and 3 for a higher block and power limit.

Gummi blocks

See also: Kingdom Hearts item locations

The original version of Kingdom Hearts often placed relatively worthless Shell-G and Dispel-G blocks in chests, whereas Final Mix removed these. Instead it placed various weapon, engine, special, and a few cockpit gummis, to more accurately reflect the various blocks you would make use of. Due to this Final Mix also removed all the better quality blocks from Cid's Gummi Shop.

Cockpits

All cockpits have a size of 2 by 2 by 2 (2x2x2) blocks, a base armor level of 200, and a limit (and requirement) of one per ship. As their only change is the innate ability of each cockpit it is recommended to always equip the best one available.

Weapons

Although the Comet-G, Meteor-G, and Ultima-G weapons are a series of increasingly stronger homing lasers, the Thunder-G weapons are each unique. The basic Thunder-G fires straight-ahead, whereas the Thundara-G fires in an outward V-shaped pattern, and it can also be placed on its side to fire both up and down. The Thundaga-G cannon fires a slower, but powerful missile. The Thunder-G series also does not consume Power, unlike the other three (except for the Thundaga-G, whose consumption is a very minor 0.1).

Design blocks

Section incomplete

These unique blocks were introduced in Final Mix and can only be found by completing gummi missions. Although their main purpose is to visually make your ship stand out more, they do have some minor armor levels. The Angel-G, Dark-G, and Drill-G gummis were also used in Kingdom Hearts II and do actually have a function in the first game. The two wings have a handling level of respectively 125 and 130, and the Drill-G has a power level of 35 , letting you use it to ram enemies. None of them also have collision detection. The Rock-G, Paper-G, and Scissors-G gummis also come in left and right variations.
