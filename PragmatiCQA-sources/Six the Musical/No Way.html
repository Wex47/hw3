Theme

The song recounts how Catherine was loyal to Henry throughout their entire marriage, even while she knew he was actively cheating on her. She remained silent about his acts and put up with his behaviour. It also discusses how Catherine fought for years against the annulment, bringing up Henry's excuses for divorcing her and how Catherine knelt in front of Henry at the Legatine Court on 21 June 1529, explaining every way she had been a good wife to him. She declares that because Henry married her, she will be queen until the end of her life.

Trivia

  • At the BroadwayCon panel, Toby Marlow and Lucy Moss revealed that No Way was the first song they had written together for the show.
    • Toby also stated that this song has gone through more revisions than any other song in the show.
  • The lines, "And even though you've had your fun/Running around with some pretty young thing/And even though you've had one son/With someone who don't own a wedding ring" are about Henry's affair with Bessie Blount and their illegitimate son, Henry FitzRoy .
  • The lines, "So you read a Bible verse that I'm cursed/'Cause I was your brother's wife/You say it's a pity 'cause quoting Leviticus/I'll end up kiddy-less all my life" refer to the Bible passage Leviticus 20:21, which reads, "If a man marries his brother's wife, it is an act of impurity; he has dishonored his brother. They will be childless". Catherine had previously been married to Henry's brother, Arthur Tudor , before he died.
  • In live performances, the actress playing Catherine usually says, "Oh, you don't remember?" instead of "Aw, hi baby".
  • In live performances, the actress playing Catherine says, "Give it up for Maria on the drums!" during the drum solo.
