" Six " is the ninth and final song in Six the Musical . It reimagines what the queens' lives would be like if they had not married King Henry VIII .

Trivia

  • Anne Boleyn's verse yet again references Greensleeves , an English folk song commonly rumoured to have been written by Henry VIII for Anne. It is commonly referenced at other points in the show, usually by Anne herself.
  • The "Shakesy-P" referenced by Anne during her verse refers to William Shakespeare , a famous English poet and playwright.
    • Although Anne may have been alive when Shakespeare was born if she wasn't executed, she likely wouldn't have lived long enough to work with him.
    • This is also partially a reference to Shakespeare's play Henry VIII , in which Anne appears.
  • In Jane Seymour's verse, she references the von Trapp family ("You could perhaps call us/The Tudor von Trapps") and the Rolling Stones ("We're called the Royalling Stones").
    • Additionally, Jane's song Heart of Stone shares a title with one of the Rolling Stones' songs, and their song Lady Jane was named after her.
