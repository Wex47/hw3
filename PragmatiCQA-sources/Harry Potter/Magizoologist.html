A Magizoologist was a wizard or witch who studied magical creatures professionally (a field known as magizoology ); though they may also have been referred to as wizarding naturalists. In certain cases, a magizoologist's obligations may have not been limited to merely observing and taking care of magical creatures, as they may even have been immersed in sanctioned breeding, and even actively tracking and rescuing them from less-principled parties. A wizard or witch may not have needed to have graduated from a Wizarding school to become a Magizoologist, as Newt Scamander was expelled from Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry , and still managed to become a highly renowned Magizoologist in his adult life.

History

The Scamander family had many members that have worked as magizoologists. One of the most famous magizoologists was Newt Scamander . He was the author of Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them . Early in life, Scamander developed an interest in magical creatures that was influenced by his mother 's breeding of hippogriffs . Newt's grandson Rolf and his wife Luna were considered wizarding naturalists.

A Care of Magical Creatures was a class taught at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry for students who wish to learn about magical beasts. Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them was a required textbook and the professor of the subject could be counted as an magizoologist, since they were educating others about creatures.

A Dragonologist was a magizoologist who specialised in the study and handling of dragons .
