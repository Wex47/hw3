This article covers information about the sixth year at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry .

A student in their sixth year at Hogwarts was called a sixth-year (with a hyphen).

This was the first year in which students advanced to N.E.W.T. -level classes, based on the individuals' O.W.L. scores for certain subjects, and depending on whether or not enough students wanted to take the specialised classes offered at that level.

During the sixth year

At Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry , sixth-years were typically 16 to 17 years of age.

The sixth year was the year in which students advanced to N.E.W.T. -level classes. Based off of their O.W.L. scores, and depending on the minimum requirements of the Professor teaching the subject at that time, students were allowed to take any number of classes as long as they met the requirements. Neither the core classes nor the elective courses were available to any student who did not meet said requirements. While students did have the opportunity to choose whether they wished to continue in particular subjects, those who began studying N.E.W.T.-level subjects in their sixth year are expected to carry on with the subject into the seventh year , and sit the N.E.W.T. exam in that subject.

Sixth-year students were initially excited to have more free time, but this extra time was intended to help them study and do homework, as many, if not all, teachers assigned more homework and gave more difficult lessons in their N.E.W.T.-level classes.

Students in the sixth year could also elect to take part in Apparition lessons for a fee of twelve Galleons . Specialised classes such as Alchemy become available if there was sufficient demand.

Nonverbal spells were introduced to sixth-year students. They were expected to be used in every lessons that required spell -casting, which could prove troublesome if students possessed dogwood wands, which refused to perform nonverbal spells.
