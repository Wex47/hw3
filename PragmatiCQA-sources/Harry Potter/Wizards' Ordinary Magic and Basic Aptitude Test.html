The Wizards' Ordinary Magic and Basic Aptitude Test (or W.O.M.B.A.T. ) was a series of tests administered by the Wizarding Examinations Authority , concerning a variety of subjects. The test scores from the W.O.M.B.A.T.s, like the O.W.L.s and N.E.W.T.s , were recognised and accepted as a valid form of credentials for witches and wizards wanting to apply for a job. Unlike the two latter examinations, however, the W.O.M.B.A.T.s consisted exclusively of written papers. Therefore, W.O.M.B.A.T.s could in theory be sat by Muggles as well as wizards and witches , although the Wizarding Examinations Authority did not believe that any Muggle would have the degree of inner knowledge required to pass it.

The test

The point of the W.O.M.B.A.T.s was to test the worth of an individual in order to discern whether or not the sitter were able to exist safely and effectively within the magical world. A W.O.M.B.A.T. usually took twenty-five minutes to complete, although more advanced levels (such as Grade 3) took more time; thirty-five minutes. The Ministry would then spend "a few days" going over and marking each test, whereupon an owl was sent to deliver the results to the people that took the test; as well as provide feedback on how well they did and what their prospects were relative to the achieved test scores. The results were delivered in the form of a framed certificate in the case of a passing grade, or a letter if it is a failing grade.

Behind the scenes

  • The W.O.M.B.A.T. was a series of tests presented on J. K. Rowling's official site , prior to its 2012 redesign. Presented partially as a Harry Potter trivia test, there is nevertheless additional information that can be gleaned from the questions.
  • Amusingly, Neil Murray, the husband of J. K. Rowling, took the first W.O.M.B.A.T. and only achieved a score of Acceptable, despite being in the same room as his wife when she was composing the quiz and listening to her read the answers out loud. This discouraged him from attempting either of the following two tests.
  • Interestingly, even though the W.O.M.B.A.T. are ministered by the Wizarding Examination Authorities and are graded the same way that the O.W.L. and N.E.W.T.-examination are, they were never mentioned in the original Harry Potter books and hence seemingly not handed out to Hogwarts students, even as an optional test. As such, considering these tests were so significant in terms of magical education to merit the production of its own separate revision guide and given how said Ministry division even encouraged those that failed the tests to undergo the coursework offered by Kwikspell Company before trying again, these tests were presumably - from a purely in-universe perspective - a way for people to to renew their credentials later in life if they achieved bad marks on their school exams.
  • It is also interesting to note how, given that the series of character and aptitude tests in question were solely written exams aimed at establishing and assessing the taker's ability to function in the magical community, and considering the results were accepted as valid credentials yet seemingly not taken by Hogwarts students, not only could this test hypothetically serve as a citizenship test for immigrating wizards moving to the UK, but young witches and wizards that did not receive a Hogwarts education might also be given these tests as an alternative to the O.W.L.s and N.E.W:T.s examinations in the event that they were home schooled. Indeed; as it would be unreasonable to expect a child tutored by their parents to have the same level of magical dexterity as those receiving a more thorough education from learned experts at a wizarding school, as the W.O.M.B.A.T.s would also allow the Ministry of Magic to determine whether their instruction in magic was sufficiently well-rounded to allow them to become a functioning member of the wizarding community of Great Britain without holding them to the exact same standard as a Hogwarts student, and encourage them to try again if they failed the first time.
