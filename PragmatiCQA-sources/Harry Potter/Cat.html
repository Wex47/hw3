Description

Cats were sometimes kept as pets of witches and wizards . Students at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry were allowed to bring cats, among owls and toads , as their permitted pet at the school. The Magical Menagerie in Diagon Alley had many cats for sale. Among the students known to have brought cats were Hermione Granger and Millicent Bulstrode .

Rubeus Hagrid remarked to Harry Potter on 31 July 1991 that he disliked cats, as they made him sneeze, implying he was allergic to them.

In 1992 , Hermione accidentally partly transformed herself into a cat with a botched Polyjuice Potion , having inadvertently added a hair from Bulstrode's cat , believing the hair belonged to its owner . As the potion was not intended for cross-species transformations , this resulted in Hermione spending the rest of her Christmas holidays in the school's Hospital wing to be returned to normal.

Cats were also able to interbreed with Kneazles , and generally had up to eight kittens in every litter. Some wizards and witches made a living by breeding Kneazles or part-Kneazles. Arabella Figg , a Squib , made her living in this manner.

It was a possible corporeal form of the Patronus Charm . Minerva McGonagall 's Patronus was a cat. There were also Animagi wizards and witches who could assume the form of a cat, notably Minerva McGonagall.

They could see or detect Dementors , as Mr Tibbles was able to inform his owner Arabella Figg of the Dementors present in Little Whinging on the 2 August , 1995 .

In addition to having a persian cat Patronus, Dolores Umbridge seemed to have a fondness for kittens (baby or young cats). She owned a set of ornamental plates featuring kittens as well as pink notes with pictures of kittens in the corners, and attached one with the note "To be punished" to a wanted poster of Harry Potter labelling him as " Undesirable Number One". However, she found the real ones to be too much of a hassle to take care of, so she only focused on cat-themed decorations.

Behind the scenes

  • In Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows , Aberforth Dumbledore lied to some Death Eaters in Hogsmeade about wanting to put his cat out to break curfew, when in reality he was concealing the fact that he had just let the trio enter his pub for protection. It is unknown if Aberforth actually owned a pet cat or not.
  • In the film adaptation of Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets , Vernon Dursley blamed the loud thumping noises made by Dobby on a non-existent family cat. Additionally in this film, the Weasley family 's fireplace featured a motif of an orange cat.
  • In Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery , Jacob's sibling (the player) can own a pet cat of customisable appearance, with several cat varieties available for purchase in the game. However, the player has to first purchase the cat with the default orange look before being able to select other varieties.
