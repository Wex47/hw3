Creatures in the wizarding world were comprised of both magical and non-magical entities, which were split by the British Ministry of Magic into three classifications: Being , Beast , or Spirit . The current definition for these categories was laid down by Minister for Magic Grogan Stump in 1811 .

Ministry rating

The Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures classified magical creatures by perceived dangerousness on a scale from one (X) to five (XXXXX):

Classification Description Examples
X Boring Flobberworm , Horklump
XX Harmless / might be domesticated Gnome , Bowtruckle , Ghoul
XXX Competent wizard should cope Pixie , Doxy , Hippogriff
XXXX Dangerous / required specialist knowledge / skilled wizard might handle Erumpent , Erkling , Unicorn
XXXXX Known wizard killer / impossible to train or domesticate Dragon , Acromantula , Werewolf

All known beasts , beings and spirits fell into these categories.

Mythological creatures

There were several creatures that the Lovegood family believed in the existence of, which might be mythological such as the Crumple-Horned Snorkack . Stories of these creatures had been known to appear in the Lovegoods' magazine, The Quibbler .

Behind the scenes

  • Some beasts , such as ones documented by Newton Scamander : the Swooping Evil and Zouwu , have an unknown rating.
  • Similarly, the rating for Non-beings such as the Dementor or Boggart , is unknown.
