Frank Darabont (b. 28 January 1959) is a writer and director. He wrote eight scripts for The Young Indiana Jones Chronicles (seven of which were filmed), and wrote a rejected script for Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull entitled Indiana Jones and the City of the Gods . He is well-known for writing and directing three films based on Stephen King novels: The Shawshank Redemption , The Green Mile and The Mist . The Green Mile is structured similar to the average Young Indy episode, in that it is bookended by an elderly man telling a remarkable story from his younger years.

In 1993, Darabont was asked to write the screenplay for Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace . However, George Lucas ended up writing it himself.
