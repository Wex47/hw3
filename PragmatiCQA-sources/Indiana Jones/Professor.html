Professor is an academic title, typically given to a tenured faculty member of a university or college. Becoming a professor usually involves having a doctorate in a field, and then becoming university faculty. Professors often have to split their time between two duties: teaching classes to students, and academic research.

Indiana Jones was a professor of archaeology , while his father Henry Jones, Sr. was a professor of medieval literature.
