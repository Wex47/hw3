Peanuts has been adapted into comic books as well as the short form comic strips. The first comics were published in the 1950s and 1960s and initially featured newspaper strip reprints; and starting in the late 1950s, new stories were presented with original work by Jim Sasseville early on, with some material by Charles M. Schulz himself, and later Dale Hale and others. Schulz also drew original covers for all issues titled Peanuts . Few Peanuts comics were published until Kaboom! Studios acquired a license to make new stories in the 2010s. Kaboom! Studios normal comics issues (in Volumes 1 and 2) feature a mix of reprinted classic strips and new stories. Kaboom! Studios also has produced new stories in individual graphic novels.
