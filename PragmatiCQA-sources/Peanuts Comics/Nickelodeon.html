Nickelodeon , (shortened as Nick ), is a children's cable network that began broadcasting in April 1979. Owned by the Paramount Kids & Family Group, a unit of the Paramount Media Networks division of Paramount Global (formerly controlled by WarnerMedia's predecessor, Warner Communications, and Warner-Amex Satellite Entertainment, a joint venture between Warner Bros. and American Express), Nickelodeon started out as a local channel on the Warner-Amex cable system, QUBE, in Columbus, Ohio. It is known for several programs, such as Rugrats , SpongeBob SquarePants , The Fairly OddParents , iCarly , and The Loud House .

Nickelodeon owned the broadcast rights to the animated Peanuts specials and shows from January 1998 to summer 2000. On Nickelodeon, the cartoons were broadcast under the title You're on Nickelodeon, Charlie Brown . Hour-length specials, such as It's an Adventure, Charlie Brown and , were shown split into two half-hour parts each. A Charlie Brown Christmas , It's the Great Pumpkin, Charlie Brown , and A Charlie Brown Thanksgiving , which were airing on CBS at the time, were not included in Nickelodeon's package, though the first two were eventually aired on Nick at Nite as part of a four-hour marathon paying tribute to Charles Schulz on February 15, 2000. It's the Girl in the Red Truck, Charlie Brown and Why, Charlie Brown, Why? were also never aired on the channel at all.

The June/July 1998 issue of Nickelodeon Magazine included an interview with Schulz, which also promoted the Peanuts cartoons airing on the channel.

The main Nickelodeon network ceased airing You're on Nickelodeon, Charlie Brown in the summer of 2000, but they reappeared on sister network NickToons in the 2003-04 television season, right before Nickelodeon's contract to run the specials expired altogether. To date, no other television network has yet obtained the broadcast rights to the Peanuts specials (aside from the above-mentioned holiday specials, as well as those made since 2000 and several pre-2000 specials).

From 2002 to 2005, Nickelodeon annually ran a series of shorts spoofing classic Christmas specials every December. One particular short, titled A Chuckie Finster Christmas, Channukah, Kwaanza, Winter Solstice , is a parody of A Charlie Brown Christmas starring the main characters from Rugrats .

In 2010, the children's areas in several Cedar Fair theme parks (including Kings Island and Kings Dominion), got a massive re-theming from Nicktoon characters (such as SpongeBob and Dora the Explorer ), to the Peanuts gang. This was due to the company's contract with Viacom expiring, and their already having a contract to use the Peanuts characters.

In October 2015, Nickelodeon ran an Orange Carpet promo advertising The Peanuts Movie , featuring Madisyn Shipman , who voiced Violet Gray in the film. New animation of the Peanuts characters was produced specifically for this promo. The movie will be airing on Nickelodeon on February 4, 2022, marking the Peanuts franchise's first official appearance on any of the Nickelodeon networks since 2004.
