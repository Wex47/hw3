Dale Hale was one of two cartoonists—along with Jim Sasseville —who worked with Charles M. Schulz in his art studio and helped Schulz produce Peanuts comic books . Later in life, Hale wrote and illustrated four children's books: Couldn't Say No Joe and the Rabbits with Bad Habits , Flag Bears , A Childen's Book for Adults (Who Forgot a Lot) , and Willy's Walk, Name the Dogs . During the late 1970's Hale worked for Kim Casali, as her husband was dying from cancer and she was in no mood to draw her comic Love Is... . He only kept this up for a year stating in a 2012 interview, "I really had a hard time drawing that poorly. Maybe if I draw it with my left hand, it'll look bad enough."
