Universal Pictures is an American film studio based in the Los Angeles County, California unincorporated community of Universal City, owned by Philadelphia, Pennsylvania-based cable television conglomerate Comcast. In the 1980s the studio was known as Universal City Studios. Universal City Studios was used for dubbing for It's the Girl in the Red Truck, Charlie Brown , the animated TV version of and This is America, Charlie Brown . Universal Studios Sound was used for the specials Why, Charlie Brown, Why? and Snoopy's Reunion . Universal has never released a Peanuts special or movie on VHS or DVD, though.

Universal Studios Japan , a theme park that is owned by Universal Pictures' owner NBCUniversal has a license to use the Peanuts characters and likenesses for shows, attractions and parades.
